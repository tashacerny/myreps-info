summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend the Truth in Lending Act (TILA) to expand its regulatory scope to include \"buy now, pay later\" (BNPL) loans and the companies that issue them. Currently, TILA primarily governs traditional credit products like credit cards and personal loans. The bill would redefine \"credit card\" and \"credit issuer\" under TILA to encompass BNPL arrangements, which are short-term financing products that allow consumers to purchase items and defer payment in installments, often with no interest if paid on schedule.  The bill would affect BNPL companies and consumers who use these services. BNPL issuers would become subject to TILA's disclosure and consumer protection requirements, which typically include clear disclosure of terms, interest rates, fees, and payment schedules. Consumers using BNPL products would gain protections currently available to credit card users under federal law, such as standardized disclosures and dispute resolution procedures.  Key provisions would likely require BNPL issuers to comply with TILA's existing disclosure standards, fair lending rules, and other consumer protection measures currently applied to traditional credit card issuers. The exact scope of compliance obligations would depend on the bill's specific language, but the fundamental purpose is to extend the regulatory framework governing traditional credit products to the growing BNPL market."
