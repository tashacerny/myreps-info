summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes two main actions related to veteran suicide prevention. First, it would formally codify (write into law) the National Veteran Suicide Prevention Annual Report, which the Department of Veterans Affairs currently produces. By codifying this report, the bill would establish it as a permanent, legally required publication rather than leaving it as an administrative practice that could be changed or discontinued. Second, the bill directs the VA Secretary to conduct a study examining whether and how the VA's Chaplain Service affects suicide risk among veterans.  The bill affects the Department of Veterans Affairs, which would be responsible for continuing the annual report and conducting the new study. It also affects veterans, as the study and report would focus on understanding suicide prevention efforts and chaplain services available to them.  Key provisions include the requirement to maintain the annual suicide prevention report as law and the directive to complete a study of the Chaplain Service's impact on veteran suicide risk. The study would likely inform future decisions about chaplaincy resources and suicide prevention strategies within the VA."
