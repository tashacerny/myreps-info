summary: "# Bill Summary: Repeal of the 90/10 Rule for Proprietary Schools  This bill proposes to repeal the \"90/10 rule\" as it applies to proprietary (for-profit) schools that participate in federal student aid programs under Title IV of the Higher Education Act of 1965. The 90/10 rule currently requires that proprietary schools derive at least 10 percent of their revenue from non-federal sources; schools that fail to meet this threshold lose eligibility for federal student aid funding. The bill would eliminate this requirement entirely for proprietary institutions.  The bill would directly affect for-profit educational institutions and their students. For-profit schools could operate with a higher percentage of revenue coming from federal student aid programs, while students at these institutions would maintain or potentially expand access to federal financial aid. The change could also indirectly affect federal spending on student aid programs and competition in the higher education market.  The key provision is the outright repeal of the 90/10 revenue requirement. Currently, this rule serves as a financial sustainability check on for-profit schools by incentivizing them to attract students who pay through non-federal means (private loans, out-of-pocket funds, employer funding, etc.). Repealing it would remove this constraint on how much of a proprietary school's operating budget can rely on federal Title IV aid."
