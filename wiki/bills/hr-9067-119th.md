summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would amend the Communications Act of 1934 to add a new content descriptor for video programming related to gender identity. Content descriptors are rating labels that help viewers, particularly parents, identify program content that may be unsuitable for certain audiences. This proposal would establish a standardized gender identity descriptor to be included alongside existing descriptors (such as violence, language, or sexual content) in the rating systems for television and other video programming.  The bill would primarily affect broadcast and cable television networks, streaming services, and other video programming distributors, which would need to implement the new descriptor in their rating systems. It would also impact viewers and families who use content ratings to make viewing decisions. The key provision is the creation and implementation of the gender identity descriptor as part of the existing content rating framework, though the specific details of how the descriptor would be defined, labeled, or applied are not available from the title alone."
