summary: "# Bill Summary: New World Screwworm Grant Program  This bill proposes to establish a federal grant program focused on the New World screwworm, an invasive parasitic fly that affects livestock and wildlife. The program would provide funding to support activities related to preventing, preparing for, and responding to outbreaks of this pest in the United States.  The grants would likely be available to state agricultural agencies, universities, research institutions, and possibly other eligible entities involved in animal health and agricultural protection. The funding would support activities such as surveillance and monitoring, research on outbreak prevention and treatment, emergency response coordination, and potentially eradication efforts if outbreaks occur.  The bill affects livestock producers, agricultural agencies, veterinary professionals, and wildlife management authorities who work to protect animals from parasitic diseases. By establishing dedicated federal funding, the bill aims to strengthen the nation's capacity to detect and manage screwworm infestations before they spread or cause significant economic losses to agricultural operations."
