summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to modify utility allowance policies for public housing by including high-speed internet service costs as a covered utility expense. Currently, utility allowances in public housing typically cover traditional utilities such as electricity, water, gas, and sewage. The bill would expand these allowances to account for the cost of broadband internet service, recognizing it as a reasonable and necessary expense for residents.  The primary groups affected would be families living in public housing administered by the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) and local public housing authorities. By including internet costs in utility allowances, the bill aims to reduce the out-of-pocket expenses these residents must pay and potentially increase their access to broadband services.  The key provision establishes that high-speed internet service costs should be considered part of standard utility allowances when calculating rent and assistance levels for public housing residents. The bill's implementation would require HUD and local housing authorities to revise their utility allowance calculations and budgets to reflect this addition."
