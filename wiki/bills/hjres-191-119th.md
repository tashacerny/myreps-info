summary: "# Summary of Constitutional Amendment Proposal  This bill proposes a constitutional amendment that would grant Congress and state legislatures the power to regulate and limit monetary contributions and spending in elections for public office, as well as in ballot initiatives and referendums. Currently, federal campaign finance restrictions are limited by Supreme Court interpretations of the First Amendment, particularly the 2010 *Citizens United* decision, which struck down certain spending limits. This amendment would explicitly authorize government bodies to impose such restrictions without those constitutional constraints.  The amendment would affect candidates for federal and state office, political parties, donors, and organizations that spend money to influence elections and ballot measures. It would also impact voters, who might see changes to how campaigns are funded and conducted if Congress and state legislatures choose to implement new contribution or spending limits under this expanded authority.  Key provisions would include giving Congress authority to regulate federal election spending and contributions, giving states similar authority over state and local elections and ballot measures, and likely allowing both Congress and states to enforce these regulations through legislation. The amendment does not itself impose specific limits; rather, it removes constitutional barriers that currently prevent such limits from being implemented."
