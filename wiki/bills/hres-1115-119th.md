summary: "# Summary of Procedural Bill  This bill is a procedural measure that sets the terms for House floor consideration of three separate pieces of legislation. It does not itself enact policy but rather establishes rules for how and when these bills will be debated and voted on.  The three bills covered are: (1) H.R. 556, which would prohibit the Secretaries of Interior and Agriculture from banning lead ammunition or fishing tackle on federal lands and waters they manage; (2) H.R. 1958, which would amend immigration law to make aliens convicted of defrauding the U.S. government or illegally receiving public benefits subject to deportation; and (3) H.R. 4638, which would make aliens convicted of harming law enforcement animals inadmissible to the U.S. and deportable. The bill also addresses procedural rules for motions to suspend normal House rules during consideration.  The measure affects hunters and anglers using federal lands, federal agencies' regulatory authority, immigration enforcement, and the House legislative process. This type of procedural bill allows the House to bundle consideration of multiple pieces of legislation and set parameters for debate without modifying the substance of the bills themselves."
