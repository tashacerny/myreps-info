The bill affects federal agencies and departments that control spending on the Supreme Court Building and its surrounding grounds, as well as the Congressional appropriations process. It would limit the executive branch's ability to use existing federal funds or newly appropriated funds for this specific infrastructure project.  The key provision is a straightforward funding prohibition: no federal dollars may be used for permanent fencing installation at the Supreme Court site. The bill does not appear to address temporary fencing, non-federal funding sources, or other security measures. It represents a legislative constraint on executive discretion regarding the use of appropriated funds for this particular capital improvement."
