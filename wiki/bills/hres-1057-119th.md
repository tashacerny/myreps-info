summary: "# Bill Summary  This is a procedural rule that establishes the terms for House floor consideration of four separate bills. The rule allows the House to debate and vote on these bills under specified conditions. The four bills covered are: S. 1383 (establishing a Veterans Advisory Committee on Equal Access), H.R. 2189 (updating federal firearms laws to address technological advances and less-lethal weapons), H.R. 261 (modifying marine sanctuary requirements for undersea fiber optic cables), and H.R. 3617 (amending Department of Energy authority related to critical minerals and energy resources). The rule also grants a waiver from standard House procedural requirements regarding certain resolutions from the Rules Committee.  # Who It Affects  This rule primarily affects the House legislative process and the federal agencies and stakeholders involved in the four underlying bills: veterans (S. 1383), firearms manufacturers and users (H.R. 2189), telecommunications and marine industries (H.R. 261), and the Department of Energy and critical minerals sector (H.R. 3617).  # Key Provision  The main provision is establishing the procedural framework for considering these four bills on the House floor. By passing this rule, the House determines how much time will be allocated for debate, what amendments are permitted, and the voting procedures for each bill. The waiver of Rule XIII clause 6(a) allows the House flexibility in its standard procedural requirements for this legislative package."
