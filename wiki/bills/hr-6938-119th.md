summary: "# Bill Summary: FY 2026 Appropriations for Commerce, Justice, Science, Energy, Water, and Interior  This bill would provide federal funding for three major areas of government operations for fiscal year 2026: (1) Commerce, Justice, and Science agencies; (2) Energy and Water Development; and (3) Interior and Environment departments. The bill would set spending levels across dozens of federal agencies and programs that touch nearly every state and many sectors of the economy.  The Commerce, Justice, and Science portion funds the Department of Commerce (including the Census Bureau and National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration), the Department of Justice, and the National Science Foundation. The Energy and Water Development section covers the Department of Energy, Army Corps of Engineers, and related water projects. The Interior and Environment portion funds the Department of Interior, Environmental Protection Agency, and related agencies. Together, these appropriations support activities ranging from law enforcement and scientific research to environmental protection, infrastructure projects, and natural resource management.  This is an omnibus appropriations bill, meaning it consolidates funding decisions for multiple departments into a single legislative vehicle. It would affect federal employees across these agencies, as well as state and local governments, universities, and organizations that receive federal grants and contracts in areas such as research, law enforcement, environmental conservation, and water resource management."
