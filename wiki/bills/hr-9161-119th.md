The bill would affect aluminum scrap exporters, the USITC, and potentially the Department of Commerce or other relevant agencies involved in trade policy. It would also impact trading relationships with countries classified as \"countries of concern,\" though the specific countries in this category would depend on how the bill defines this term.  Key provisions would likely include a mandate for the USITC to complete the investigation within a specified timeframe, analyze the national security implications of aluminum scrap exports, and potentially make recommendations regarding export restrictions or regulations. The bill may also include provisions for reporting findings to Congress or for implementing restrictions on these exports based on the investigation's results."
