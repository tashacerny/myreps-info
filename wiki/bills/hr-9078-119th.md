summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend the Omnibus Crime Control and Safe Streets Act to expand federal crime reporting requirements to include felony convictions for animal cruelty offenses. Currently, the federal crime reporting system collects data on various felony convictions, but animal cruelty offenses may not be systematically tracked at the federal level. This bill would establish or clarify that felony animal cruelty convictions must be reported through existing federal crime reporting mechanisms.  The bill would primarily affect law enforcement agencies, state and local prosecutors, and the federal crime statistics system. By requiring reporting of animal cruelty felonies, it would create a more comprehensive national database of these convictions, allowing for better tracking of trends and patterns across jurisdictions.  The key effect of this legislation would be increased data collection and transparency regarding felony animal cruelty convictions at the federal level, without necessarily creating new criminal penalties or enforcement mechanisms. This would support evidence-based policymaking and research on animal cruelty offenses."
