summary: "# Summary of Bill Consideration  This bill provides for House floor consideration of H.R. 8035, which would extend authorities under Title VII of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act (FISA) of 1978 as amended in 2008. Title VII permits the government to conduct surveillance of non-U.S. persons reasonably believed to be located outside the United States without requiring individualized court approval for each target, though the surveillance must comply with procedures approved by the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court. The bill would extend these authorities through October 20, 2027, rather than allowing them to expire on their current sunset date.  The extension affects federal intelligence agencies—primarily the National Security Agency, FBI, and CIA—that rely on Title VII authorities to collect foreign intelligence. It also has broad implications for privacy advocates and civil liberties organizations concerned with surveillance scope, as well as for telecommunications companies that must comply with government directives.  Key provisions include the extension of surveillance authorities and \"other purposes\" referenced in the title, though the specific additional provisions are not detailed in the title alone. The bill itself (H.R. 8035) may contain modifications to existing FISA procedures, reporting requirements, or oversight mechanisms, but these details cannot be confirmed from the title information provided."
