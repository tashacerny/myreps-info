summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes an amendment to the Emergency Food Assistance Act of 1983 that would expand sourcing options for food commodities distributed through the emergency food assistance program. Specifically, it would allow program administrators to procure fresh fruits and vegetables through the Department of Defense Fresh Fruit and Vegetable Program, in addition to existing procurement channels.  The change would primarily affect recipients of emergency food assistance—including food banks, soup kitchens, and other organizations serving low-income individuals—as well as the federal agencies responsible for administering these programs. The Department of Agriculture and Department of Defense would be involved in implementing any new procurement procedures.  The key provision is establishing the Defense Department's fresh produce supply program as an authorized source for emergency food assistance commodities. This could potentially provide an alternative distribution channel and may affect how and where fresh fruits and vegetables are sourced for emergency food programs, though the specific operational details, cost implications, and implementation requirements would depend on the full legislative language not provided here."
