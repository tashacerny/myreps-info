summary: "# Bill Summary: Interest on Escrow Balances  This bill would amend the Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act of 1974 (RESPA) to require that interest be paid on escrow account balances. Escrow accounts are typically used in real estate transactions to hold funds for property taxes, homeowners insurance, and other recurring costs. Currently, federal law generally prohibits lenders from paying interest on these accounts. This bill would change that requirement, allowing or requiring interest to be paid to borrowers on the funds held in their escrow accounts.  The bill would primarily affect homeowners with mortgages and the lenders or servicers who manage their escrow accounts. Under current law, lenders benefit from holding borrower funds without paying interest; this change would shift some financial benefit back to borrowers by compensating them for the use of their money. The specific provisions would likely address details such as the interest rate to be applied, how interest would be calculated and distributed, and any exemptions or thresholds that might apply.
