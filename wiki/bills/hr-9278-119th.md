summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend federal administrative law to change how courts review certain agency decisions. Specifically, it would require courts to conduct a \"de novo trial of the facts\"—meaning courts would independently re-examine all factual evidence rather than giving deference to agency findings—when an agency takes action that imposes a sanction on an individual or entity. Currently, under the Administrative Procedure Act, courts typically review agency factual findings with some deference, meaning they generally uphold those findings if they are reasonable and supported by substantial evidence on the record.  The bill would affect any individual, company, or organization that faces a sanction (such as a fine, penalty, license revocation, or similar enforcement action) from a federal agency. It would also impact federal agencies that pursue enforcement actions. Under this change, regulated parties challenging an agency sanction could compel courts to hold a new trial-like proceeding to examine the facts, rather than limiting their challenge to reviewing the agency's existing record.  The practical effect would be to reduce judicial deference to agency factfinding in sanction cases and give courts broader authority to reach independent conclusions about disputed facts. This could make it easier for regulated entities to overturn agency enforcement decisions on factual grounds, though the bill's text is not available for review of specific scope or potential exceptions."
