summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill is a procedural resolution informing the Senate that the House of Representatives has achieved a quorum (sufficient number of members present to conduct business) and has completed the election of its two top officers: the Speaker of the House and the Clerk of the House. This is a formal notification that takes place at the beginning of a new congressional session, after House members have been sworn in.  The bill affects Congress as an institution, as it enables official communication between the two chambers about the House's operational readiness. It impacts all members of both the House and Senate, as it signals that the House can now conduct legislative business. The key provision is the notification itself—confirming that the House has met constitutional and procedural requirements to function as a legislative body.  This is a routine, noncontroversial procedural measure with no direct impact on external parties or policies. It is typically passed early in each new Congress as part of standard organizational procedures."
