summary: "# Summary of Procedural Resolution  This resolution is a procedural measure that sets the terms for House floor consideration of four separate bills and one resolution. It does not itself enact policy but rather establishes the rules—such as debate time, amendment procedures, and voting processes—that will govern how the House considers these four legislative items.  The substantive bills covered include: H.R. 8800, which would authorize defense spending for fiscal year 2027 across the Department of Defense, military construction, and Department of Energy defense programs, and would set military personnel levels; H.R. 8595, which would appropriate funds for national security, State Department, and related programs for fiscal year 2027; and H.R. 8884, which would extend demonstration authority for the Social Security disability insurance program. The resolution also provides for consideration of H. Res. 1383, which commemorates the one-year anniversary of the Working Families Tax Cuts.  This type of procedural resolution is standard in the House of Representatives and allows the chamber to organize its legislative calendar. The resolution affects all members of Congress and any constituent groups with interests in defense policy, international affairs, Social Security disability programs, or tax policy, depending on the specific provisions in each underlying bill."
