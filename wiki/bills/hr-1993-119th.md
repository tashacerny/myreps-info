summary: "# Summary of the 25th Anniversary of 9/11 Commemorative Coin Act  This bill authorizes the U.S. Mint to design and issue commemorative coins marking the 25th anniversary of the September 11, 2001 terrorist attacks. The coins would be produced in various denominations and compositions (typically gold, silver, and clad options for commemorative coins) and sold to the public. The bill affects the U.S. Mint, collectors, and any individuals or organizations interested in purchasing commemorative coins related to this historical event.  Key provisions typically include specifications for coin design (which may involve imagery related to 9/11 remembrance), the number of coins that may be minted, and the surcharge placed on coin sales. Revenue from surcharges on commemorative coin sales is generally designated for charitable purposes—in this case, likely organizations related to 9/11 victims, first responders, or related memorials. The bill establishes the timeline for minting and sale, with coins generally issued around the anniversary date."
