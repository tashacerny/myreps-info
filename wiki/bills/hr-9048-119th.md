summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act to classify gluten-containing grain as a major food allergen under federal law. Currently, the law identifies nine major food allergens: milk, eggs, peanuts, tree nuts, fish, crustacean shellfish, soy, wheat, and sesame. While wheat is already listed, this bill would expand the definition to specifically include all gluten-containing grains (such as barley and rye, in addition to wheat).  The bill would affect food manufacturers, importers, and distributors, who would be required to clearly label products containing gluten-containing grains. It would also affect consumers with celiac disease, non-celiac gluten sensitivity, and wheat allergies, potentially providing them with clearer allergen labeling and disclosure requirements. Food facilities would need to comply with new labeling standards when marketing products.  Key provisions would likely include mandatory disclosure of gluten-containing grains on food labels in a consistent format and location, similar to existing allergen labeling requirements. The bill may also establish compliance timelines for manufacturers to update their labels and packaging."
