The bill would affect communities with significant immigrant populations, mental health service providers, and individuals who have experienced or fear immigration law enforcement actions. It may also impact HHS operations and budgeting, as it would require the department to allocate resources to create and operate this new office.  Key provisions would likely include establishing the office within HHS, defining its responsibilities for coordinating mental health services, and potentially providing grants or technical assistance to local and community-based mental health organizations. The office would serve as a resource to connect affected individuals with treatment and support services.
