summary: "# Bill Summary: Social Media Harms Victim Remembrance Day Resolution  This resolution designates June 23, 2026, as \"Social Media Harms Victim Remembrance Day\" at the federal level. The measure creates an official day of recognition intended to honor and remember individuals who have been harmed by social media platforms or activities conducted through social media.  The resolution primarily affects public observance and awareness rather than establishing new regulations or programs. It calls attention to harms that can occur through social media use, which may include cyberbullying, harassment, misinformation, mental health impacts, exploitation, or other documented harms. The date itself may have significance related to a specific incident or advocacy effort, though the resolution title does not specify the reason for selecting June 23.  As a resolution rather than legislation, this measure does not create enforceable requirements or allocate federal funding. Its primary effect is symbolic—establishing an official day for commemoration, reflection, and potentially raising public awareness about social media-related harms. It may encourage organizations, institutions, and individuals to recognize the day through events, statements, or educational activities."
