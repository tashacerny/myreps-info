summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would direct the President to remove United States Armed Forces from any hostilities involving Iran, invoking the War Powers Resolution. The War Powers Resolution is a 1973 federal law that establishes procedures for presidential military action, including a requirement that the President notify Congress within 48 hours of committing armed forces to military action and prohibits armed forces from remaining for more than 60 days without congressional authorization. Section 5(c) of that law allows Congress to direct the President to remove armed forces from military action.  **Key Effects:**  The bill would affect active military operations or engagements involving Iran or Iranian forces. It would apply to all branches of the U.S. Armed Forces operating under presidential command. If passed, the President would be required to withdraw military personnel from such hostilities.  **What the Bill Does:**  This is a directive measure that uses Congress's constitutional authority over military matters and the procedures established in the War Powers Resolution. The bill does not authorize military action or provide funding; rather, it orders the cessation of existing hostilities. Without the full text, specific details—such as timelines for withdrawal or exceptions for certain operations—cannot be confirmed, but the core provision is a congressional mandate to end U.S. armed conflict with Iran."
