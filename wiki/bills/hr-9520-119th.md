summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes amendments to two federal workers' compensation laws: the War Hazards Compensation Act and the Longshore and Harbor Workers' Compensation Act. The primary change would require the Federal Government to pay interest on reimbursements that are made late to beneficiaries or third parties under these programs. Currently, no such interest requirement exists for overdue payments.  The bill affects federal employees and workers covered under these compensation programs, as well as employers and insurers who receive reimbursements from the government under these acts. The War Hazards Compensation Act covers federal employees injured by war-related hazards, while the Longshore and Harbor Workers' Compensation Act covers maritime workers. By adding an interest requirement, the bill aims to compensate claimants for delays in receiving owed funds.  The key provision is establishing federal interest obligations on late reimbursements, though the specific interest rate and calculation method would be determined by the legislative text. This represents a procedural change to ensure timely payment and provide compensation for payment delays under these two federal workers' compensation programs."
