It would apply to all branches of the Armed Forces and any ongoing or future military engagements related to Cuba.  The bill would primarily affect the Department of Defense and military commanders, who would be required to cease or withdraw from unauthorized hostilities. It could also impact U.S. foreign policy toward Cuba and U.S. military operations in the Caribbean region. The measure reflects broader constitutional concerns about the congressional war powers clause, which grants Congress—rather than the President alone—the authority to declare war and authorize military action.
