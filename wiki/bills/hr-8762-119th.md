summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would amend the Commodity Credit Corporation (CCC) Charter Act to change how certain crops are treated in federal agricultural programs. Specifically, it proposes to exclude from CCC support any agricultural commodities that are used to produce biofuel. The CCC is a government-owned corporation that provides loans, payments, and other financial support to farmers and agricultural producers to stabilize farm income and support domestic agricultural production.  The practical effect would be to prevent farmers from receiving federal agricultural assistance (such as price supports, loan programs, or subsidy payments) for crops grown specifically for biofuel production. This would affect farmers who grow crops like corn for ethanol production or soybeans for biodiesel, as well as related agricultural sectors involved in biofuel supply chains.  The bill targets agricultural commodity programs administered through the CCC, potentially changing incentives for farmers deciding what crops to plant. The scope and specific mechanics would depend on implementation details not available in the title alone, but the core proposal is to create a distinction in federal support between crops grown for traditional food and feed uses versus those designated for biofuel production."
