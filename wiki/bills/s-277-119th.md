summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill authorizes the federal government to release its reversionary interest in land located in Chester County, Tennessee, and to convey mineral interests associated with that property. A reversionary interest is a legal right that allows the federal government to reclaim property under certain conditions. By releasing this interest, the federal government would relinquish its claim to regain control of the land. The bill also transfers mineral rights—the legal entitlements to extract or profit from minerals beneath the surface—to another party, likely a private owner or local entity.  The bill primarily affects the current or prospective private landowners in Chester County, Tennessee, as well as any entities with existing claims to the mineral rights in question. By removing federal claims and clarifying mineral ownership, the legislation could affect land use, development potential, or resource extraction activities on the affected property.  The specific provisions would depend on the bill's full text, which typically includes details such as the exact acreage involved, which party receives the mineral interests, any conditions placed on future land use, and whether compensation or considerations are exchanged.
