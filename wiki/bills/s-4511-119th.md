summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would modify the Internal Revenue Code to allow certain distributions from employer-sponsored retirement plans to be excluded from a person's taxable income when those distributions are made to qualified charities. Currently, distributions from retirement plans like 401(k)s and traditional IRAs are generally treated as taxable income to the recipient. This proposal would create an exception for charitable giving, meaning beneficiaries could direct retirement plan money to charities without owing federal income taxes on that amount.  The provision would primarily affect individuals with employer-sponsored retirement plans who wish to make charitable donations and those charities receiving such donations. It could also impact federal tax revenue, as excluding charitable distributions from income would reduce the taxable income base.
