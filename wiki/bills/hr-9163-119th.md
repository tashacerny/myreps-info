summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes an amendment to Medicare law (Title XVIII of the Social Security Act) regarding Critical Access Hospital (CAH) designations. Critical Access Hospitals are small, rural hospitals that receive special Medicare payment rates and exemptions from certain regulations. The bill addresses a specific concern: that hospitals could lose their CAH status if nearby roadway or transportation infrastructure improvements change their geographic location or accessibility classification, even though the hospital itself has not substantially changed.  The bill aims to protect hospitals from losing CAH eligibility solely due to external transportation improvements over which they have limited control. Currently, CAH status depends partly on location criteria, including distance from other hospitals. If infrastructure improvements alter how distance or accessibility is measured, a hospital could technically lose its special status despite remaining operationally unchanged. This bill would prevent such automatic loss of status based on these external factors.  The primary beneficiaries would be Critical Access Hospitals and their communities, particularly in rural areas where CAH designations are concentrated. These designations help sustain smaller hospitals that might otherwise be economically unviable, supporting rural healthcare access and local employment."
