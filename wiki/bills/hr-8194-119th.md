summary: "# Agent Orange Service Medal Act – Summary  **What the Bill Proposes**  This bill would establish a new military service medal to recognize and honor service members who were exposed to Agent Orange during their military service. Agent Orange was a herbicide used by the U.S. military during the Vietnam War. The medal would serve as an official acknowledgment of this exposure and the service members' participation in operations where the chemical was deployed.  **Who It Affects**  The medal would apply to military service members who served in areas where Agent Orange was used or stored, primarily during the Vietnam War era. This includes veterans and their families, as exposure to Agent Orange has been linked to various health conditions. The bill recognizes a specific category of service that was not previously honored with a dedicated medal.  **Key Provisions**  The bill authorizes the creation and awarding of this new service medal through the Department of Defense. It would establish eligibility criteria for service members to receive the medal based on documented exposure or service in affected areas. The medal would be awarded to those meeting the criteria, either during their military service or posthumously, and could be awarded to veterans who are no longer living."
