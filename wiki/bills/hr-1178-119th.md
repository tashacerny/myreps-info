The bill likely proposes requiring food manufacturers to disclose the presence of alpha-gal as an allergen on product labels, similar to existing requirements for common allergens like peanuts, tree nuts, and shellfish.  **Who It Affects**  The bill would affect individuals diagnosed with alpha-gal syndrome, who can experience allergic reactions ranging from mild to severe when consuming mammalian meat products. It would also impact food manufacturers and producers, who would need to update labeling and potentially reformulate products to comply with new disclosure requirements.  **Key Provisions**  While the full text is unavailable, such legislation typically includes requirements for manufacturers to identify alpha-gal-containing products on labels, establish testing or verification standards, and implement compliance timelines.
