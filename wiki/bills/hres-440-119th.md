summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill is a commemorative resolution that uses Memorial Day 2025 (May 26) to bring attention to the voting representation status of certain populations in the District of Columbia. Specifically, it recognizes active duty servicemembers, National Guard members, reservists, veterans, and their families who live in D.C. and highlights that these individuals lack voting representation in Congress and do not have the ability to vote on statehood for the District.  The bill affects D.C. residents in military service or military-connected households. Currently, D.C. residents—including military personnel stationed there—cannot vote for voting members of Congress and have limited local self-governance compared to state residents. The resolution frames this as a notable issue affecting this particular population on a day dedicated to honoring military service.  As a commemorative resolution, this bill does not propose changes to law or policy. Rather, it serves to formally acknowledge and draw attention to the voting and representation status of military-connected residents of D.C., potentially as a symbolic statement ahead of broader debates about D.C. representation or statehood."
