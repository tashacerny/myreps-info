summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would direct the Secretary of Commerce to create a standardized methodology for determining and identifying the country of origin of shrimp. Such a methodology would establish consistent standards for labeling and tracking shrimp products, allowing consumers and regulators to know where shrimp have been sourced or processed. The bill addresses gaps in current origin-labeling requirements and aims to provide clearer information about shrimp supply chains.  The bill would primarily affect shrimp importers, processors, retailers, and consumers. Importers and processors would need to comply with whatever methodology the Secretary develops, potentially requiring changes to labeling, documentation, or tracking practices. Consumers would benefit from clearer origin information on shrimp products. The bill may also affect domestic shrimp producers by increasing transparency in the marketplace.  Key provisions would include directing the Commerce Department to develop the methodology and presumably establishing how it should be applied across the shrimp industry. The bill likely addresses technical questions such as whether origin is determined by where shrimp are caught, where they are initially processed, or where final processing occurs—distinctions that matter significantly in global seafood trade."
