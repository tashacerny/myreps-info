summary: "# Bill Summary: Medical Debt Prohibition in Consumer Reports  **What the Bill Proposes**  This bill would amend the Fair Credit Reporting Act to prohibit credit reporting agencies from including medical debt on consumer credit reports. The legislation aims to prevent medical debt from affecting consumers' credit scores and creditworthiness assessments used by lenders, employers, and other entities that access credit reports.  **Who It Affects**  The bill would directly affect consumers who have incurred medical debt, potentially improving their access to credit and other services that rely on credit scores. It would also impact credit reporting agencies, which would be required to remove or exclude medical debt from their reporting practices. Lenders, employers, and other entities that use credit reports for decision-making would see changes in the information available to them.  **Key Provisions**  While the full text is unavailable, the core provision would prohibit medical debt from appearing on consumer credit reports maintained by credit reporting agencies. The bill may include provisions addressing how existing medical debt currently on reports should be handled, implementation timelines, and enforcement mechanisms to ensure compliance with the new requirements."
