summary: "# Summary of Procedural Bill  This bill is a procedural measure that sets rules for House floor consideration of four separate legislative items. It does not enact policy itself but rather establishes the terms under which the House will debate and vote on these four proposals: two congressional disapproval resolutions targeting Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) regulations, one bill modifying federal court injunction authority, and one bill addressing voter registration requirements.  The bill addresses regulations on overdraft lending practices at large financial institutions and rules defining participation in digital payment applications. It also proposes changes to federal court procedures regarding injunctive relief and would amend the National Voter Registration Act of 1993 to require proof of U.S. citizenship when registering to vote in federal elections. These items represent a mix of financial regulation, judicial procedure, and election administration matters.  As a procedural resolution, this bill affects Congress's internal operations and does not directly regulate individuals or organizations. However, the underlying legislative items it enables would affect financial institutions subject to CFPB rules, federal courts, and individuals seeking to register to vote. The resolution itself simply permits the House to consider these four measures, potentially under specific debate and amendment parameters."
