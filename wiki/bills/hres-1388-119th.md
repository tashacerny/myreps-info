summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill is a congressional resolution that recognizes the ecological and agricultural importance of pollinators in the United States and expresses support for designating a \"National Pollinator Week.\" Pollinators—primarily bees, butterflies, and other insects—play a critical role in fertilizing plants that produce food crops and maintain wild plant ecosystems. The bill acknowledges these contributions and endorses an annual observance week dedicated to raising public awareness about pollinator health and conservation.  The resolution affects no specific groups directly, as it creates no new laws, regulations, or funding. Rather, it serves an educational and symbolic purpose, encouraging public attention to pollinator issues among citizens, farmers, agricultural businesses, environmental organizations, and policymakers. The bill may indirectly benefit organizations working on pollinator conservation and agricultural stakeholders interested in promoting crop pollination.  Key aspects of the bill include formally recognizing pollinators' value to both natural ecosystems and U.S. agriculture, and supporting the establishment of an annual National Pollinator Week to educate the public. As a resolution, it does not mandate specific actions or allocate resources, but rather expresses the sense of Congress that pollinators deserve recognition and protection through voluntary awareness and conservation efforts."
