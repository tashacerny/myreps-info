summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend the Military Land Withdrawals Act of 2013 by withdrawing and reserving additional public land near Yuma Proving Ground in Arizona for military use. The bill would designate certain parcels of federal public land in the vicinity of the proving ground as withdrawn from public use and reserved for military purposes.  The affected parties include the Department of Defense (which would gain control over the designated lands), federal land management agencies currently overseeing the affected public lands, and potentially private parties or interest groups with activities or claims related to the area. The lands involved are located in the vicinity of Yuma Proving Ground, an existing military testing and training facility in southwestern Arizona.  While specific acreage and exact parcel descriptions are not available from the title alone, the bill's key provision is the formal withdrawal and reservation of public land to support military operations at or near Yuma Proving Ground. This type of land withdrawal removes public land from activities like grazing, mining, or recreational use and dedicates it exclusively to military purposes."
