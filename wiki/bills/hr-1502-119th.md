summary: "# Summary of the North Platte Canteen Congressional Gold Medal Act  This bill authorizes the awarding of a Congressional Gold Medal to commemorate the North Platte Canteen, a historical service organization. The North Platte Canteen was a volunteer-run hospitality program operated by residents of North Platte, Nebraska, during World War II. It provided food, refreshments, and hospitality to soldiers traveling through the area by train en route to military deployment.  The bill recognizes the efforts of the volunteers who staffed the canteen and honors their contribution to supporting military personnel during wartime. By awarding the Congressional Gold Medal—one of the highest civilian honors bestowed by Congress—the legislation acknowledges the historical significance of this grassroots civilian effort and the volunteers' service to the nation. The medal would serve as a formal recognition of the North Platte community's dedication to supporting troops during World War II."
