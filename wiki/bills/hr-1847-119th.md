## What This Bill Does

The Infrastructure Modernization and Jobs Act allocates $1.2 trillion over ten years to repair and upgrade the physical infrastructure that Americans rely on daily. The funding is divided into five major categories:

- **Roads and bridges** ($350 billion): Repairs over 45,000 structurally deficient bridges and resurfacing programs for the Interstate Highway System.
- **Passenger rail** ($210 billion): Expands Amtrak service to 30 new cities and upgrades the Northeast Corridor to reduce travel times.
- **Broadband** ($180 billion): Ensures high-speed internet access reaches every home and business in the United States by 2030, with priority given to rural areas, tribal lands, and lower-income urban communities.
- **Water and wastewater** ($160 billion): Replaces lead service lines and upgrades aging sewer systems and treatment plants.
- **Ports, airports, and freight** ($300 billion): Expands port capacity and upgrades air traffic control systems.

## Who Does It Affect?

Every American who drives, travels by train, uses tap water, or relies on the internet is affected by this bill. The Congressional Budget Office estimated the legislation would create or support approximately 1.5 million jobs annually during the construction phase.

## How It Is Funded

The bill is funded through a combination of unspent COVID-19 relief funds, new revenue from tighter IRS enforcement of tax compliance among high-income earners, and the issuance of new Treasury bonds. Supporters argued the long-term economic return from infrastructure investment exceeds the borrowing cost; opponents contended the bill adds too much to the national debt.
