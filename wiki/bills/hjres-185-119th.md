summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill invokes the Congressional Review Act (CRA) to disapprove of a specific regulatory action by the Bureau of Consumer Financial Protection (CFPB). The action being challenged is the CFPB's withdrawal of Consumer Financial Protection Circular 2024-03, which addressed unlawful and unenforceable contract terms and conditions in consumer financial products and services. If passed, the disapproval would prevent the CFPB from withdrawing this guidance and would prohibit the agency from issuing substantially similar rules in the future without congressional authorization.  The bill affects the CFPB's regulatory authority and, by extension, financial institutions and consumers. The circular in question provided guidance to financial companies about which contract terms—such as forced arbitration clauses, class action waivers, and other provisions—the CFPB considers unlawful or unenforceable under existing consumer protection law. Disapproving the withdrawal would reinstate this guidance and constrain the agency's ability to modify its position on these contract practices.  The Congressional Review Act allows Congress to disapprove of agency rules through an expedited process. This bill represents a procedural mechanism for legislative oversight of executive branch regulatory actions, requiring passage by both chambers and the President's signature to take effect."
