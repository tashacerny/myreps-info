summary: "# Bill Summary  This resolution acknowledges and apologizes for historical mistreatment and discrimination against LGBT individuals who served in the U.S. military, Foreign Service, and federal civil service. The resolution recognizes the contributions of these service members and federal employees while acknowledging the discriminatory policies and practices they faced, including employment dismissals, security clearance denials, and social stigma based on sexual orientation or gender identity.  The resolution commits Congress to pursuing equal rights, protections, and respect for LGBT individuals currently serving or seeking to serve in these capacities. It does not establish new law or create enforceable requirements but instead serves as a formal statement of acknowledgment and a commitment to future action regarding the treatment of LGBT service members and federal employees.  The resolution affects current and former LGBT service members in the military, employees in the Foreign Service (diplomatic corps), and federal civil service workers, as well as those who may seek such positions in the future. By expressing this commitment, the resolution signals congressional intent to support policies ensuring equal treatment regardless of sexual orientation or gender identity in the federal workforce."
