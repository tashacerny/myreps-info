summary: "# Summary of the Ocmulgee Mounds National Park Redesignation Act  This bill proposes to change the official designation of Ocmulgee National Monument in Georgia to Ocmulgee Mounds National Park. The change would elevate the site from monument to national park status, a higher classification in the National Park System. The bill affects the management and administration of this archaeological and historical site located in Macon, Georgia.  The key effect of this redesignation would be to grant the site full national park status, which typically carries increased protections, expanded visitor services, and potentially enhanced funding and staffing opportunities. The site contains significant Native American archaeological resources, including ancient ceremonial mounds dating back thousands of years. A redesignation would not necessarily alter the site's core mission or primary protections but would formally recognize it as a national park rather than a monument, which may affect its operational structure and management priorities."
