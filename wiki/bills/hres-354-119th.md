summary: "# Summary of Bill Providing for Consideration of Multiple Congressional Disapproval Resolutions  This bill is a procedural measure that sets the terms for House consideration of six separate joint resolutions that would use the Congressional Review Act (a mechanism allowing Congress to disapprove federal agency rules) to overturn specific regulations issued by federal agencies. The resolutions target rules from the National Park Service, U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, and Environmental Protection Agency covering topics including vehicle regulations in national recreation areas, endangered species protections for smelt, and California state vehicle emissions standards.  The bill affects federal agencies that have issued the targeted rules and potentially impacts regulated entities in the transportation and environmental sectors, as well as stakeholders interested in national park management, species protection, and vehicle emissions standards. The specific resolutions address: (1) National Park Service rules on motor vehicles at Glen Canyon National Recreation Area; (2) Fish and Wildlife Service rules protecting the San Francisco Bay-Delta smelt under the Endangered Species Act; and (3) EPA rules approving California's vehicle and engine emission standards for heavy-duty trucks, passenger vehicles, and nonroad engines.  By establishing consideration procedures for these resolutions, the bill would allow the House to vote on whether to disapprove each regulation. If passed by both chambers and signed into law (or if passed by veto-proof majorities), these joint resolutions would invalidate the targeted agency rules. The bill itself does not substantively change policy; rather, it enables the House to proceed with voting on the underlying disapproval measures."
