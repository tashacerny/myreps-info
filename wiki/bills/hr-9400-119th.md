This bill would recognize the historical contributions of U.S. citizens who took action to save lives during the Holocaust, either through direct rescue efforts, provision of shelter, assistance with escape, or other forms of aid.  The primary recipients of this honor would be American individuals (or potentially their estates or descendants) who engaged in rescue or assistance activities during the Holocaust era. The bill would also affect the institutions responsible for producing and presenting the medals, as well as potentially museums or memorial organizations that might display or commemorate the awards.  Without access to the full text, key provisions likely include: the authorization to mint and present Congressional Gold Medals; criteria for determining eligibility of rescuers; the number of medals to be issued; and procedures for nomination and selection.
