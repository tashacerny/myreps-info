H.R. 2550 would reverse an Executive Order that excluded certain entities or employees from federal labor-management relations programs. The exact scope of these exclusions cannot be detailed without access to the referenced Executive Order, but the underlying bill would affect federal employees and employers involved in labor relations covered by federal law. By nullifying the order, H.R. 2550 would restore participation rights or protections that the Executive Order had removed.  This type of measure is a standard House procedural step. The consideration bill typically includes details about how long debate will last, whether amendments can be offered, and how the final vote will be conducted. Its passage would allow the full House to proceed to debate and vote on the substance of H.R. 2550 itself."
