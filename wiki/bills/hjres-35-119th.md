summary: "# Summary of Congressional Disapproval Resolution  This resolution seeks to block an Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) rule regarding waste emissions charges for petroleum and natural gas systems. Under the Congressional Review Act (Chapter 8, Title 5, U.S. Code), Congress can disapprove of federal agency rules through expedited procedures. The rule in question establishes procedures to help companies comply with emissions charges, including provisions for netting (offsetting emissions reductions) and exemptions. If passed, this resolution would prevent the EPA rule from taking effect or continuing in force.  The resolution directly affects oil and natural gas producers and operators who would be subject to the emissions charge program and compliance procedures. The rule addresses how these entities can demonstrate compliance, reduce their overall obligations through netting mechanisms, and potentially qualify for exemptions. By disapproving the rule, Congress would eliminate the EPA's specified procedures for facilitating such compliance, though it would not necessarily eliminate the underlying emissions charge program itself.  This is a straightforward tool for legislative oversight of executive branch regulations, allowing either chamber of Congress to register formal disapproval of agency rules within a defined timeframe after those rules are submitted to Congress."
