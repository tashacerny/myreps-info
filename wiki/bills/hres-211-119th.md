summary: "# Summary of Procedural Legislation  This bill is a procedural measure that establishes the rules for House floor consideration of three separate legislative items. It does not itself create policy but rather sets the process by which the House will debate and vote on these three proposals.  The bill addresses three main matters: (1) H.J. Res. 25, which would disapprove an IRS rule requiring brokers to report gross proceeds from digital asset (cryptocurrency) sales; (2) H.R. 1156, which would extend the statute of limitations for prosecuting fraud related to unemployment benefits under the CARES Act; and (3) H.R. 1968, which would provide continuing appropriations funding for federal government operations through fiscal year 2025 and make other extensions.  This type of procedural bill (often called a \"rule\") is required before the House can consider multiple bills. It determines details such as debate time, permitted amendments, and voting procedures for each item. The measure affects all members of Congress and the general public, since the three underlying bills would impact tax reporting requirements, unemployment fraud enforcement, and federal government funding."
