summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes amendments to the federal low-income housing tax credit (LIHTC) program, a tax incentive that encourages private investment in affordable rental housing. The modifications aim to direct tax credits toward projects that meet specific development criteria: affordable housing located near public transportation (transit-oriented development), and housing projects built in economically challenging areas designated as difficult development areas. The bill affects developers, investors, and financial institutions that participate in affordable housing projects, as well as low-income renters who may benefit from increased affordable housing supply in targeted locations.  The key provisions would likely include adjustments to how tax credits are allocated or valued based on project characteristics such as proximity to public transit and location in distressed areas. By offering enhanced incentives for these project types, the bill seeks to influence where affordable housing gets built—prioritizing locations with existing or planned transportation infrastructure and areas facing economic development challenges. This approach aims to simultaneously address affordable housing shortages and support broader urban development and economic revitalization goals in underserved communities."
