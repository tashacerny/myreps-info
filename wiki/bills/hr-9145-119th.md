summary: "# Summary of Federal Lands Recreation Enhancement Act Amendment  **What the Bill Proposes**  This bill would amend the Federal Lands Recreation Enhancement Act to provide free annual passes to licensed foster families. The passes would grant access to federal recreation lands and facilities that typically require an entrance fee or annual pass for admission. By providing free passes to foster families, the bill aims to reduce or eliminate a financial barrier to accessing federally managed recreational areas.  **Who It Affects**  The primary beneficiaries would be licensed foster families—both foster parents and the children in their care. The bill would also affect federal land management agencies (such as the National Park Service and U.S. Forest Service) that administer the pass program and collect related fees. State and local child welfare systems that license foster families would likely play a role in verifying eligibility.  **Key Provisions**  While the full bill text is unavailable, the central provision would establish eligibility for free annual recreation passes based on licensed foster family status. The amendment would need to address implementation details such as how foster families would obtain passes, what documentation would verify licensed status, and how agencies would distribute passes without disrupting existing fee structures."
