The rule being targeted would establish minimum efficiency requirements for this category of appliances. This legislation uses the Congressional Review Act (Chapter 8 of Title 5, U.S. Code), a process that allows Congress to overturn federal regulations within a specified timeframe after their issuance.  The bill would primarily affect manufacturers of gas-fired instantaneous water heaters and consumers who purchase these appliances. If approved, it would prevent the DOE's efficiency standards from taking effect, maintaining previous standards or allowing manufacturers to continue operating under existing requirements. The Congressional Review Act is a procedural tool that requires a simple majority vote in both chambers to pass and cannot be filibustered in the Senate.
