summary: "# Bill Summary: Excise Tax on Remittance Transfers  **What the Bill Proposes**  This bill would modify the excise tax on remittance transfers under the Internal Revenue Code of 1986. Remittance transfers are money transfers sent from individuals in the United States to recipients in foreign countries, typically used by immigrants to send funds to family members abroad. The bill adjusts how this excise tax is applied, though without the full text, the specific nature of the modifications—whether they increase, decrease, or restructure the tax—cannot be determined from the title alone.  **Who It Affects**  The bill would primarily affect individuals who send money internationally, money transfer service providers and financial institutions that process these transfers, and recipients of remittances in foreign countries. Immigrants, migrant workers, and families relying on international money transfers would likely experience direct or indirect effects depending on how the tax changes are structured.  **Key Considerations**  Any modifications to remittance transfer taxation could influence the cost of sending money abroad, the volume of transfers processed, and potentially the methods people use to move funds internationally. The bill's broader implications would depend on whether changes increase or decrease the tax burden and how they are structured relative to existing regulations."
