summary: "# Bill Summary: ARFID Recognition and Support  This bill seeks to formally recognize Avoidant/Restrictive Food Intake Disorder (ARFID) as a serious feeding and eating disorder at the federal level. ARFID is characterized by persistent difficulty eating certain foods, which can result in nutritional deficiencies, weight loss, or dependence on nutritional supplements. The disorder differs from anorexia nervosa or bulimia in that it is not primarily driven by concerns about body image or weight. The bill calls for increased federal attention to advancing awareness of the condition, improving early identification methods, and expanding research into its causes and treatments.  The bill affects individuals diagnosed with ARFID—including children, adolescents, and adults—as well as healthcare providers, researchers, and the families of those living with the disorder. It also impacts federal agencies involved in health research and public health initiatives.  Key provisions likely include calls to increase public and professional awareness of ARFID; support for early detection programs in educational and clinical settings; funding or promotion of research into the disorder's etiology and treatment; and efforts to ensure equitable access to evidence-based care and mental health services for those affected. The bill is a recognition and advocacy measure rather than a funding or regulatory mandate, though it may direct agencies to prioritize ARFID in existing health programs."
