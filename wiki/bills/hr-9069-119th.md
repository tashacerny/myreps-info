summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would impose restrictions on the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) regarding immigration enforcement operations and detention practices. Specifically, it would prohibit DHS from detaining children and would restrict or prohibit the detention of individuals with cognitive disabilities. The bill would also require that immigration enforcement actions at sensitive locations—such as schools, hospitals, courthouses, and places of worship—only occur when DHS has obtained a court-issued criminal warrant, rather than allowing enforcement based on administrative warrants or other immigration authorities.  The bill affects DHS immigration enforcement personnel, immigration detainees, individuals with cognitive disabilities, families with children in immigration proceedings, and operations at sensitive institutions. By limiting where and under what circumstances enforcement can occur, the measure also impacts schools, healthcare facilities, and courts that serve immigrant populations.  Key provisions would establish new categorical prohibitions on detention for certain vulnerable populations and create a warrant requirement for enforcement at locations where people seek essential services or protection. The practical effect would be to shift how and where DHS can conduct immigration enforcement activities."
