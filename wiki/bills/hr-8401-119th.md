summary: "# Summary  This bill proposes to amend the Marine Mammal Protection Act of 1972 to create an exception allowing the transport, purchase, and sale of northern sea otter pelts and products made from sea otters (such as handicrafts, garments, and art) in Southcentral and Southeast Alaska. Currently, the Marine Mammal Protection Act generally prohibits the sale of marine mammal parts, though it does permit Alaska Native subsistence hunting of sea otters. This bill would extend those permissions by allowing the commercial trade of materials derived from sea otters that are lawfully taken for subsistence purposes in these specific Alaska regions.  The bill affects Alaska Native communities in Southcentral and Southeast Alaska who engage in subsistence sea otter hunting, as well as artisans and craftspeople who work with sea otter materials. It also impacts the market for sea otter products in these regions. The key provision is the creation of a limited exception to the Marine Mammal Protection Act's prohibition on selling marine mammal parts, specifically for sea otter products from subsistence hunts in the named Alaska regions."
