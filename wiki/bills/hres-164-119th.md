summary: "# Summary of H. Res. 23 Consideration Resolution  This resolution provides the procedural framework for the House of Representatives to consider H. Res. 23, which proposes to allow parents serving as members of Congress to vote remotely by proxy. The resolution governs how the House will debate and vote on the underlying parental remote voting proposal.  H. Res. 23 itself would establish a new remote voting option specifically for members who are parents, presumably to accommodate childcare responsibilities and parenting duties. Under this arrangement, affected members could authorize another member to cast votes on their behalf while they are absent from the chamber. This would supplement existing proxy voting procedures available to all members.  The resolution affects members of Congress with parenting responsibilities and potentially House operations more broadly. Key aspects would likely include defining eligibility criteria for parental remote voting, procedures for designating proxy voters, and any limitations on how frequently or under what circumstances the option could be used. The exact provisions and scope would be determined in the full text of both the procedural resolution and the underlying policy resolution."
