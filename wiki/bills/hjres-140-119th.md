summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill uses the Congressional Review Act (CRA) to disapprove a rule issued by the Bureau of Land Management (BLM) related to Public Land Order No. 7917. That order withdrew federal lands from certain uses in Cook, Lake, and Saint Louis Counties, Minnesota. If passed, this bill would reverse the BLM's rule and restore those lands to their previous status, allowing them to potentially be used for activities that were prohibited under the withdrawal order.  The bill affects federal land management in a specific region of northeastern Minnesota and impacts any parties with interests in using or developing those lands. The affected counties and any stakeholders who opposed the original withdrawal—such as mining companies, recreational users, or others seeking resource access—would be the primary beneficiaries of disapproval. Conversely, those who supported the original withdrawal for conservation or environmental reasons would see their protections removed.  Under the Congressional Review Act process, if both chambers pass this disapproval resolution, the BLM rule cannot be reissued in substantially the same form without new congressional authorization. The CRA is a procedural tool that allows Congress to overturn recent agency rules with a simple majority vote, bypassing the standard rulemaking process."
