summary: "# Summary of Supreme Court Term Limits Amendment Proposal  This bill proposes a constitutional amendment that would establish term limits for U.S. Supreme Court justices. Currently, the Constitution provides that justices serve \"during good behaviour,\" which has been interpreted to mean lifetime appointments. This proposal would change that arrangement by limiting the length of time an individual justice may serve on the Court.  The amendment would affect all Supreme Court justices and would establish a specific term length for future appointments. The exact length of the proposed terms—whether 18 years, 10 years, or another duration—would depend on the specific language of the amendment. The change would apply going forward and would alter the appointment process by allowing presidents more frequent opportunities to nominate justices throughout their tenure and subsequent administrations.  To become part of the Constitution, this amendment would need to pass both chambers of Congress with a two-thirds supermajority in each, then be ratified by three-fourths of the state legislatures. This high threshold reflects the difficulty intentionally built into the constitutional amendment process."
