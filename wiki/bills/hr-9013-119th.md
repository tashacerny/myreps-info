summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend federal law to classify the unlawful interstate transportation of fireworks as \"specified unlawful activity\" under money laundering statutes. Currently, money laundering laws apply to a defined list of serious crimes. This change would add illegal fireworks transport to that list, making it possible to prosecute individuals who move money through financial systems in connection with illegal fireworks trafficking.  The bill would primarily affect businesses and individuals engaged in the illegal interstate transport of fireworks, as well as those who may finance or profit from such activities. It would expand the scope of federal money laundering charges, allowing prosecutors to pursue financial crimes connected to fireworks trafficking. This could result in enhanced penalties for violators, since money laundering charges typically carry significant criminal sentences.  The specific mechanics of the bill—such as whether it applies only to certain types of fireworks or all unlawful transport—cannot be determined from the title alone. The practical impact would depend on how federal authorities enforce the provision and the prevalence of organized fireworks trafficking operations that generate sufficient proceeds to attract money laundering investigations."
