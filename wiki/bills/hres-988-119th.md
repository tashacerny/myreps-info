summary: "# Summary of Procedural Measure on Labor and Retirement Bills  This is a procedural resolution that sets the terms for House floor consideration of five separate labor and employment bills. Rather than substantive legislation itself, it establishes the rules—such as debate time and amendment procedures—that will govern how these bills are debated and voted on in the full House.  The five bills covered address different aspects of labor law. H.R. 2988 would modify pension regulations under the Employee Retirement Income Security Act regarding how financial and non-financial factors are evaluated. H.R. 2262 and H.R. 2270 would amend overtime and wage calculations under the Fair Labor Standards Act, specifically by excluding certain childcare-related activities and expenses from overtime compensation calculations. H.R. 2312 would revise the definition of \"tipped employees\" under wage and hour law. H.R. 4366 would clarify how two or more employers can be classified as \"joint employers\" under labor law—a significant issue affecting liability and worker protections.  These bills would primarily affect employers, employees, pension plan sponsors, and workers in sectors including hospitality, childcare, and franchised businesses. The joint employer provision would have particular relevance for franchise operations and staffing arrangements."
