summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) of 1993 and federal civil service law to explicitly classify organ donation surgery as a \"serious health condition.\" Under current law, a serious health condition typically involves inpatient care or continuing treatment by a healthcare provider. By clarifying that organ donation qualifies, the bill would ensure that employees who donate organs are eligible to take job-protected leave under the FMLA.  The bill would affect private-sector employees covered by the FMLA and federal government employees. It would allow organ donors to take unpaid, job-protected leave for the surgery and recovery period without risk of losing their jobs or benefits. This change could encourage organ donation by removing potential workplace barriers that donors might face.  The key provision is the definitional clarification that adds organ donation surgery to the list of conditions qualifying as \"serious health conditions\" under federal leave law. No other major changes to the FMLA framework are indicated by the bill's title."
