summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill is a congressional disapproval resolution under the Congressional Review Act (CRA). It seeks to reject a rule issued by the Bureau of Land Management (BLM) related to a Record of Decision and Approved Resource Management Plan for the North Dakota Field Office. Resource Management Plans guide how the BLM manages federal lands under its jurisdiction, including decisions about land use, conservation, recreation, and resource extraction. The specific plan addressed in this bill covers BLM-administered lands in North Dakota.  If passed, this resolution would invalidate the North Dakota Field Office plan and prevent the BLM from issuing substantially the same rule in the future without new congressional authorization. The disapproval would affect individuals, businesses, and organizations with interests in how federal lands in North Dakota are managed—including energy companies, ranchers, conservation groups, outdoor recreation operators, and state and local governments.  The bill does not propose new legislation; rather, it uses the expedited congressional review process to overturn an existing regulatory action. The specific impacts of disapproving the rule would depend on the particular provisions in the North Dakota Field Office plan, which are not detailed in the bill title alone."
