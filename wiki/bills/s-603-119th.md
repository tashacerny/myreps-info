summary: "# Bill Summary: General George C. Marshall House Designation  This bill proposes to designate the General George C. Marshall House located in Virginia as an affiliated area of the National Park System. An affiliated area designation allows a historic site to be officially associated with the National Park System while potentially remaining under non-federal management, such as by a state or local organization. This designation would recognize the historical significance of the property and potentially increase its visibility and access to visitors.  The bill affects the Marshall House property itself and any organization that manages it, as well as the National Park Service, which would add the site to its system of affiliated areas. The designation may also affect visitors and the local community in Virginia where the house is located. Key provisions likely include the official recognition of the site's historical importance and possibly authorization for the National Park Service to provide technical assistance, interpretive support, or other services related to the property's management and preservation."
