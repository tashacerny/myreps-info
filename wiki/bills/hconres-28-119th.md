summary: "# Bill Summary  This resolution expresses Congress's position that votes cast by overseas servicemembers must be counted and honored in accordance with the Uniformed and Overseas Citizens Absentee Voting Act (UOCAVA). The measure does not create new law or requirements, but rather states Congress's sense—a formal expression of legislative intent—regarding the proper treatment of absentee ballots from military personnel and other U.S. citizens living abroad.  The resolution affects overseas servicemembers who are eligible to vote under UOCAVA, a federal law enacted in 1986 that establishes voting procedures for this population. It also applies to state and local election officials responsible for processing absentee ballots from military voters. Key to the resolution is the principle that votes from these voters should be counted consistent with existing federal law requirements.  The measure emphasizes Congress's commitment to ensuring that military voters' ballots receive proper handling and are included in election results as the law requires. While a sense-of-Congress resolution carries no binding legal authority, it signals to election officials and other stakeholders the legislative branch's position on the importance of counting overseas servicemembers' votes."
