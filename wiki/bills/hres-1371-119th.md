summary: "# Bill Summary: World Sickle Cell Awareness Day  This bill expresses congressional support for designating a \"World Sickle Cell Awareness Day\" to increase public understanding of sickle cell disease in the United States and globally. The measure emphasizes the importance of raising awareness about the disease itself and the medical needs associated with it, including the necessity for continued research funding and development of new treatments.  The bill identifies several key areas where awareness and action are needed: early detection and screening programs, research to develop novel and more effective treatments, efforts toward finding a cure, and preventative care programs designed to reduce complications from sickle cell anemia and related conditions. By supporting a designated awareness day, the bill aims to draw attention to these priorities and encourage continued focus on addressing sickle cell disease as a public health matter.  This is a symbolic resolution rather than legislation that creates new programs or funding. It does not directly affect specific populations or create new requirements, but rather signals congressional support for raising awareness about sickle cell disease among the general public and the international community."
