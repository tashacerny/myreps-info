summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would grant the Secretary of State authority to extend the terms of limited consular appointments from their standard length to eight years, with the possibility of an additional two-year extension if the Foreign Service determines there is an operational need. Limited consular appointments are temporary positions in U.S. consulates abroad, typically filled by foreign nationals or U.S. citizens on non-career tracks. The bill affects consular staff employed at U.S. embassies and consulates worldwide.  The key provision allows the Secretary of State to approve longer appointment terms—up to a maximum of ten years total—when circumstances warrant. This flexibility would enable the State Department to retain experienced consular personnel in their positions for extended periods, rather than requiring appointment renewals or replacements at fixed intervals. The bill appears designed to address staffing continuity and operational efficiency in consular operations."
