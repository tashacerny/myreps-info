summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to modify the tax code's depreciation rules for equipment used in draft alcohol production (such as beer and wine dispensing systems). Currently, such equipment is classified under standard depreciation schedules. The bill would reclassify \"qualified energy-efficient draft alcohol property\" as 15-year property, meaning businesses could spread the cost of these assets over 15 years for tax depreciation purposes.  The primary beneficiaries would be breweries, wineries, bars, restaurants, and other businesses that use draft systems for serving beer, wine, or similar beverages. The change would apply only to equipment meeting certain energy-efficiency standards, narrowing the scope to environmentally efficient systems.  The key provision establishes a 15-year depreciation period for qualifying equipment, which could reduce annual taxable income for affected businesses in the years following purchase. This change would lower tax liability for businesses investing in energy-efficient draft systems, while reducing federal tax revenue during the depreciation period."
