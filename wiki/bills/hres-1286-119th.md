The resolution does not establish specific trade agreements or tariffs; rather, it expresses a stated preference for trade policies that consider these five constituencies in their design and impact.  The resolution affects policymakers responsible for trade negotiations and implementation, including the President, the U.S. Trade Representative, and Congress. It also broadly concerns the groups named—workers in domestic industries, consumers purchasing imported and domestic goods, farmers operating independently (as opposed to large agricultural corporations), small business owners engaged in trade, and entities with environmental interests.  Key aspects of the resolution include its emphasis on balancing potentially competing interests: for example, protections that benefit domestic workers might affect consumer prices, while environmental standards could impact trade competitiveness. The resolution signals that future trade policy should aim to consider all five areas rather than prioritizing some interests over others.
