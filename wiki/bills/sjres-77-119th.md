summary: "# Bill Summary  This joint resolution would terminate the national emergency that was declared to impose tariffs (import duties) on goods coming from Canada. A national emergency declaration allows the President to take executive actions without requiring Congressional approval through the normal legislative process. This resolution uses Congress's authority to end such a declaration, which would remove the legal basis for any tariffs imposed under that emergency authority.  The bill would affect importers, businesses that rely on Canadian goods, and consumers who purchase products made from Canadian imports or materials. It would also impact U.S. exporters to Canada, as terminating the U.S. emergency declaration could lead to the removal of Canadian retaliatory tariffs on American products.  The key provision is the termination itself—if passed, it would nullify the emergency declaration, which would typically result in the removal of associated tariffs unless they were separately authorized through other legal mechanisms. Such a resolution requires passage in both the House and Senate and the President's signature to take effect (or a two-thirds majority in both chambers to override a presidential veto)."
