Commemorative coins are special-edition coins issued in limited quantities to mark significant national events. This legislation would allow the Mint to create coins celebrating these international sporting competitions and their connection to the United States.  **Who It Affects**  The bill affects the U.S. Mint, which would be responsible for producing the coins, and potentially collectors and the public who purchase commemorative coins. Depending on the bill's specific terms, it may also benefit the organizations that host or organize the Olympic and Paralympic Games, as commemorative coin programs sometimes include revenue-sharing provisions that direct proceeds to relevant organizations.  **Key Provisions**  While the full text is unavailable, commemorative coin legislation typically specifies: the design and number of coins to be issued, the denominations involved, when sales will occur, and the surcharge (if any) on each coin's sale price.
