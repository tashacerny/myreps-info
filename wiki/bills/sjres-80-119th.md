summary: "# Bill Summary  This joint resolution seeks to disapprove a rule issued by the Bureau of Land Management (BLM) regarding the National Petroleum Reserve in Alaska (NPRA) Integrated Activity Plan. Under the Congressional Review Act (Chapter 8 of Title 5, U.S. Code), Congress can vote to disapprove federal agency rules within a specified timeframe. If passed, this resolution would invalidate the BLM's rule and prevent the agency from issuing substantially the same rule without explicit congressional authorization.  The rule being challenged establishes a Record of Decision for how the NPRA will be managed and developed. This affects oil and gas companies operating or seeking to operate in the reserve, federal land management agencies, environmental stakeholders, and Alaska communities that depend on resource development or have interests in the region's conservation. The NPRA covers approximately 23 million acres in northern Alaska.  The key provision is the disapproval mechanism itself: if both chambers of Congress pass this joint resolution and it is signed into law (or passes over a presidential veto), the BLM rule would be nullified. This represents a congressional override of an executive branch regulatory decision, shifting policy direction back to whatever rules or practices preceded this Record of Decision."
