summary: "# Bill Summary  This resolution expresses the House of Representatives' position that mifepristone—a medication used in medical abortion—should be recognized as safe and effective based on decades of real-world evidence and peer-reviewed research. The resolution calls for law and policy regarding access to medication abortion to be grounded in scientific evidence, applied equitably across the country, and made transparent to the public.  The resolution does not propose new laws or regulations. Instead, it is a statement of sentiment intended to affirm the House's view that medication abortion policy should prioritize scientific findings. The resolution would affect federal policy discussions and potentially influence how federal agencies approach regulations governing mifepristone access. It may also carry symbolic weight in ongoing national debates about abortion medication policy.  Key elements of the resolution include recognizing 25+ years of safety and efficacy data, emphasizing peer-reviewed studies, and calling for equitable and transparent access policies based on scientific evidence. As a sense-of-the-House resolution, it does not carry the force of law but represents the expressed position of the legislative body on this policy matter."
