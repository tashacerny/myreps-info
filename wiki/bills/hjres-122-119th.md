summary: "# Bill Summary  This proposal would amend the U.S. Constitution to expand the authority of Congress and state legislatures to regulate money in elections. Specifically, it would allow Congress and the states to impose limits on campaign contributions and expenditures made by individuals, organizations, and other entities with the intent to influence election outcomes. The amendment would also authorize Congress and states to establish systems for public financing of political campaigns.  The amendment would affect candidates, political parties, donors, interest groups, and other entities that participate in or fund election activities. It would also affect voters and taxpayers, depending on how public financing systems are designed and funded.  The key provision grants legislative bodies the power to regulate and limit campaign spending and contributions, as well as to create alternative funding mechanisms for campaigns using public resources. The amendment would essentially reverse or modify the effect of court decisions that have limited campaign finance regulations on constitutional grounds, allowing elected officials greater discretion to write campaign finance laws at both the federal and state levels."
