summary: "# Bill Summary: Overtime Pay Rate Adjustment  This bill proposes to amend the Fair Labor Standards Act of 1938 by increasing the overtime pay rate that employers must provide to eligible employees. Currently, the law requires employers to pay overtime compensation at one and one-half times (1.5x) an employee's regular hourly rate for hours worked over 40 hours per week. This bill would raise that requirement to two times (2x) the regular rate, representing a significant increase in overtime compensation.  The bill would affect all employers subject to the Fair Labor Standards Act, as well as the millions of employees who are eligible for overtime pay. The change would apply broadly across industries and sectors, though certain workers exempted under current law (such as salaried executives, professionals, and administrative employees above specified salary thresholds) would likely remain exempt. Employers would be required to adjust their payroll practices and compensation structures to comply with the new overtime rate.  The primary provision is the change to the overtime multiplier itself, from 1.5x to 2x regular pay. This would increase labor costs for employers with overtime-eligible workforces, while providing increased compensation to employees who work overtime hours. The bill does not appear to address other aspects of overtime law such as which workers qualify for overtime protection or how the regular rate of pay is calculated."
