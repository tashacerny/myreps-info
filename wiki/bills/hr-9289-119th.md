summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to repeal the tax credit for individual contributions to scholarship granting organizations under the Internal Revenue Code. Currently, taxpayers in certain states can claim a tax credit (a dollar-for-dollar reduction in taxes owed) when they donate money to organizations that distribute scholarships to students. This bill would eliminate that credit, meaning individuals would no longer receive this tax benefit for such donations.  The bill would primarily affect individual donors who contribute to scholarship granting organizations, as well as the scholarship organizations themselves, which may see reduced funding if the tax incentive is removed. Students who receive scholarships from these organizations could also be indirectly affected if donations decline. The specific states where this credit currently applies, and the extent of the revenue impact, would depend on the full legislative text.  The stated purpose is to repeal this tax credit mechanism.
