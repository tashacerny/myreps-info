summary: "# Bill Summary  This proposal seeks to amend the U.S. Constitution to establish that the Supreme Court must have exactly nine justices. Currently, the Constitution does not specify the Court's size; Congress has the authority to set the number of justices through legislation, which it has changed several times throughout history. This amendment would remove that flexibility by making nine justices a constitutional requirement, meaning any future change to the Court's size would require a constitutional amendment rather than a simple act of Congress.  The amendment would affect the Supreme Court's structure and any future efforts to expand or reduce its membership. It would also constrain Congress's current constitutional power to determine the Court's size. The amendment would apply to all future sessions of Congress and could only be altered through the constitutional amendment process, which requires approval by two-thirds of both chambers of Congress and ratification by three-fourths of the states."
