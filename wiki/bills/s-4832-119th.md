summary: "# Summary  This bill proposes to amend the Indian Self-Determination and Education Assistance Act to expand the Secretary of Agriculture's authority to contract with Indian Tribes and Tribal organizations. Specifically, it would allow these contracts to cover the operation of supplemental nutrition assistance programs (commonly known as SNAP or food assistance programs). Currently, the Indian Self-Determination and Education Assistance Act authorizes self-determination contracts primarily for health, education, and social services, but this bill would add nutrition assistance to the list of programs that tribes can manage through such contracts.  The bill would affect Indian Tribes and Tribal organizations seeking greater control over nutrition assistance programs serving their communities, as well as the Department of Agriculture, which administers SNAP. The key provision is the authorization for self-determination contracts specifically for supplemental nutrition assistance programs, allowing tribes to plan, conduct, and control these programs rather than having them administered solely by federal or state agencies. This follows the existing self-determination model used in other tribal service areas, giving tribes increased autonomy in managing programs that serve their populations."
