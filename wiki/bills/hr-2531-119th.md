summary: "# Summary: Workplace Violence Prevention for Health Care and Social Service Workers Act  This bill would establish federal requirements for workplace violence prevention programs in health care and social service settings. The legislation aims to reduce incidents of violence—both physical assaults and threats—that workers in these fields experience while performing their jobs. The bill would likely require covered employers to implement safety protocols, training programs, and incident reporting procedures designed to identify, prevent, and respond to workplace violence.  The bill would apply to hospitals, clinics, nursing homes, mental health facilities, and social service agencies, affecting millions of health care and social service workers nationwide. Key provisions would typically include mandatory risk assessments, development of written violence prevention plans, staff training on de-escalation and safety procedures, and procedures for reporting and investigating incidents. The bill may also establish standards for workplace design and security measures intended to reduce worker exposure to violence.  The legislation would create a new federal mandate affecting how these employers operate and allocate resources toward safety infrastructure and training. Implementation would require agencies to establish oversight mechanisms and compliance procedures, with potential penalties for non-compliance."
