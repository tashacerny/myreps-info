summary: "# Bill Summary: PDMP Compliance for Veterinarians  This bill proposes to strengthen compliance requirements for veterinarians regarding the Prescription Drug Monitoring Program (PDMP). PDMPs are state-run databases that track controlled substance prescriptions to help prevent drug diversion and misuse. The bill would extend or clarify PDMP participation and reporting obligations to veterinarians, who prescribe controlled medications for animal treatment. The measure aims to bring veterinary prescribing practices into closer alignment with the monitoring systems already used for human medicine.  The bill would primarily affect licensed veterinarians and veterinary practices that prescribe controlled substances. It would also impact state pharmacy boards and regulatory agencies responsible for administering PDMPs, as they may need to update systems and enforcement mechanisms. Veterinary patients and their owners could be affected if new reporting requirements change prescribing procedures or access to medications for animals.  While the exact provisions cannot be detailed without the full text, the bill likely includes requirements for veterinarians to register with their state PDMP, query the database before prescribing controlled substances, and report prescription data. The measure may also specify penalties for non-compliance and establish timelines for implementation. The stated goal is to increase transparency in veterinary drug distribution and reduce opportunities for prescription drug diversion through the veterinary channel."
