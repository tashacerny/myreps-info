summary: "# Summary of National Day of Reason Resolution  This resolution expresses congressional support for designating May 4, 2026, as a \"National Day of Reason.\" The measure is a non-binding expression of sentiment rather than legislation that creates new laws or programs. It recognizes what the resolution characterizes as the importance of reason and rational thinking to human progress and improvement.  The resolution does not create any new federal requirements, spending, or regulatory changes. It affects no specific groups in a legal sense, as it is a symbolic designation. May 4 was selected to coincide with the annual celebration of Reason organized by various secular and humanist organizations. The resolution essentially asks Congress to formally acknowledge the value of reason and evidence-based thinking as contributions to human welfare and advancement.  This type of resolution allows Congress to make formal statements on topics of perceived importance without implementing policy changes. It serves as a congressional acknowledgment of the designated day but carries no enforcement mechanism or direct practical impact."
