summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would amend federal law to remove all existing exceptions that currently allow certain non-citizens to vote in U.S. elections. Under current law, there are limited circumstances in which non-citizens may be permitted to vote in specific local or territorial elections. This bill would eliminate those exceptions and establish a blanket prohibition on voting by any person who is not a U.S. citizen in all federal, state, and local elections.  The bill would primarily affect non-citizens residing in the United States, including permanent residents (green card holders), temporary visa holders, and undocumented immigrants. It could also impact jurisdictions that currently permit non-citizen voting in municipal or school board elections, requiring those areas to change their voting eligibility rules to comply with federal law.  Key provisions would strike existing statutory exceptions from Title 18 (federal criminal code) that relate to non-citizen voting eligibility. The exact scope and implementation details cannot be fully determined without access to the complete bill text, but the stated purpose is to create uniform federal prohibition on non-citizen voting across all election levels."
