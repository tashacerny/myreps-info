summary: "# Summary of the Mullica River Watershed Wild and Scenic River Study Act of 2026  **What the Bill Proposes**  This bill directs the federal government to conduct a study of the Mullica River watershed to determine whether it meets the criteria for designation as a Wild and Scenic River under the Wild and Scenic Rivers Act. The study would assess the river's natural, cultural, recreational, and scenic values, as well as examine current conditions and potential impacts of designation. Based on the study's findings, Congress would then decide whether to formally add the Mullica River to the National Wild and Scenic Rivers System.  **Who It Affects**  The bill primarily affects property owners, local governments, and communities in the Mullica River watershed area (located in New Jersey). It also involves the federal agencies responsible for conducting the study and managing any future designation. Recreational users, conservation organizations, and businesses operating in or near the watershed may also be affected, depending on the study's outcome and any subsequent designation.  **Key Provisions**  The bill likely requires the Department of Interior or relevant federal agency to complete the study within a specified timeframe and submit findings to Congress. A Wild and Scenic River designation, if approved, would impose certain protections and restrictions on development and resource use in designated sections of the river to preserve its natural and scenic character, though the specific restrictions would depend on the designation category selected."
