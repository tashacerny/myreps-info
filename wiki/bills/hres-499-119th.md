summary: "# Summary of H.R. 4 Consideration Bill  This bill is a procedural measure that sets the terms for the House to consider H.R. 4, which deals with budget rescissions. The bill would allow Congress to vote on rescinding (canceling) certain federal spending that the President proposed to rescind in special messages sent to Congress on June 3, 2025. These rescissions are being processed under the Impoundment Control Act of 1974, which is the law governing how the President can propose to cancel previously appropriated funds.  The bill affects the federal budget and any agencies or programs targeted in the President's rescission proposals from June 3, 2025. Without access to the specific list of proposed rescissions, the exact programs and amounts cannot be detailed here. Generally, rescission bills allow Congress to approve or reject the President's proposals to reduce spending in particular accounts. This procedural bill determines how and when the House will debate and vote on those proposals.  The measure is a \"rule\" for House floor consideration, meaning it establishes the debate parameters and voting procedures the chamber will follow when considering the rescission bill itself. Such procedural bills are routine in the legislative process and do not themselves authorize or cut spending—they simply establish the process for considering legislation that would do so."
