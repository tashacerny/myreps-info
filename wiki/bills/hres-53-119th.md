summary: "# Summary of Bill  This is a procedural resolution that sets the terms for floor consideration of two separate bills in the House of Representatives. It does not itself create policy but rather establishes the rules—such as debate time and amendment procedures—under which these two bills will be debated and voted on.  The first bill covered (H.R. 471) addresses forest management on federal and tribal lands. It proposes to streamline the environmental review process under the National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA) for forest management activities aimed at reducing fire risk and improving forest health on National Forest System lands, Bureau of Land Management public lands, and Tribal lands. The bill is intended to expedite projects that remove excess vegetation and restore resilience to overgrown, fire-prone forests.  The second bill covered (S. 5) concerns immigration enforcement. It would require the Secretary of Homeland Security to take into custody aliens (non-citizens) who have been charged in the United States with theft. This affects immigration enforcement priorities and detention policies. Both bills are advanced together under this single procedural resolution, allowing the House to establish debate and voting parameters for each."
