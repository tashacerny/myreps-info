summary: "# Bill Summary: Long-Term Trust Land Leases for Indian Tribes  This bill would authorize federally recognized Indian Tribes to lease land held in trust by the federal government for periods of up to 99 years. Currently, trust land leases are limited to shorter terms. The bill would expand the maximum lease duration, giving tribes greater flexibility in how they use and develop tribal lands for economic purposes.  The bill would affect federally recognized Indian Tribes and entities seeking to lease tribal trust lands, including businesses and organizations entering into long-term arrangements with tribes. Key provisions would include establishing the 99-year lease authorization as the new maximum term allowed for trust land leases. This change could facilitate long-term economic development projects, infrastructure investments, or resource management agreements that require extended timelines to be financially viable.  The practical effect would be to give tribes more control over lease terms and potentially improve their ability to attract investment for projects requiring multi-decade commitments, such as agricultural operations, renewable energy development, or commercial ventures."
