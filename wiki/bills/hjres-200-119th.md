summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes a congressional disapproval resolution regarding a foreign military sale to Turkey. Under the Arms Export Control Act, Congress has the authority to review and potentially block proposed sales of U.S. defense articles and services to foreign countries. This resolution would exercise that authority to disapprove a specific arms sale package to Turkey that the executive branch has proposed.  The resolution affects the Turkish government (the proposed recipient of defense equipment and services) and U.S. defense contractors involved in the sale. If passed, it would prevent the sale from proceeding as proposed. Congress typically files such disapproval resolutions when members have concerns about the proposed sale—which could relate to the recipient country's human rights record, regional stability, strategic alignment, or other foreign policy considerations.  Without access to the full bill text, the specific defense articles, services, and stated rationale cannot be detailed.
