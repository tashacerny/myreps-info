summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill uses the Congressional Review Act (a procedure allowing Congress to disapprove federal regulations) to block a Department of Energy rule issued by the Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy. The rule establishes energy efficiency standards for commercial refrigerators, freezers, and refrigerator-freezer units. If passed, this bill would prevent the rule from taking effect and prohibit the Department of Energy from issuing substantially similar rules without new legislative authorization.  The rule would directly affect manufacturers of commercial refrigeration equipment and businesses that purchase or operate such equipment, including grocery stores, restaurants, food service providers, and other commercial facilities. The efficiency standards would likely increase manufacturing costs and product prices, though they would reduce operating costs through lower energy consumption over time.  The bill contains no additional provisions—it is a straightforward disapproval measure that requires a simple majority vote in both chambers. Congress may use the Congressional Review Act to overturn regulations within 60 legislative days of their submission. The bill does not propose alternative standards or modify the Department of Energy's regulatory authority; it simply rejects this particular rulemaking."
