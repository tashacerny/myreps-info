summary: "# Bill Summary  This resolution commemorates the 70th anniversary of the United States Forest Service Institute of Pacific Islands Forestry. A commemorative resolution is a legislative measure that honors or recognizes a specific event, organization, or milestone without creating new laws or appropriating funds. In this case, Congress is marking seven decades of the Institute's establishment and operations.  The Institute of Pacific Islands Forestry is a research and technical organization within the Forest Service that focuses on forestry issues specific to Pacific island regions, including territories and jurisdictions in the Pacific Ocean. The resolution acknowledges the Institute's work over 70 years, which typically involves research, resource management, and forestry expertise relevant to the unique environmental and ecological conditions of Pacific island communities.  This commemorative measure affects the Forest Service and Pacific island communities by formally recognizing the Institute's contributions to forestry and natural resource management in the region. As a resolution, it carries symbolic significance but does not establish new policies, create obligations, or require spending—it simply expresses Congressional recognition of the organization's anniversary."
