summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes two main amendments to existing federal law. First, it would amend the Public Health Service Act to establish a grant program that awards funds to eligible crisis centers. These grants would support follow-up services for individuals who have received suicide prevention and crisis intervention services, with the goal of providing continued care and support after someone reaches out to or interacts with a crisis center.  Second, the bill would amend the Communications Act of 1934 to improve accessibility of the 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, which is the three-digit dialing code for suicide prevention and crisis intervention services. This provision aims to enhance how people can access the crisis service, potentially through improved phone systems or broader availability.  The bill affects crisis centers and mental health service providers that would be eligible to receive grants, as well as the general public who would benefit from improved follow-up services and better access to the 988 crisis line. The phrase \"and for other purposes\" indicates there may be additional provisions not evident from the title alone."
