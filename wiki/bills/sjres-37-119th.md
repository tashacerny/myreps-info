summary: "# Bill Summary  This joint resolution proposes to terminate a national emergency declaration that was used to impose tariffs (duties) on goods imported from Canada. The resolution would end the legal basis for these tariffs by revoking the emergency declaration under which they were enacted.  The bill would affect trade between the United States and Canada, potentially reversing tariffs on Canadian imports. This impacts businesses and consumers who import or purchase Canadian goods, as well as Canadian exporters selling to the U.S. market. The key provision is the termination of the national emergency declaration itself, which would remove the executive authority to maintain the tariffs.  Under current law, a joint resolution is one mechanism Congress can use to terminate a national emergency declared by the President. If passed by both chambers and signed by the President (or passed over a presidential veto), the resolution would end the emergency declaration and typically require the removal of associated policies, including these tariffs."
