summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill expresses congressional opposition to digital services taxes (DSTs) and similar measures imposed by other countries on U.S. companies. Digital services taxes are levies that some nations have implemented targeting revenue from digital services—such as online advertising, e-commerce platforms, and data services—often applied at rates of 2–6% on qualifying transactions. The bill takes a position that these taxes unfairly target American technology and digital companies operating internationally.  The bill does not propose new legislation or impose direct requirements; rather, it is a resolution expressing the sense of Congress on this trade and tax policy issue. It signals congressional concern about what proponents view as discriminatory taxation that affects U.S. companies' competitiveness abroad. The measure affects stakeholders involved in digital commerce, including major U.S. technology firms, online retailers, and digital service providers that operate in countries with DSTs, as well as U.S. trade negotiators who handle international tax and trade disputes.  Without access to the full text, the specific provisions likely include statements opposing DSTs, potential directives to executive branch officials to address the matter in trade negotiations, and possibly recommendations regarding retaliatory measures or multilateral discussions on fair international taxation standards."
