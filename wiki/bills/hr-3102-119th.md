## What This Bill Does

The Clean Energy Transition Act sets a legally binding national standard requiring all electricity sold in the United States to come from carbon-free sources by January 1, 2040. It also creates a major federal program to support workers whose jobs are displaced by the shift away from fossil fuels.

### Clean Electricity Standard

Electric utilities and retail electricity providers must meet annual clean electricity benchmarks, starting at 60% carbon-free power by 2028, rising to 100% by 2040. Qualifying sources include solar, wind, geothermal, hydropower, and nuclear energy. Utilities that do not meet annual targets must purchase tradeable clean electricity credits from utilities that exceed their targets.

### Workforce Transition Fund

The bill creates a $40 billion fund administered by the Department of Labor to provide:
- Up to five years of wage replacement at 90% of prior salary for workers who lose jobs in coal mining, oil and gas extraction, or coal-fired power generation.
- Free enrollment in job training programs at community colleges for new careers in clean energy installation, grid maintenance, and related fields.
- Early retirement benefits for workers over 55 in affected industries.

### Grid Investment

An additional $25 billion is allocated to expand and modernize the electrical grid to accommodate higher shares of renewable energy, including long-distance transmission lines and battery storage systems.

## Who Does It Affect?

The legislation affects every electric utility, power plant operator, and retail electricity provider in the country. Workers in coal and oil-producing states would be eligible for transition support. Consumers could see changes in electricity rates, though the bill's sponsors argue that the long-term cost of renewable energy is lower than fossil fuels.

## Current Status

The bill passed the House in November 2025 with 228 votes in favor and 207 against. It has been referred to the Senate Committee on Energy and Natural Resources, which has scheduled hearings for early 2026.
