summary: "# Summary of Black Music Month Resolution  This resolution recognizes the historical contributions of Black Americans to U.S. music and designates June 2026 as \"Black Music Month.\" The resolution acknowledges the significant influence of Black musicians and musical traditions—including jazz, blues, gospel, hip-hop, and other genres—on American musical culture and the broader cultural landscape of the United States.  The resolution also emphasizes the need for increased access to music education for Black students. This reflects concerns about disparities in educational opportunities and calls for greater availability of music programs and instruction in schools serving Black communities.  The resolution is ceremonial in nature, serving to officially recognize these contributions and priorities through congressional action. It does not establish new funding, programs, or legal requirements, but rather expresses the sense of the legislature on the importance of Black musical heritage and equitable music education access."
