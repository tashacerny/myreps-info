summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would authorize the General Services Administration (GSA) and the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) to create programs for selling or transferring underutilized federal properties to eligible entities for economic redevelopment purposes. The bill establishes two mechanisms: a pilot program and a grant program. These initiatives would allow federal agencies to dispose of properties they are not fully using and direct them toward redevelopment projects that could generate economic activity.  The bill would affect federal agencies holding underutilized property, as well as eligible entities seeking to acquire and redevelop such properties—potentially including nonprofits, local governments, developers, or other organizations. The property transfers would occur under terms determined through these coordinated programs between GSA and HUD.  Key provisions would include authorizing the creation and administration of both the pilot and grant programs, establishing HUD as the administrator of the grant component, and enabling the transfer of federal properties to support economic development goals. The bill also includes language indicating additional purposes beyond those specified in the title, though the specific details would depend on the full legislative text."
