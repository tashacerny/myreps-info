The legislation mandates that relevant federal agencies or officials assess and document the impact of AI technologies on job creation, job displacement, wage levels, and workforce skills requirements across different industries and sectors of the economy.  The bill affects federal agencies responsible for labor, technology, or economic policy, as well as potentially private companies or research institutions that may be required to provide data for these reports. Workers and job seekers could be affected indirectly, as the findings could inform future workforce development or retraining programs. The reports would likely serve as a factual basis for understanding AI's labor market effects and could inform future policymaking decisions.  Key provisions would likely include: establishing a reporting timeline and frequency; defining which sectors or types of AI technologies are covered; specifying what data must be collected (such as job losses, new positions created, and skill gaps); and determining which agencies bear responsibility for conducting and publishing the reports. The bill's ultimate purpose appears to be creating transparency around AI's workforce impacts so Congress and the public have reliable information for decision-making."
