summary: "# Bill Summary  **Purpose and Scope** This bill would modify the Internal Revenue Code to change how taxpayers can use the low-income housing tax credit (LIHTC). Specifically, it would allow taxpayers to carry back unused tax credits for five years, rather than the current rules that limit or do not permit carryback of these credits in many cases.  **Who Is Affected** The bill would primarily affect developers, investors, and owners of low-income housing projects who receive the LIHTC. These entities could use excess credits from a given year to offset tax liabilities in prior years (up to five years back), rather than only carrying credits forward to future years. This change could improve cash flow for housing developers and make low-income housing projects more financially attractive to investors.  **Key Provision** The main provision extends the carryback period for unused LIHTC to five years. This is a technical tax provision that does not change which housing projects qualify for the credit or the credit amount itself—only the timing and flexibility of when taxpayers can apply unused credits to their tax liabilities."
