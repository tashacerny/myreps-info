summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would require the Secretary of the Army to include a specific plan in final recommendations for water resources development projects undertaken by the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers. Whenever the Corps proposes a water resources project, it would need to demonstrate how the project will maintain equivalent levels of public access to existing recreational amenities in the affected area. This requirement applies to the recommendation stage of the project approval process.  The bill affects the Corps of Engineers' project planning procedures and potentially impacts communities where water resources projects are proposed or developed. It may also affect project applicants and stakeholders interested in recreational access to public lands and waterways. By requiring explicit plans to preserve recreational access, the bill seeks to ensure that water resources development does not reduce the public's ability to use existing recreational facilities and areas.  Key provisions include the mandate to include an access maintenance plan as part of final project recommendations and the requirement that such plans preserve \"equivalent levels\" of existing recreational access. The bill's language \"and for other purposes\" suggests it may contain additional provisions beyond the main recreational access requirement, though these cannot be detailed without access to the full text."
