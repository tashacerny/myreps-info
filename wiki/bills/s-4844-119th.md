Currently, most turnout gear uses PFAS-based coatings to provide water and oil resistance. The bill intends to drive innovation toward alternative materials and technologies that can maintain or improve the protective performance of this gear while eliminating PFAS content.  **Who It Affects** The bill directly affects firefighters and fire departments, which would benefit from safer protective equipment. It also impacts manufacturers of firefighting gear, research institutions developing new materials, and potentially workers or communities exposed to PFAS during gear production. The bill may also have broader implications for public health, as PFAS chemicals have raised environmental and health concerns due to their persistence in the environment and human body.  **Expected Provisions** While the full text is unavailable, bills with this focus typically include mechanisms such as grants or funding for research and development, accelerated testing and certification pathways for new gear materials, collaboration between government agencies and private manufacturers, or incentives for adoption of PFAS-free alternatives.
