summary: "# Bill Summary  This concurrent resolution proposes that Congress hold a joint session in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, on July 2, 2026, to commemorate the 250th anniversary of the Declaration of Independence. A joint session brings together members of both the House of Representatives and the Senate for a formal meeting, typically to hear an address or mark a significant occasion. The date chosen—July 2—marks when the Declaration was adopted by Congress, two days before the document was publicly issued on July 4.  The resolution affects Congress as an institution by establishing the time and place for this commemorative gathering. It also involves the people and government of Philadelphia, as the city would host the event at a location historically significant to American independence. The measure itself is procedural in nature and does not authorize spending or create new policies; rather, it arranges an official ceremony to recognize an important milestone in U.S. history."
