summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would require the Secretary of Homeland Security to establish and maintain a public database containing information about individuals classified as \"criminal aliens\" who are released from custody. The database would be accessible to the public, making this information widely available rather than restricted to government agencies. The bill aims to increase transparency regarding the release of foreign nationals with criminal records from federal custody.  The primary groups affected would include: (1) foreign nationals with criminal convictions who are in DHS custody, (2) state and local law enforcement agencies that may use the database for public safety purposes, and (3) the general public, who would gain access to this information. DHS would bear the administrative responsibility for creating, maintaining, and updating the database.  Key provisions would likely include requirements that the database identify released individuals, document their criminal histories, and specify the reasons for and dates of their release. The bill addresses a policy concern about tracking foreign nationals with criminal records in the immigration system, though the specific data elements to be included and any privacy or security limitations are not detailed in the title alone."
