summary: "# Summary: Breast Cancer Research Stamp Reauthorization Act of 2026  This bill reauthorizes the Breast Cancer Research Stamp program, a federal initiative that allows the U.S. Postal Service to issue and sell special postage stamps dedicated to breast cancer research. The revenue generated from the sale of these stamps—beyond the standard postage value—is directed to the National Institutes of Health to fund breast cancer research grants. The bill extends the program's authorization, which would otherwise expire, allowing the Postal Service to continue issuing these stamps for an additional period.  The bill directly affects researchers and medical institutions receiving NIH breast cancer research funding, as well as the general public who purchase stamps. It has no direct fiscal impact on the federal budget, since stamp revenue comes from voluntary consumer purchases rather than appropriated funds. The key provision is the reauthorization itself, which maintains the mechanism for dedicating stamp surcharge revenue to breast cancer research without requiring new appropriations."
