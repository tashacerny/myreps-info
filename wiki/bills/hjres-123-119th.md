summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill is a disapproval resolution under the Congressional Review Act (CRA), a legislative procedure that allows Congress to overturn federal agency regulations. Specifically, it seeks to disapprove a rule issued by the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) related to the Affordable Care Act's health insurance marketplace. Based on the rule's title, the regulation addresses integrity and affordability measures within the ACA marketplace system where individuals can purchase health insurance.  The bill would affect CMS's authority to implement the rule and would impact the operation of the health insurance marketplace. This could include effects on insurers offering plans through the marketplace, individuals who purchase insurance through these exchanges, and the regulatory framework governing marketplace operations. If passed and signed into law, the disapproval would invalidate the rule and generally prevent CMS from issuing substantially similar regulations without new legislative authorization.  Without access to the full text of the underlying CMS rule, specific provisions cannot be detailed; however, CRA disapprovals are straightforward procedural votes on whether to reject the agency action entirely rather than modify it. The resolution would require passage in both chambers of Congress and the President's signature to take effect."
