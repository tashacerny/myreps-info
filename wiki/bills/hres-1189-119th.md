summary: "# Summary of H.R. 4690 and Related Bills Consideration  This resolution is a procedural measure that establishes rules for the House of Representatives to consider four separate legislative items. The resolution itself does not enact policy but rather sets the terms under which the House will debate and vote on these bills.  The bills covered include: H.R. 4690, which would repeal certain federal building energy efficiency performance standards under the Energy Conservation and Production Act; H.R. 1897, which would amend the Endangered Species Act to modify how species are prioritized for conservation, adjust permitting processes, and expand incentives for private landowners to support wildlife conservation; and H.R. 5587, which would modify the Geothermal Steam Act of 1970 by waiving certain federal drilling permit requirements and exempting some activities from environmental review under the National Environmental Policy Act. The resolution also provides for consideration of H. Res. 1182, a non-binding resolution expressing support for rural communities as environmental stewards and energy producers.  These bills affect federal agencies overseeing energy and environmental policy, private landowners involved in conservation and energy development, and industries related to building efficiency, wildlife management, and geothermal energy. The procedural resolution determines how debate time is allocated and what amendments, if any, members may propose during consideration of each bill."
