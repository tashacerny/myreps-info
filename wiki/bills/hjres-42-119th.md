summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to use the Congressional Review Act (a legal mechanism that allows Congress to disapprove of federal regulations) to reject a Department of Energy rule regarding appliance standards. The rule being challenged covers certification requirements, labeling requirements, and enforcement provisions for consumer appliances and commercial equipment. If passed, this bill would nullify the DOE regulation and prevent the department from issuing substantially similar rules without further congressional action.  The rule affects manufacturers and sellers of covered appliances and commercial equipment, as well as consumers who rely on appliance labeling and certification standards. The specific appliances and equipment covered are not identified in the title alone. Key aspects of the DOE rule that would be disapproved include how manufacturers must certify their products meet energy conservation standards, how products must be labeled to inform consumers about efficiency, and how the DOE enforces compliance with those standards.  If enacted, this bill would overturn the DOE's regulatory action and establish that Congress opposes this particular approach to appliance certification and labeling. Manufacturers would no longer be required to follow the specific certification and labeling procedures established by the rule, though baseline federal appliance efficiency standards would remain in effect under existing law."
