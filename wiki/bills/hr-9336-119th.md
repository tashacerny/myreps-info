summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would amend Medicaid law to require states to consider performance metrics when assigning beneficiaries to managed care organizations (MCOs). Currently, states have flexibility in how they assign Medicaid enrollees to MCOs. Under this proposal, states would be required to incorporate performance data into their assignment processes, meaning assignment decisions would reflect how well MCOs meet specified standards or benchmarks rather than relying solely on other factors like geography or enrollment capacity.  The bill would affect Medicaid beneficiaries (low-income individuals enrolled in state Medicaid programs) and managed care entities that contract with states to provide Medicaid services. States administering Medicaid would need to establish or modify their assignment systems to track and apply performance information when placing new enrollees or reassigning existing ones.  Key provisions would likely include requirements for states to define what \"performance\" means (such as quality metrics, customer satisfaction, or access to care measures), establish data collection and evaluation systems, and potentially allow beneficiary movement toward higher-performing plans. The intent appears to be linking enrollment assignments to measurable MCO performance, potentially incentivizing better service delivery and giving states a tool to reward higher-performing plans with more members."
