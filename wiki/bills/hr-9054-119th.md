summary: "# Summary: Medicare International Coverage Demonstration Program  This bill proposes to amend Medicare (Title XVIII of the Social Security Act) by establishing a demonstration program to test coverage for medical services received in other countries. The program would allow Medicare to study whether and how to extend healthcare coverage to beneficiaries who receive treatment internationally, rather than limiting coverage primarily to U.S. providers and facilities.  The demonstration would likely affect Medicare beneficiaries who travel or relocate abroad, as well as U.S. healthcare providers and international medical facilities participating in the program. The bill would enable the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) to collect data on the feasibility, costs, and outcomes of covering international medical services.  Key provisions would typically include establishing program parameters such as participating countries or facilities, eligibility criteria for beneficiaries, covered services, payment rates, and a timeline for the demonstration. The program would generate evidence to inform future policy decisions about expanding Medicare coverage internationally, which currently has very limited provisions for overseas care."
