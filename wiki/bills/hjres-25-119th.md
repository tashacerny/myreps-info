summary: "# Summary of Congressional Disapproval Resolution  This resolution uses the Congressional Review Act (CRA) to disapprove an Internal Revenue Service rule regarding broker reporting requirements for digital asset transactions. The rule, titled \"Gross Proceeds Reporting by Brokers That Regularly Provide Services Effectuating Digital Asset Sales,\" establishes new reporting obligations for brokers who facilitate the sale of digital assets (such as cryptocurrency). If passed, this resolution would nullify the IRS rule and prevent the agency from issuing substantially similar rules without new legislative authorization.  The rule affects brokers and exchanges that provide digital asset trading services, as well as the IRS's ability to collect tax information. Under the disapproved rule, these brokers would be required to report gross proceeds from digital asset sales to both the IRS and customers on Form 1099-DA. The stated intent of the original rule is to improve tax compliance and information reporting in the digital asset market.  Key provisions of the resolution would rescind the reporting requirement and prohibit the IRS from implementing the rule or any substantially identical regulation without explicit Congressional approval. If enacted, brokers would not be obligated to provide the additional reporting under this rule, though they may remain subject to other applicable tax reporting laws."
