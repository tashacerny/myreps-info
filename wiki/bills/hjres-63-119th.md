summary: "# Summary of Bill to Redesignate Robert E. Lee Memorial  **What the Bill Proposes**  This bill would change the official name of the Robert E. Lee Memorial, located at Arlington House in Arlington, Virginia, to \"Arlington House National Historic Site.\" The memorial is currently situated within Arlington National Cemetery and consists of the antebellum mansion and surrounding grounds that once belonged to Robert E. Lee.  **Who It Affects**  The change would affect how the National Park Service officially designates and refers to the site in its administrative materials, signage, and public communications. It may impact visitors, educators, historians, and others who interact with the site or its historical documentation.  **Key Provisions**  The bill's primary action is a redesignation—renaming the memorial without necessarily altering its physical structure, collection, or core operations. The specific rationale for the name change, the effective date, and any related modifications to how the site is interpreted or managed would depend on the full legislative text. Redesignations of federal historic sites are typically administrative changes intended to reflect evolving historical understanding or institutional priorities."
