summary: "# Summary of National Service and Conservation Corps Day Resolution  This resolution proposes to designate the second Friday in June as \"National Service and Conservation Corps Day\" in the United States. The designation would create an annual observance to recognize and honor service and conservation corps organizations and their members.  National Service and Conservation Corps are civilian programs that employ individuals—often young adults—in environmental conservation, infrastructure improvement, and community service projects. These corps engage participants in work such as trail maintenance, forest restoration, park development, and disaster relief. The designation would provide an official day to acknowledge these organizations' contributions to environmental stewardship and public service.  The resolution is symbolic in nature and does not create new programs, funding, or legal requirements. It simply establishes an annual date for national recognition. The observance would affect government agencies, corps organizations, and members who might choose to mark the occasion through events or acknowledgment, though participation would be voluntary."
