summary: "# Bill Summary: Older Americans Month Designation  This resolution designates May 2026 as \"Older Americans Month.\" The measure is a symbolic action by Congress recognizing and honoring older Americans during that month. Such designations do not create new programs, funding, or legal requirements; they serve as official acknowledgments that encourage public awareness and recognition of a particular group or cause.  The designation would affect older Americans and organizations that serve them, including senior centers, advocacy groups, and community programs, which may use the official recognition to organize events and raise awareness about issues affecting aging populations. Federal agencies may also participate in related observances or initiatives during the designated month.  Key to this resolution is that it is largely ceremonial in nature. It does not establish new policies, allocate government resources, or impose requirements on any individuals or entities. The resolution simply expresses Congress's formal recognition of the contributions and importance of older Americans in society."
