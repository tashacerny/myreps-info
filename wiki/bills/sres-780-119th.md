summary: "# Summary of World Sickle Cell Awareness Day Resolution  This resolution expresses support for designating June 19, 2026, as \"World Sickle Cell Awareness Day\" in the United States. The designation is intended to increase public awareness about sickle cell disease among both the U.S. population and the global community. The resolution does not create new programs or funding; rather, it serves as a statement of congressional support for recognizing this health awareness day.  The resolution highlights several areas of focus related to sickle cell disease, including the need for empirical research, early detection screening programs, development of novel and effective treatments, and work toward finding a cure. It also emphasizes the importance of preventative care programs that address complications from sickle cell anemia and related conditions. Sickle cell disease is a genetic blood disorder that predominantly affects Black Americans and people of African descent, though it impacts individuals of various ethnic backgrounds.  By supporting this awareness day, the resolution aims to draw attention to the medical, research, and public health dimensions of sickle cell disease, encouraging increased focus on these areas among healthcare providers, researchers, policymakers, and the general public."
