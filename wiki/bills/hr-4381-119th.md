summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would direct the Secretary of Defense to create a pilot program that would allow pregnant individuals to enroll in TRICARE Select—the Department of Defense's managed care health insurance plan for military families and retirees—outside of the standard annual enrollment period. Currently, TRICARE Select enrollment is generally limited to specific times of year and certain qualifying life events. Under this proposal, pregnancy would be added as a \"qualifying event\" that triggers eligibility for enrollment outside normal windows.  The pilot program would affect pregnant individuals who are eligible for TRICARE benefits, including active duty service members' dependents, military retirees, and their families. By treating pregnancy as a qualifying event, the bill aims to ensure that pregnant individuals can enroll in coverage when they become pregnant, rather than waiting for the next designated enrollment period. This would allow them to access maternity and prenatal care through TRICARE Select.  The bill requires the Secretary of Defense to establish, implement, and evaluate this pilot program, though the specific duration, scope, and evaluation metrics would be determined by the Department of Defense in implementing the legislation."
