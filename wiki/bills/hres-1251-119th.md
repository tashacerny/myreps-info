Instead, it addresses elected officials at federal, state, and local levels, as well as civil society organizations, civic groups, and educational institutions, encouraging them to undertake these efforts voluntarily. The measure seeks to influence public discourse and policy priorities through official endorsement.  Key provisions likely include language urging opposition to antisemitic rhetoric and discrimination, encouraging educational initiatives about Jewish-American history and achievements, and promoting interfaith and intercommunal dialogue.
