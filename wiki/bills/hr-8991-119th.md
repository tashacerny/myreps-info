summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend Section 2101 of Title 28 of the United States Code, which governs stays of judgment pending appeal. A stay is a court order that pauses enforcement of a judgment while an appeal is being considered. The bill would establish specific factors that courts must consider when deciding whether to grant a stay while a case is on appeal.  The amendment would affect parties involved in federal appeals—including plaintiffs, defendants, and other litigants—by clarifying the legal standards judges should apply when evaluating stay requests. Currently, courts apply general principles developed through case law, but this bill would codify explicit factors into statute. This would provide more consistency across federal courts and give litigants clearer guidance about what judges will weigh in these decisions.  Without access to the full text, the specific factors cannot be detailed, but such provisions typically address considerations like the likelihood of success on appeal, potential harm to the parties, the public interest, and whether the applicant would be irreparably injured without a stay. The amendment would apply to all federal litigation where parties seek to pause enforcement of lower court judgments during the appellate process."
