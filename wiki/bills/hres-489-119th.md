summary: "# Summary of Procedural Consideration Bill  This is a procedural measure that sets the terms for House floor consideration of four separate bills. It does not enact legislation itself but rather establishes the rules under which these bills may be debated and voted upon. The four bills addressed are: H.R. 884 (voting eligibility in Washington, D.C.), H.R. 2056 (D.C. immigration law compliance), H.R. 2096 (D.C. police discipline and collective bargaining), and S. 331 (fentanyl scheduling under federal drug law).  **H.R. 884** would prohibit non-citizens from voting in D.C. elections and repeal the 2022 Local Resident Voting Rights Amendment Act, which currently allows non-citizens to participate in some local elections. **H.R. 2056** would require D.C. to comply with federal immigration laws. **H.R. 2096** would restore collective bargaining rights for matters involving discipline of D.C. Metropolitan Police Department officers and restore the statute of limitations for bringing disciplinary cases against police members and civilian employees. **S. 331** would modify how fentanyl-related substances are scheduled under the Controlled Substances Act.  This procedural resolution affects D.C. governance, federal drug policy, and local labor practices for police personnel. It allows the House to consider these bills, but passage of this resolution does not guarantee passage of the underlying bills themselves."
