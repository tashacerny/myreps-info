summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill invokes the War Powers Resolution to direct the President to remove U.S. Armed Forces from any ongoing or imminent hostilities involving Iran. The War Powers Resolution, enacted in 1973, allows Congress to require the President to cease military operations in specified circumstances. This bill would use that authority to mandate withdrawal from such hostilities without prior Congressional authorization for those specific military actions.  **Who It Affects:** The bill directly affects the President's command of U.S. military personnel currently engaged in or preparing for military operations related to Iran. It also has implications for U.S. foreign policy toward Iran and could affect diplomatic and military personnel stationed in the region.  **Key Provisions:** The bill relies on Section 5(c) of the War Powers Resolution, which permits Congress to direct the President to remove armed forces from military hostilities by concurrent resolution. If enacted, the President would be required to cease U.S. military involvement in hostilities with Iran. The specific timeline and implementation details would depend on the bill's full text. This represents an assertion of Congressional authority over military operations, as the War Powers Resolution requires Congressional action to extend military commitments beyond specified periods without a formal declaration of war."
