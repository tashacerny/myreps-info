summary: "# Summary of the Artemis II Congressional Gold Medal Act  This bill would authorize the U.S. Congress to award the Congressional Gold Medal to the crew members of Artemis II, a NASA mission planned as part of the Artemis program to return humans to the Moon. The Congressional Gold Medal is one of the highest civilian honors bestowed by Congress and is typically awarded to individuals or groups for distinguished achievements and contributions to American society.  The bill would recognize the astronauts and crew of Artemis II for their participation in this spaceflight mission. The Artemis II mission is designed as a crewed lunar flyby mission that will precede the planned lunar landing mission (Artemis III). By authorizing this medal, Congress would formally honor the crew's service and contribution to space exploration and scientific advancement.  The medal would be awarded to the flight crew members designated for the Artemis II mission. The specific logistics—such as the timing of the award ceremony and the number of medals to be struck—would be determined through standard Congressional Gold Medal procedures."
