summary: "# Legislative Summary  This bill proposes comprehensive amendments to U.S. immigration law across multiple categories. The legislation would establish a \"national-interest standard\" as a framework for immigration decisions and eliminate several existing immigration pathways, including certain family-sponsored categories and the diversity visa program. It would also revise standards for determining good moral character in immigration proceedings and modify rules governing public charge determinations and sponsor financial support requirements.  The bill would affect immigrants, visa applicants, employers, and U.S. citizens with family members abroad. Key provisions include changing naturalization requirements for citizenship; reforming employment-based immigration and H-1B visa programs; eliminating Optional Practical Training (work authorization for foreign students) unless specifically authorized by statute; revising asylum procedures and eligibility; requiring employment eligibility verification; and establishing additional penalties for unlawful presence and visa overstays. The legislation would also modify the executive branch's authority to grant parole status (temporary entry for humanitarian or public interest purposes).  The bill represents a significant restructuring of the current immigration system, as it would reduce available visa categories, create new restrictions and verification requirements, and alter how various immigration statuses are granted and maintained."
