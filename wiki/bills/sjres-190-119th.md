summary: "# Summary of Congressional Disapproval Resolution  This joint resolution seeks to overturn a regulatory rule issued by the Executive Office for Immigration Review (EOIR) that governs appellate procedures for the Board of Immigration Appeals (BIA). Under the Congressional Review Act (Chapter 8 of Title 5, U.S. Code), Congress may disapprove certain federal agency rules within a specified timeframe. If passed by both chambers and signed by the President, this resolution would nullify the EOIR's appellate procedures rule and prevent substantially similar rules from being adopted without further congressional action.  The rule being challenged concerns how the BIA handles appeals in immigration cases. The BIA is the highest administrative body for reviewing immigration decisions made by lower immigration courts. Any changes to appellate procedures could affect how quickly cases are processed, what evidence may be presented, how decisions are written, or other aspects of the appeal process. This would impact immigration judges, attorneys, and individuals appealing immigration decisions.  Without access to the full text, the specific procedural changes being disapproved cannot be detailed. The resolution would be of interest to members concerned about immigration case processing, administrative efficiency, or the scope of executive rulemaking authority. Its passage would depend on whether a majority in both chambers and the President view the EOIR rule as problematic."
