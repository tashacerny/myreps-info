summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would amend the Federal Election Campaign Act of 1971 to create additional disclosure requirements for political committees. Specifically, it would require committees to file separate reports for contributions of $1,000 or more that are received within 20 days before an election, but only when the committee makes contributions, expenditures, or electioneering communications related to candidates or political parties in that election.  The bill primarily affects political committees, including candidate committees, party committees, and independent expenditure committees. It also impacts donors who contribute $1,000 or more to these committees during the specified 20-day window, as their contributions would be subject to separate reporting and potentially greater visibility. Voters and the general public would gain access to more granular information about late-arriving campaign funding.  The key provision establishes a separate reporting mechanism for large late contributions, which would provide real-time or near-real-time disclosure of significant money flowing to committees immediately before elections. This is intended to increase transparency around funding sources in the final weeks of campaign activity. The phrase \"and for other purposes\" suggests the bill may contain additional minor provisions beyond the main contribution reporting requirement."
