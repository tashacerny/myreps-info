summary: "# Bill Summary: E-Verify Mandatory Employment Eligibility System  **What the Bill Proposes**  This bill would make the E-Verify employment eligibility verification system permanent and require all U.S. employers to use it when hiring new employees. E-Verify is a federal online system that allows employers to check whether job applicants are legally authorized to work in the United States by cross-referencing Social Security Administration and Department of Homeland Security databases. Currently, E-Verify participation is voluntary for most employers, though it is required for federal contractors and certain other employers in specific states.  **Who It Affects**  The bill would directly affect all employers across the United States, who would be required to verify the work eligibility of new hires through the E-Verify system. It would also affect job applicants and new employees, as their information would be checked against federal databases during the hiring process. Workers and employers in states with existing mandatory E-Verify requirements would see the most significant change in federalizing the requirement.  **Key Provisions**  The bill's primary provision is the mandate for universal employer participation in E-Verify. By making E-Verify permanent, the bill would ensure the system continues to operate indefinitely (rather than requiring periodic reauthorization). The legislation may also include provisions specifying implementation timelines, penalties for non-compliance, and procedures for handling verification disputes."
