summary: "# Summary of Constitutional Amendment Proposal on Voting Eligibility  This bill proposes a constitutional amendment to establish that only U.S. citizens are eligible to vote in federal elections. The amendment would add an explicit citizenship requirement to the Constitution regarding voting rights in elections for federal office (President, Vice President, Senate, and House of Representatives).  The amendment would affect voting eligibility standards across all states. It would apply to anyone seeking to vote in federal elections, regardless of state residency or other qualifications. The proposal addresses the constitutional framework for federal voting rights, which currently addresses qualifications through the 14th Amendment (citizenship for federal representation) and prior amendments but does not explicitly state a citizenship requirement for voting itself in federal elections.  Key provisions would include codifying citizenship as a mandatory qualification for voting in federal elections. This is separate from state authority over local and state elections. The amendment process requires approval by two-thirds of both the House and Senate, followed by ratification by three-fourths of state legislatures to become part of the Constitution."
