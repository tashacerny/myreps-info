summary: "# Summary of House Procedural Resolution  This resolution is a procedural measure that sets the terms for House floor consideration of four separate bills. It establishes the rules—such as debate time, amendment procedures, and voting protocols—that will govern how the House will consider each of these bills. This type of resolution must pass before the underlying bills can be debated and voted on by the full House.  The four bills covered by this resolution address different policy areas: H.R. 2483 concerns reauthorization of federal programs related to opioid use disorder prevention, treatment, and recovery services. H.R. 2931, H.R. 2966, and H.R. 2987 all relate to Small Business Administration (SBA) operations and lending programs, with provisions addressing office locations in certain jurisdictions, citizenship documentation requirements for loan applicants, and limits on the number of SBA lending companies.  This resolution affects members of Congress by determining how they may participate in debate and amendment of these bills, and it indirectly affects individuals and organizations that would be impacted by the substantive provisions of the four underlying bills—including opioid treatment providers, small business owners and applicants, and the SBA itself."
