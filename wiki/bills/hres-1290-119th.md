summary: "# Bill Summary  This resolution recognizes the behavioral health needs—including mental health and substance use services—of individuals and families living with rare diseases and those who care for them. Rare diseases affect millions of Americans, and the bill acknowledges that people with these conditions often experience depression, anxiety, and other mental health challenges that may be overlooked or underaddressed in medical treatment. The resolution also recognizes similar behavioral health impacts on family members and caregivers who support patients with rare diseases.  The bill calls attention to gaps in behavioral health services and support systems for this population. It may encourage greater awareness among healthcare providers, policymakers, and the public about the mental health dimensions of rare disease management and the need for integrated care approaches. The resolution affects individuals with rare diseases, their families, caregivers, healthcare providers, and mental health professionals involved in treating this population.  While this is a recognition resolution (rather than a bill creating new programs or funding), it aims to establish congressional acknowledgment of an underrecognized health need and may serve as a foundation for future legislative action or policy initiatives addressing behavioral health in the rare disease community."
