summary: "# Federal Death Penalty Prohibition Act — Summary  **What the Bill Proposes**  This bill would eliminate the federal death penalty by prohibiting its use as a punishment for federal crimes. It would replace capital punishment with life imprisonment without the possibility of parole as the maximum penalty available in the federal criminal justice system.  **Who It Affects**  The bill would affect individuals convicted of federal crimes currently eligible for death sentences, including certain cases involving murder, treason, espionage, and crimes related to drug trafficking or terrorism. It would also impact federal prosecutors, federal courts handling capital cases, and the federal Bureau of Prisons, which currently maintains death row facilities. Additionally, it may affect families of victims in cases where capital punishment was previously an available sentencing option.  **Key Provisions**  The legislation would remove death penalty statutes from federal law and establish life sentences without parole as the alternative maximum sentence. It would likely apply prospectively to future convictions, though the bill text would clarify whether it affects individuals currently on federal death row and any ongoing capital cases."
