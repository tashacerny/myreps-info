summary: "# Summary: Abolish Super PACs Act  **What the Bill Proposes**  This bill would eliminate Super PACs (Political Action Committees), which are currently independent political committees that can raise and spend unlimited amounts of money on elections, provided they do not coordinate directly with candidates or parties. The bill would prohibit the formation and operation of these entities and likely would impose restrictions on unlimited independent political spending in federal elections.  **Who It Affects**  The legislation would impact political organizations, donors, candidates, and campaigns. Super PACs and their donors would no longer be able to operate under current rules. Candidates and political parties would be affected by changes to the landscape of campaign finance. Voters could experience different types and volumes of political advertising and messaging during elections.  **Key Considerations**  Any such legislation would likely involve significant constitutional questions, as Super PACs were authorized by the 2010 *Citizens United v. FEC* Supreme Court decision, which ruled that independent political spending is protected speech. Abolishing Super PACs would require either a change in that court precedent, a constitutional amendment, or legislation designed to comply with existing constitutional standards while limiting their operations.  *Note: Without the full bill text, this summary addresses the general implications of the title.
