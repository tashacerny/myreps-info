summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill uses the Congressional Review Act (CRA) to disapprove a Department of Education regulation titled \"Reimagining and Improving Student Education-Federal Student Loan Program Final Regulations.\" Under the CRA process, Congress can reject federal agency rules within 60 legislative days of their submission. If passed, this disapproval would nullify the regulation and prevent the Department of Education from issuing substantially similar rules without new congressional authorization.  The regulation being challenged addresses federal student loan policy. While the full text is unavailable, regulations under this title typically cover matters such as loan forgiveness programs, repayment plans, borrower protections, and administration of federal student lending. The disapproval would affect millions of federal student loan borrowers and the Department of Education's implementation of student aid programs.  The key provision is the formal disapproval mechanism itself: if both chambers of Congress pass this measure and it is signed into law (or Congress overrides a presidential veto), the challenged regulation ceases to have effect. This represents a direct legislative check on executive branch rulemaking in the student loan policy area."
