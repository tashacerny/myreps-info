summary: "# Summary of the Procedural Rule  This bill is a procedural measure that sets the terms for House consideration of three separate legislative items. It does not enact substantive policy itself but rather establishes how and when the House will debate and vote on these matters.  The bill addresses three distinct issues: (1) Senate amendments to H.R. 7148, the consolidated appropriations bill for fiscal year 2026, which funds federal government operations; (2) H.J. Res. 142, a joint resolution that would disapprove a Washington, D.C. tax law (the D.C. Income and Franchise Tax Conformity and Revision Temporary Amendment Act of 2025); and (3) H.R. 4090, legislation to codify executive orders related to domestic mining and hardrock mineral resource extraction.  This type of procedural rule is used to manage floor time and debate parameters for multiple bills. It affects members of Congress by determining which measures receive House floor consideration and under what conditions. The actual policy impacts would result from passage of the three underlying bills, not from this procedural measure itself."
