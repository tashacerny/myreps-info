Chafee Foster Care Program  **What the Bill Proposes**  This bill would amend Section 477 of the Social Security Act to update the stated goals and purposes of the John H. Chafee Foster Care Program for Successful Transition to Adulthood. Specifically, the amendment would revise the program's purpose statement to emphasize the importance of long-term relationships in helping youth transition successfully from foster care to adulthood. The changes appear to be based on recent research findings and input gathered directly from young people who have experienced the foster care system.  **Who It Affects**  The bill would affect youth currently in or aging out of the foster care system, as well as the state and local agencies that administer the Chafee Program. These include child welfare agencies, educational institutions, and organizations providing services to foster youth. The changes would guide how these entities conceptualize and prioritize their support services for transitioning youth.  **Key Provisions**  While specific language is not available, the bill's primary action is to update the program's stated purposes to reflect that long-term, stable relationships are critical to successful outcomes for youth leaving foster care. This represents a shift in how the program's mission is formally defined, likely to better align federal guidance with current research and the perspectives of young people with direct foster care experience."
