summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to repeal the Hughes Amendment, a provision within the Firearm Owners' Protection Act of 1986. The Hughes Amendment currently prohibits the civilian manufacture and importation of fully automatic firearms (machine guns) produced after May 1986, though it allows lawful possession of fully automatic weapons manufactured before that date. Repealing this provision would remove the federal ban on manufacturing new fully automatic firearms for civilian purchase, though other existing federal firearms regulations would remain in effect.  The bill would affect firearms manufacturers, firearms dealers, and individuals interested in purchasing fully automatic weapons. It would also impact federal law enforcement and the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives (ATF), which administers firearms regulations. Currently, civilians can only legally own pre-1986 fully automatic weapons, which are heavily regulated, registered with the federal government, and subject to significant taxes and background checks.  The key provision is the repeal of the Hughes Amendment itself, which would eliminate the manufacturing cutoff date and allow new fully automatic firearms to be produced and sold to civilians under existing regulatory frameworks. The bill does not appear to propose changes to other requirements such as background checks, registration, or federal taxation of automatic weapons, based on the title alone."
