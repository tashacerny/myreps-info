summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would authorize state governors to conduct health and safety inspections of immigration detention facilities operating within their states. Currently, oversight of these facilities falls primarily to federal agencies. The bill would create a new inspection authority for state executives, allowing them to directly assess conditions related to health, safety, and other welfare standards at these locations.  The bill also establishes a reporting requirement, obligating inspectors to document and communicate their findings to Congress. This reporting mechanism would create a formal channel for legislative review of detention facility conditions. The bill affects immigration detention facility operators, state governors and their agencies, federal immigration authorities, and Congress as recipients of inspection reports.  Key provisions would include defining the scope and standards for these inspections, establishing procedures governors must follow, specifying what information must be included in reports to Congress, and potentially addressing how state and federal oversight authorities interact when both are present at a facility."
