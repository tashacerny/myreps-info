summary: "# Legislative Summary  This rule establishes floor procedures for consideration of four related measures focused on federal fraud prevention and government accountability. The rule allows the House to debate and vote on: H.R. 8312, which would create fraud prevention functions within the Treasury Department and establish a permanent government-wide Inspector General for Fraud, Accountability, and Recovery; H.R. 8464, which would authorize the Treasury to pause and segment federal payments; H. Res. 1335, a resolution condemning fraud against the federal government and expressing support for fraud prevention reforms; and S. 2, a reconciliation bill related to the budget resolution S. Con. Res. 33.  The bills primarily affect the Treasury Department, federal agencies that distribute payments, and federal program beneficiaries. H.R. 8312 and H.R. 8464 establish new tools and oversight structures to identify and prevent fraudulent or improper payments across federal programs. H. Res. 1335 expresses the House's position that federal eligibility should be verified before payments are made and that such reforms would strengthen government finances. These measures collectively aim to reduce fraud and improper payments in federal benefit and grant programs."
