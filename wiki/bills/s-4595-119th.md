summary: "# Summary of the Mullica River Watershed Wild and Scenic River Study Act of 2026  This bill directs the federal government to conduct a study of the Mullica River watershed (located in New Jersey) to determine whether it meets the criteria for designation as a Wild and Scenic River under the National Wild and Scenic Rivers Act. The study would evaluate the river's scenic, recreational, geological, fish and wildlife, historical, and cultural values. The bill affects the river itself, surrounding communities in the Mullica River watershed area, and federal land management agencies responsible for conducting the assessment.  Key provisions likely include authorizing and funding a comprehensive study period (typically 2–3 years) to gather data and assess eligibility, requiring consultation with state and local governments and stakeholders, and producing a formal report with recommendations on whether to recommend the river for Wild and Scenic designation to Congress. If designated, a Wild and Scenic River receives federal protections limiting certain development activities and establishing management guidelines, though the specific protections depend on the river's classification (Wild, Scenic, or Recreational). The study itself does not impose any restrictions; designation would only occur if Congress acts on a favorable recommendation."
