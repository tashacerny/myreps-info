summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would use the Congressional Review Act (CRA) to disapprove a regulatory action by the Bureau of Consumer Financial Protection (CFPB). Specifically, it targets the CFPB's decision to withdraw a previously issued rule related to \"Fair Credit Reporting; File Disclosure.\" Under the CRA process, Congress can vote to reject a federal agency rule within a set timeframe, effectively preventing it from taking effect.  The rule in question deals with how consumer credit reporting agencies must disclose file information to consumers. By withdrawing this rule, the CFPB is reversing or eliminating a previous regulatory requirement. This bill would prevent that withdrawal from occurring, meaning the original disclosure requirements would remain in place.  The bill would primarily affect the CFPB, credit reporting agencies, and consumers who rely on file disclosure protections in the credit reporting process. If passed, it would require the CFPB to maintain the fair credit reporting disclosure rule rather than allowing it to be withdrawn, though the specific details of what the rule requires would need to be reviewed in full legislative text."
