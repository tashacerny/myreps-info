summary: "# Cell-Site Simulator Warrant Act of 2025 — Summary  Based on the bill title, this legislation would establish warrant requirements for the use of cell-site simulators (also called \"IMSI catchers\" or \"Stingrays\"). These are surveillance devices that mimic cell towers to capture mobile phone signals and location data. The bill would likely require law enforcement agencies to obtain judicial warrants before deploying such technology, establishing legal standards and procedures for their use.  The bill would primarily affect law enforcement agencies at federal, state, and local levels that currently use or may use cell-site simulators in investigations. It could also impact individuals whose cell phones might be exposed to these devices, by establishing privacy protections. Telecommunications companies may be affected depending on provisions regarding notification or cooperation requirements.  Key provisions would likely include: requiring a warrant based on probable cause before deployment; specifying which agencies can use the technology; potentially mandating notification to affected parties or courts; and possibly establishing oversight, reporting, or record-keeping requirements. The bill may also define the scope of data collection permitted and establish penalties for unauthorized use."
