summary: "# Bill Summary  This procedural bill from the House of Representatives sets the terms for floor debate and voting on two separate Congressional Review Act (CRA) resolutions that seek to disapprove and overturn recently issued federal regulations. The first resolution (S.J. Res. 13) targets a rule by the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency regarding the review process for bank merger applications. The second resolution (S.J. Res. 31) targets an Environmental Protection Agency rule that reclassifies certain major pollution sources as area sources under the Clean Air Act. The bill establishes the procedural framework—including debate time, amendment rules, and voting procedures—that will govern consideration of these two disapproval resolutions on the House floor.  The bill affects federal banking regulators, the EPA, financial institutions subject to merger review, and facilities regulated under the Clean Air Act's major source provisions. It also includes a waiver of House Rule XIII clause 6(a), which is a technical provision allowing the consideration of resolutions reported from the Rules Committee under modified procedures.  In practical terms, this is a procedural measure that allows the House to vote on whether to reject these two specific federal regulations. The underlying resolutions themselves would need separate approval to actually overturn the rules, but this bill clears the way for those votes to occur."
