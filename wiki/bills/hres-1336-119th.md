summary: "# Summary  This bill waives a House procedural requirement under Rule XIII, clause 6(a), which normally requires that resolutions reported from the Committee on Rules meet certain conditions before they can be considered by the full House. By waiving this requirement for \"certain resolutions,\" the bill allows the Committee on Rules to bring specified resolutions to the House floor without satisfying the standard procedural prerequisites, expediting their consideration.  # Who It Affects  This measure affects the House of Representatives' legislative process. The Committee on Rules, which manages how bills and resolutions are debated and voted on in the House, would be able to proceed with certain resolutions under an exception to normal rules. Individual members and the broader legislative agenda could be affected depending on which specific resolutions receive this waiver.  # Key Provision  The central provision is the waiver itself—a suspension of Rule XIII, clause 6(a), for an unspecified set of resolutions. Without access to the full text, the specific resolutions covered and the exact scope of the waiver cannot be detailed, but the practical effect is to bypass one layer of procedural review and allow faster floor consideration of those resolutions."
