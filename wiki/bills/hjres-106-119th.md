summary: "# Summary of Congressional Disapproval Bill  This bill proposes to disapprove a rule issued by the Bureau of Land Management (BLM) regarding the \"Central Yukon Record of Decision and Approved Resource Management Plan.\" Under the Congressional Review Act (chapter 8 of title 5, U.S. Code), Congress can pass a joint resolution to disapprove federal agency rules within 60 legislative days of their submission. If passed, this resolution would overturn the BLM's Central Yukon resource management plan and prevent the agency from issuing substantially the same rule without new legislative authority.  The rule affects land management, resource use, and regulatory decisions affecting central Yukon, Alaska, where the BLM oversees federal lands. The disapproval would impact any activities or policies governed by this resource management plan, potentially affecting industries such as mining, oil and gas, timber, recreation, and conservation efforts in the region.  Without access to the full bill text, the specific policy changes contained in the Central Yukon plan cannot be detailed. However, the core mechanism is straightforward: passage would invalidate the BLM's rule and require the agency to follow different procedures if it wishes to establish similar land management policies in the future."
