The bill affects current and former members of Congress, congressional staff, and the public's access to information about how harassment allegations have been resolved. It requires the Ethics Committee to maintain these records and release them in a public format, changing what has traditionally been confidential or restricted settlement information.  Key provisions include requiring preservation of settlement records and mandating their public release. The bill appears designed to increase transparency and accountability regarding how Congress has addressed sexual harassment allegations internally, allowing constituents and the public to access information about these cases."
