summary: "# Bill Summary: 761st Tank Battalion Congressional Gold Medal Act  This bill proposes to award a Congressional Gold Medal to the 761st Tank Battalion, a military unit with historical significance. The Congressional Gold Medal is the highest civilian honor Congress can bestow and is typically awarded to individuals or groups for outstanding achievements or contributions to American history and society.  The 761st Tank Battalion would be the recipient of this medal. Based on historical records, this battalion is notable as one of the first African American tank units to see combat in World War II. The bill affects the legislative process by requiring Congressional action to authorize and provide funding for the medal's creation and presentation. There are no direct regulatory or operational impacts on the general public, though the bill may involve appropriations for the medal's design and minting.  Key provisions would include authorizing the medal's creation, directing its design, and specifying arrangements for its presentation. The bill may also include language allowing duplicates of the medal to be made available for museums or educational institutions. As with other Congressional Gold Medal acts, passage requires approval from both chambers of Congress and the President's signature."
