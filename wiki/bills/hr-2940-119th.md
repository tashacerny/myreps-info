summary: "# Summary of the Advancing Water Reuse Act  **Purpose and Scope**  The Advancing Water Reuse Act is legislation designed to promote the increased use of recycled and reclaimed water in the United States. The bill aims to establish or expand policies and programs that treat and reuse water from municipal wastewater systems, stormwater, and other sources for non-potable purposes (such as irrigation, industrial cooling, and toilet flushing) and potentially for potable (drinking water) purposes where appropriate treatment standards are met.  **Who It Affects**  The bill would affect water utilities, municipalities, states, and the federal government, as well as agricultural and industrial users who could benefit from alternative water supplies. It may also impact environmental protection agencies and public health authorities responsible for establishing water quality and safety standards for recycled water systems.  **Key Provisions**  While specific provisions depend on the bill's full text, legislation with this title typically includes measures such as: federal funding or grants for water recycling infrastructure; coordination between federal agencies (EPA, reclamation agencies) to develop consistent standards for recycled water; support for research and pilot projects; streamlined permitting processes for recycling systems; and technical assistance to states and localities. The goal is to reduce dependence on traditional freshwater sources and support long-term water sustainability, particularly in water-stressed regions."
