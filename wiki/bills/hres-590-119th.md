summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill addresses a presidential budget rescission request submitted on June 3, 2025. Under the Congressional Budget and Impoundment Control Act of 1974, the President can propose to cancel, or \"rescind,\" previously approved federal spending. This bill deals with the Senate's amendments to H.R. 4, which pertains to the President's rescission proposals from that June date. The bill essentially establishes the procedure for Congress to consider the Senate's version of legislation responding to the President's rescission request.  The measure affects federal budget authority—the legal authorization for government agencies to spend money. By considering these rescissions, Congress would be deciding whether to allow the President to cancel certain appropriated funds or require that the money be spent as originally approved. This process involves both the House and Senate reaching agreement on which rescissions to accept or reject.  **Note:** The full text of this bill is not available in this summary. To understand the specific budget items affected and the amounts involved, review the complete legislative text and the President's rescission message from June 3, 2025."
