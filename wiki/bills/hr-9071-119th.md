summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would amend federal law under title 18 of the United States Code to modify criminal prohibitions related to child sexual exploitation. Specifically, it seeks to clarify and expand existing laws that prohibit coercion and enticement of minors by explicitly including patronizing or soliciting a minor for commercial sex acts. The bill aims to strengthen federal criminal statutes that address child sex trafficking and prostitution.  The legislation would affect federal law enforcement agencies, federal prosecutors, and individuals who engage in or attempt to engage in commercial sexual activity with minors. It would also potentially affect sentencing and penalties for these offenses. By expanding the scope of what conduct is prohibited, the bill intends to provide clearer legal language and broader criminal liability for those who seek to exploit minors for sexual purposes.  Key provisions would include explicit language prohibiting the patronizing or soliciting of minors for commercial sex acts, ensuring that federal law clearly covers individuals who purchase sexual services from children, not just those who coerce or entice minors into such situations. This approach targets demand-side conduct in addition to supply-side exploitation."
