summary: "# Summary of Bill to Exempt Religious Institutions from Investment Income Excise Tax  This bill would modify the Internal Revenue Code to exempt qualified religious institutions from a federal excise tax on investment income. The excise tax in question applies to the net investment income of certain tax-exempt organizations and was established under the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017. The bill would carve out religious institutions that meet specified qualification criteria, allowing them to avoid this tax on income generated from investments such as endowments, donations, and other financial holdings.  The bill would affect religious organizations that currently pay the excise tax on investment income. These institutions would need to meet certain qualification standards to receive the exemption, though the specific criteria are not detailed in the title alone. The change would reduce federal tax revenue from this source, as fewer organizations would owe the tax.  Key provisions would include defining which religious institutions qualify for the exemption and establishing the mechanics for claiming it. Religious organizations seeking the exemption would likely need to demonstrate their status and comply with any reporting or certification requirements established by the bill."
