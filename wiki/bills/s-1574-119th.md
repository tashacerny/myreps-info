summary: "# Summary: Tribal Access to Electronic Evidence Act  **What the Bill Proposes**  This bill would grant Native American tribal governments access to electronic evidence and data in criminal and civil proceedings. Specifically, it aims to allow tribal law enforcement and courts to obtain electronic evidence (such as communications records, digital files, and other data held by private companies or service providers) during investigations and legal cases, similar to authorities available to state and federal law enforcement.  **Who It Affects**  The bill primarily affects tribal governments and their judicial systems, as well as Native American communities subject to tribal jurisdiction. It would also impact technology companies and service providers that store electronic data, as they would need to respond to tribal requests for evidence. State and federal law enforcement agencies may also be affected if the bill establishes new protocols for information-sharing with tribal authorities.  **Key Provisions**  While the full text is unavailable, such legislation typically addresses: establishing legal mechanisms for tribal courts to issue warrants or subpoenas for electronic evidence; clarifying what types of data tribal authorities can access; defining procedures for service providers to comply with tribal requests; and potentially addressing coordination between tribal, state, and federal law enforcement. The bill likely aims to close gaps where tribal law enforcement currently lacks the legal tools available to other jurisdictions."
