summary: "# Bill Summary  This joint resolution uses the Congressional Review Act (CRA) to disapprove a rule issued by the Department of Education regarding the William D. Ford Federal Direct Loan Program. The Direct Loan Program is the federal government's primary mechanism for providing student loans to eligible borrowers. Under the CRA, Congress can vote to disapprove regulatory actions within a specified timeframe, effectively overturning the rule if both chambers pass the resolution and it is signed into law (or Congress overrides a presidential veto).  The resolution affects borrowers who use federal student loans, as well as the Department of Education's administration of the Direct Loan Program. The specific provisions of the underlying rule are not detailed in the title, but generally this type of resolution seeks to prevent or reverse regulatory changes to how the Direct Loan Program operates—potentially affecting areas such as loan repayment terms, eligibility requirements, servicer responsibilities, or borrower protections.  If passed, the resolution would invalidate the Department of Education's rule and prevent the agency from reissuing substantially similar regulations without further congressional action. The resolution does not propose new legislation but rather uses Congress's existing authority to block an executive branch regulation."
