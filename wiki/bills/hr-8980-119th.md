summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would amend the Fair Labor Standards Act of 1938 to establish a federal requirement that employers pay employees at least 1.5 times their regular wage rate when they work on legal public holidays. Currently, federal law does not mandate holiday pay premiums, though some states and employers offer them voluntarily. This proposal would create a uniform federal standard for holiday compensation across the country.  The bill would affect private sector employers and their employees. It would require employers to identify which days constitute \"legal public holidays\" for compensation purposes and calculate appropriate premium pay for any work performed on those days. The change would apply broadly unless specific exemptions are included in the bill's full text.  The practical effect would be to increase labor costs for employers with employees working on federal holidays (such as Christmas, Thanksgiving, and Independence Day) and provide guaranteed additional compensation to workers in those circumstances. The bill's impact would vary by industry—for example, retail, hospitality, healthcare, and emergency services sectors typically have employees working holidays."
