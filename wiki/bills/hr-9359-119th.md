SNAP is the federal program that provides food assistance to low-income individuals and families. Currently, when benefits are stolen, households may face difficulty accessing replacement funds. This legislation would establish a mechanism to reissue lost benefits to affected households.  The bill directly affects SNAP recipients whose benefits have been compromised through fraudulent means, as well as state agencies that administer the SNAP program. It also affects the federal government, which funds SNAP benefits.  Key provisions would likely include a process for households to report stolen benefits, verification procedures to confirm theft occurred, and a system for reissuing the lost benefits to eligible households. The bill references both identity theft and skimming practices—where criminals capture card information at payment terminals—as covered fraud scenarios. Additional provisions referenced in the title suggest the bill may address related program administration matters."
