summary: "# Summary of Senate Resolution on Samuel Bankman-Fried  This resolution expresses the Senate's position that Samuel Bankman-Fried should not receive executive clemency from the President, including a pardon or commutation of his sentence. The resolution affirms the Senate's commitment to upholding the rule of law and protecting the integrity of the U.S. financial system.  Bankman-Fried was the founder of FTX, a cryptocurrency exchange that collapsed in 2022. He was convicted in November 2023 on multiple counts of fraud and conspiracy related to the mishandling of customer funds. This resolution would serve as a statement of the Senate's position regarding any potential future clemency action by the President.  The resolution affects primarily the executive branch's consideration of clemency for this individual, though it carries no binding legal force. It reflects the legislative branch's view on an individual case and the broader importance of enforcing financial regulations and fraud statutes."
