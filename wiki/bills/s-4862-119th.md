summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would direct the Secretary of Agriculture to modify eligibility rules for prevented planting payments under existing farm support programs. Prevented planting payments are federal subsidies provided to farmers when they cannot plant crops due to circumstances beyond their control. Currently, acreage that goes unplanted due to insufficient irrigation water may not qualify for these payments. The bill proposes to change this policy so that farmland left unplanted specifically because of lack of irrigation water would be considered eligible for prevented planting benefits.  The bill would primarily affect farmers in irrigation-dependent regions who experience water shortages. It could influence agricultural operations in areas affected by drought, water allocation disputes, or reduced water availability from sources like reservoirs or rivers. The change would potentially increase federal agricultural payments to farmers in affected regions.  The bill's scope appears limited to this specific eligibility modification for prevented planting payments, though the phrase \"and for other purposes\" suggests there may be additional provisions not described in the title alone."
