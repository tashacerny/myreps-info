summary: "# Summary of the Bill  This bill is a procedural measure that establishes the terms under which the House of Representatives will consider three separate pieces of legislation. It does not itself enact policy, but rather sets the debate and voting rules for considering these three matters: two Congressional Review Act (CRA) resolutions that would disapprove of specific federal regulations, and one concurrent resolution establishing the federal budget for fiscal years 2025–2034.  The first two items are CRA disapprovals targeting regulations from the Department of Energy and Environmental Protection Agency. H.J. Res. 20 would disapprove a Department of Energy rule setting energy conservation standards for consumer gas-fired instantaneous water heaters. H.J. Res. 35 would disapprove an EPA rule regarding waste emissions charges and compliance procedures for petroleum and natural gas systems. If passed under the CRA process, these would invalidate the respective regulations.  The third item, H. Con. Res. 14, is the concurrent budget resolution for fiscal year 2025 and establishes budgetary targets through 2034. This affects all federal spending and revenue levels going forward. By bundling these three separate matters into one procedural measure, the bill determines which proposals receive floor time and under what debate and amendment conditions they will be considered."
