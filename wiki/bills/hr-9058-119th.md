summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend the Public Health Service Act to create a new federal grant program focused on improving access to life-saving cardiac emergency equipment and training. Specifically, the program would provide grants to support the installation of automated external defibrillators (AEDs) and provide cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) training in federally assisted multifamily housing properties—that is, apartment buildings and similar residential complexes that receive federal financing or subsidies.  The program would affect property owners and managers of eligible multifamily housing developments, as well as residents of these properties. By making AEDs more widely available and CPR training more accessible in these residential settings, the bill aims to improve survival rates during cardiac emergencies in housing where federal assistance is involved. The specific eligibility criteria, grant amounts, application procedures, and other program details would be established through the legislation's implementing provisions."
