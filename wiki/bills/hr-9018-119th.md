summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would require the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) to notify Congress whenever a suicide or attempted suicide occurs involving a veteran at a VA facility or through a Community Care provider (private healthcare providers contracted by the VA). The notification requirement would apply to incidents involving veterans receiving care directly from the VA or those using VA-funded community healthcare services. The bill aims to give Congress visibility into these incidents so it can monitor veteran safety outcomes and the effectiveness of VA suicide prevention programs.  The bill affects the VA, Community Care providers, Congress, and veterans receiving mental health and other services through these systems. Key provisions include establishing a notice requirement to Congress for each suicide or attempted suicide incident, though the bill title does not specify details about timing, format, or what information must be included in notices.  This legislation addresses veteran suicide prevention and oversight, which has been an ongoing focus of congressional attention given elevated suicide rates among veterans compared to the general population."
