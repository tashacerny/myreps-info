summary: "# Summary of Olmstead v. L.C. Anniversary Resolution  This resolution commemorates the 27th anniversary of the Supreme Court's 1999 decision in *Olmstead v. L.C.*, a landmark case concerning the rights of people with disabilities. The resolution recognizes the significance of this ruling and honors its impact on disability policy and civil rights.  The *Olmstead* decision established that individuals with disabilities have a right to receive services in community settings rather than institutions, when appropriate. This ruling applied the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and affected policies governing mental health and long-term care services. The resolution acknowledges this decision's influence on how states and the federal government provide services to people with disabilities, emphasizing community integration as a legal principle.  As a resolution rather than legislation, this measure does not create new laws or programs. Instead, it serves a commemorative function—marking a significant date in disability rights history and reaffirming the principle that people with disabilities should have opportunities to live and receive services in integrated community settings rather than segregated institutional facilities."
