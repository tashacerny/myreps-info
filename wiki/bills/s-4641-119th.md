summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would modify Medicare's rules for skilled nursing facility (SNF) coverage by changing how hospital stays are counted toward eligibility. Currently, Medicare requires patients to spend at least 3 consecutive days in a hospital as an inpatient before they qualify for coverage of subsequent skilled nursing facility care. The bill proposes to count time spent receiving outpatient observation services in a hospital toward meeting this 3-day requirement, in addition to traditional inpatient days.  The change would primarily affect Medicare beneficiaries who receive observation services—a category of hospital care that does not classify patients as formal inpatients. Under current rules, these observation days do not count toward the 3-day threshold, even if the patient is hospitalized and later requires skilled nursing care. This bill would make it easier for patients receiving observation services to become eligible for SNF coverage by allowing those days to count toward the requirement.  The bill affects Medicare beneficiaries, hospitals, and skilled nursing facilities. Hospitals may see operational changes in how observation stays are classified and tracked, while SNFs could potentially see changes in admission patterns. The provision could increase access to covered SNF services for some patients, though the fiscal impact and implementation details would depend on the bill's specific language and regulatory guidance."
