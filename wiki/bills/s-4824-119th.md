summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend federal law to authorize hazard pay for federal employees who conduct prescribed burns. Prescribed burns are controlled fires set by land management agencies to reduce wildfire risk, clear dead vegetation, and maintain ecosystem health. The legislation would recognize the inherent dangers of this work by providing additional compensation to employees performing these duties.  The bill primarily affects federal employees in land management agencies, particularly the U.S. Forest Service and Bureau of Land Management, who carry out prescribed burning operations. These employees face occupational hazards including smoke inhalation, heat exposure, and burn injuries while managing these controlled fires.  Key provisions would establish hazard pay rates or criteria for employees engaged in prescribed burn activities. The bill may specify eligibility requirements, pay rates, and which agencies' employees qualify for this additional compensation. By designating prescribed burn work as hazard pay-eligible, the legislation aims to recognize the risks involved and potentially improve recruitment and retention for this specialized work."
