The legislation directs resources toward advancing technologies and methods that could make fuel recycling more efficient, cost-effective, and safer.  **Who It Affects**  The bill primarily affects the nuclear energy sector, including power plant operators, nuclear research institutions, and the U.S. Department of Energy. It may also have indirect effects on nuclear waste management policy and taxpayers who fund research programs. Other stakeholders include environmental and energy professionals involved in fuel cycle development.  **Key Provisions**  While specific details would require the full text, bills of this type typically include funding authorizations for research programs, establishment of collaboration between government laboratories and private industry, and directives to study the technical and economic feasibility of various recycling approaches.
