summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would direct the President to remove United States Armed Forces from any hostilities involving Iran, invoking the War Powers Resolution of 1973. The War Powers Resolution requires the President to notify Congress within 48 hours of committing armed forces to military action and limits such action to 60 days (with a possible 30-day extension) unless Congress authorizes continued involvement. Section 5(c) of the Resolution allows Congress to direct the removal of armed forces by concurrent resolution.  The bill would apply to any U.S. military personnel engaged in armed conflict with Iran or Iranian forces. It affects the President's command authority over U.S. Armed Forces and would require compliance with the directive if passed. Key provisions would include an immediate order to withdraw military forces from hostilities with Iran, with implementation according to the terms specified in the resolution.  This type of measure represents Congress's constitutional authority over military deployments and is one mechanism available under the War Powers Resolution for Congress to check executive military action without declaring or undeclaring war. The bill does not appear to address the timeframe for withdrawal or circumstances under which forces might be repositioned."
