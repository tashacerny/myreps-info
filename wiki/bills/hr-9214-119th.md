summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would authorize participants in the Thrift Savings Fund (TSF)—a retirement savings plan available to federal employees and service members—to electronically transfer their account balances to other qualified retirement plans. Currently, TSF participants have limited options for moving their savings to external retirement accounts. This legislation would expand those options by establishing procedures for direct electronic transfers to IRAs, 401(k) plans, and other eligible retirement plans.  The bill primarily affects current and former federal employees, military service members, and their beneficiaries who participate in the TSF. It would also impact the TSF administrative structure, which would need to implement systems and processes to facilitate these transfers. The key provisions would likely include defining which retirement plans qualify as acceptable transfer destinations, establishing electronic transfer procedures and timelines, addressing tax treatment of transfers, and specifying any restrictions or conditions on when transfers can occur.  The stated purpose of expanding transfer options is to provide participants with greater flexibility in managing their retirement savings and potentially consolidating accounts across multiple employers or account types."
