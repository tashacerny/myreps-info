summary: "# Summary of Procedural Bill for House Consideration  This is a procedural resolution that sets rules for the House of Representatives to consider five separate bills and one concurrent resolution. The resolution itself does not create new law but rather establishes the timeline, debate procedures, and amendments allowed for each measure on the House floor.  The five substantive bills covered are: H.R. 5625, which would require the Attorney General to publicly list states and local governments that allow cashless bail systems; H.R. 6260, which would amend federal law to prohibit fraud related to bail posting; H.R. 8365, which would establish conditions on how courts appoint monitors; H.R. 8469, which appropriates federal funding for military construction, Veterans Affairs, and related agencies for fiscal year 2027; and H. Con. Res. 96, a concurrent resolution expressing support for law enforcement. This procedural measure affects members of Congress by determining how and when they may debate and vote on these matters, but does not directly affect the public until the underlying bills are passed and enacted."
