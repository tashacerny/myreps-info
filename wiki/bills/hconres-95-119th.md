summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill invokes the War Powers Resolution to direct the President to remove U.S. Armed Forces from hostilities involving Iran. The War Powers Resolution, enacted in 1973, allows Congress to require the withdrawal of armed forces from military operations if Congress has not declared war or authorized the use of force. Section 5(c) of the resolution specifically permits Congress to direct the President to remove forces from hostilities through concurrent resolution.  The bill would affect any U.S. military personnel currently engaged in or supporting military operations against Iran or Iranian-backed forces. If passed, it would require the President to cease such operations and withdraw armed forces involved in these hostilities within a specified timeframe (typically 30 days under War Powers provisions, unless Congress authorizes continued deployment).  This measure represents congressional action on the scope and authorization of military engagement with Iran. It does not address broader diplomatic relations or sanctions policy—only the use of armed force. The bill's passage would depend on securing sufficient votes in both the House and Senate, and it could face presidential veto."
