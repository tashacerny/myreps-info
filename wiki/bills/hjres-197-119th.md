summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill is a congressional disapproval resolution under the Congressional Review Act (CRA), a procedure that allows Congress to reject federal agency rules. Specifically, it targets a regulation issued by the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) regarding benefit and payment parameters for 2027 under the Affordable Care Act and the Basic Health Program. If passed by both chambers and signed by the President, the rule would be nullified and CMS would be prohibited from issuing a substantially similar rule without new legislative authority.  The regulation affects health insurance markets and coverage options for 2027. It would impact insurers, individuals purchasing health insurance through the ACA marketplace, and states that operate the Basic Health Program (which provides coverage to individuals with income between 139% and 200% of the federal poverty level). The rule likely establishes details about covered benefits, cost-sharing limits, premium rates, and other insurance parameters for the upcoming year.  The key provision of the resolution is the disapproval itself—if enacted, it would undo the CMS rule. Under the CRA, the disapproval process is expedited and cannot be filibustered in the Senate, making it a procedural tool for Congress to exercise oversight over regulatory actions. The substantive details of what specific provisions within the rule would be rejected cannot be confirmed without access to the full regulatory text."
