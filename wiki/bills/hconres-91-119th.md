summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would direct the President to remove United States Armed Forces from any ongoing or imminent hostilities with Iran. It operates under section 5(c) of the War Powers Resolution, a 1973 law that establishes procedures for congressional oversight of military operations. Section 5(c) allows Congress to compel the President to withdraw armed forces from military action not authorized by Congress or a declaration of war, typically through a concurrent resolution.  **Who It Affects:** The bill would directly affect U.S. military personnel involved in operations related to Iran, as well as the executive branch's authority to conduct such operations. It may also have implications for U.S. foreign policy and regional allies or partners.  **Key Provision:** The central provision is a directive to cease and remove U.S. armed forces from hostilities with Iran. The bill does not propose new legislation but rather exercises Congress's constitutional authority to limit executive military action under the War Powers Resolution framework. If passed, it would require the President to withdraw military forces unless Congress subsequently authorizes their continued deployment."
