summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to require large financial institutions to defer a portion of compensation for senior employees. The deferred compensation would be held and used to pay any civil or criminal fines that the institution or its subsidiaries incurs. By linking executive compensation to potential penalties, the bill aims to create financial incentives for senior management to ensure their institutions comply with laws and regulations.  The bill would affect senior employees at large financial institutions and their subsidiaries. It establishes a mechanism where deferred pay serves as a reserve for fines rather than going directly to employees. This could impact how financial firms structure executive compensation packages and may influence internal compliance practices and risk management decisions.  Key provisions would likely include definitions of which institutions and employees are covered, the percentage or amount of compensation to be deferred, procedures for using deferred funds to pay fines, and rules for returning unused deferred compensation to employees. The mechanism aims to align the financial interests of senior management with institutional compliance and reduce incentives that might encourage excessive risk-taking."
