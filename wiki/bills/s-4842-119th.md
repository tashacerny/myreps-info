summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend the Agricultural Marketing Act of 1946 to make permanent the Resilient Food Systems Infrastructure Program, which currently operates under temporary authorization. The bill also establishes regional food systems hubs as part of the legislation. These changes affect the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA), food producers, regional agricultural networks, and communities that depend on local and regional food distribution systems.  Key provisions include converting the Resilient Food Systems Infrastructure Program from a temporary to permanent authorization, which would allow ongoing funding and operations without requiring reauthorization. The bill also establishes regional food systems hubs—facilities or networks designed to aggregate, process, and distribute food from local and regional producers to consumers and institutional buyers. This infrastructure aims to strengthen food supply chains at the regional level, potentially reducing reliance on long-distance distribution networks and supporting agricultural producers outside major national commodity markets.  The legislation would affect USDA administration and budget allocation, as well as agricultural businesses and communities seeking to develop or participate in regional food systems. The specific funding levels, eligibility criteria, and operational requirements would be detailed in the full bill text."
