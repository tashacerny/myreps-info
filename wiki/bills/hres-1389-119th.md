summary: "# Summary  This resolution expresses the House of Representatives' position that water system monitoring and regulation should not be used to surveil or track individuals' use of medication abortion. The measure appears to address concerns that testing infrastructure designed to monitor water quality could be repurposed to detect evidence of abortion medication use among populations, potentially enabling enforcement actions or restrictions on abortion access.  The resolution affects water system operators, regulatory agencies, and individuals in states where abortion access is restricted. Key implicit provisions would discourage or prohibit using water testing data—such as detecting abortion medication metabolites in wastewater—as evidence for law enforcement purposes or to restrict access to medication abortion. The measure takes no direct legislative action but states a position that water system oversight should maintain its public health focus rather than serve surveillance or enforcement functions related to abortion care."
