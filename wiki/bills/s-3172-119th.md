Sanctions are restrictions—such as trade bans, asset freezes, or import/export limitations—that the U.S. government uses as a policy tool to influence another country's behavior or actions. By repealing these Acts, the bill would remove the legal basis for these sanctions to remain in effect.  **Who It Affects**  The bill would affect U.S. businesses and individuals engaged in trade or financial dealings with Syria, as current sanctions restrict such interactions. It would also affect the Syrian government and economy, which are currently subject to these restrictions. Additionally, it impacts U.S. foreign policy by altering the government's tools for diplomatic engagement with Syria.  **Key Considerations**  Without access to the full bill text, the specific Acts to be repealed cannot be identified. However, the U.S. has maintained various Syria sanctions programs for decades related to concerns including human rights, counterterrorism, and regional stability.
