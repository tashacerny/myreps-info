summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes amendments to the Expedited Funds Availability Act (EFAA), a federal law that sets timeframes for when banks must make deposited funds available to customers. The amendments would create exceptions to the standard fund availability rules specifically for cases involving fraudulent checks or wire transfers. These exceptions would allow banks to hold funds longer than normally permitted when fraud is suspected or detected, providing financial institutions with additional protection against fraudulent transactions.  The bill would primarily affect banks and depositing customers. Banks would gain clearer legal authority to delay fund availability when handling fraudulent items, while customers attempting to deposit fraudulent checks or initiate fraudulent wire transfers would experience longer hold periods. The amendments aim to reduce losses from fraud while clarifying the legal framework under which banks can protect themselves and their customers.  Key provisions would likely include defining what constitutes fraud for purposes of the exemption, specifying how long banks may hold funds in fraud cases, and establishing notification requirements for customers whose deposits are subject to extended holds due to suspected fraud. The exact scope and procedures would depend on the specific language of the legislation."
