summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill directs three federal financial regulators—the Federal Reserve Board of Governors, the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC), and the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC)—to conduct a study on partnerships between financial technology (fintech) companies and banking organizations. The study would examine how these partnerships might facilitate the creation of new banks and improve the financial health and viability of community banks.  The bill focuses on understanding potential benefits and mechanisms through which fintech-banking partnerships could support banking formation and strengthen smaller financial institutions. Community banks, which typically serve local and regional markets, have faced consolidation pressures in recent years. The study would likely explore how fintech partnerships—such as through technology sharing, service provision, or operational collaboration—could help both new banking entrants enter the market and existing community banks remain competitive and sustainable.  As a study requirement rather than a regulatory mandate, the bill does not directly impose new rules on financial institutions. Instead, it seeks to inform policymakers about the role fintech partnerships might play in banking market dynamics. The results could inform future regulatory or legislative decisions affecting community banks, fintech companies, and banking formation requirements."
