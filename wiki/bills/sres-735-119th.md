The resolution affects federal recognition and designation only—it does not create new laws, funding, or mandates. It may encourage states, municipalities, and organizations to acknowledge the week through ceremonies, proclamations, or events. The week typically honors law enforcement officers, including those serving in federal, state, local, and tribal agencies.  Key provisions of such resolutions are typically limited to the formal designation itself. Congress may include language recognizing the contributions of police officers and encouraging public acknowledgment of their service. As a resolution rather than legislation, it carries symbolic rather than binding legal authority."
