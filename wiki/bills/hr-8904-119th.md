Currently, the Magnuson-Stevens Act governs fishery management in federal waters, but its application to marine national monuments is unclear or limited. This bill clarifies that fishing in these monuments would be subject to the Act's management and conservation requirements.  The bill would affect commercial and recreational fishing operations in marine national monuments, as well as the fishery management councils responsible for developing regulations in federal waters. It would also impact fishing communities, seafood producers, and marine conservation interests that have stakes in how these protected areas are managed.  Key provisions would likely include establishing that marine national monuments are subject to Magnuson-Stevens Act requirements for sustainable fishing practices, requiring fishery management plans or amendments for monument waters, and potentially allowing for fishing restrictions or prohibitions based on conservation needs.
