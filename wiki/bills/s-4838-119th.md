The exemption would apply to workers employed in activities such as guided hiking, hunting, fishing, camping, or similar outdoor recreation businesses. Affected workers would include guides, outfitters, and related support staff employed by businesses providing these services to the public.  Under this bill, employers in the outdoor recreation industry would no longer be required to pay affected employees the federal minimum wage or comply with overtime rules (typically requiring time-and-a-half pay for hours over 40 per week). The bill specifies that the exemption applies only to \"certain\" employees, suggesting eligibility would be limited by specific criteria, though the exact definitions would depend on the bill's full text.  This change would primarily affect small outdoor recreation businesses and their employees. Workers in these roles would lose federal wage and hour protections, while employers could reduce labor costs. The impact would depend on implementation details, such as which job categories qualify and whether exemptions apply to all hours worked or only certain activities."
