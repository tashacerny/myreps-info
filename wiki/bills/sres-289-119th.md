summary: "# Bill Summary: National Pollinator Week Resolution  This resolution designates June 15–22, 2025, as \"National Pollinator Week\" in the United States. The resolution recognizes the ecological and agricultural importance of pollinators—animals such as bees, butterflies, moths, birds, and other insects that transfer pollen between plants. By establishing this observance week, Congress formally acknowledges the role these organisms play in supporting ecosystem health and food production.  The resolution affects the general public and does not impose requirements or restrictions on individuals or businesses. Instead, it serves a symbolic purpose by encouraging awareness and celebration of pollinators during the designated week. Agricultural producers, conservation organizations, educators, and other groups may use the week to promote pollinator-related initiatives, but participation is voluntary.  Key aspects include the official designation of specific dates and an implicit statement of Congress's recognition that pollinators contribute significantly to agriculture and natural ecosystems in the U.S. This type of resolution does not create new law, appropriate funds, or establish regulatory measures; rather, it is a legislative expression of support for a cause or observance."
