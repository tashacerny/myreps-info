summary: "# Summary of Proposed Constitutional Amendment on House Vacancies  This proposed amendment to the U.S. Constitution would establish a temporary mechanism to fill vacancies in the House of Representatives when they occur. The amendment aims to ensure the House can continue operating with sufficient membership during the period between when a vacancy arises and when a permanent replacement is elected through the normal electoral process. This addresses situations where deaths, resignations, or other departures create gaps in representation.  The amendment would affect all members of the House of Representatives and their constituents. It would establish rules for how vacancies are temporarily filled, likely specifying who appoints temporary members and for how long they may serve before a special election takes place. The exact procedures would be defined within the amendment text itself.  The core purpose is to maintain congressional continuity by preventing extended periods when House seats remain empty. This could be particularly important during emergencies or situations where multiple vacancies occur simultaneously. Any such constitutional amendment would require approval by two-thirds of both the House and Senate, followed by ratification by three-fourths of state legislatures before taking effect."
