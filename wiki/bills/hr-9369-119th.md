summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend federal transportation law to allow employees or authorized agents of trucking companies to edit or annotate electronic logging device (ELD) records. Electronic logging devices are mandated systems that automatically record driver hours of service for regulatory compliance. Under this proposal, such edits or annotations would be permitted only if the person making the change is physically located in North America and the driver whose record is being modified approves the change.  The bill would primarily affect trucking companies and their administrative staff, as well as drivers whose hours-of-service records could be subject to editing. It establishes conditions intended to maintain oversight of any modifications to these records—namely the geographic requirement and driver approval—which are safety and compliance instruments regulated by the Department of Transportation.  The specific intent behind authorizing such edits—whether to correct errors, accommodate legitimate schedule changes, or address other operational needs—is not clear from the title alone. The bill's practical effects would depend on regulatory guidance about what types of changes qualify as permissible edits or annotations."
