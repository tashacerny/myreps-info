summary: "# Summary of Congressional Disapproval of BLM Coastal Plain Oil and Gas Rule  This bill proposes to use the Congressional Review Act (CRA) to disapprove a rule issued by the Bureau of Land Management (BLM) regarding the Coastal Plain Oil and Gas Leasing Program. Under the CRA, Congress can reject federal agency rules within a limited timeframe. If passed, this disapproval would invalidate the BLM's Record of Decision related to oil and gas leasing activities in the Coastal Plain region.  The rule being challenged concerns the authorization and procedures for leasing federal lands in Alaska's Coastal Plain for oil and gas development. A disapproval would prevent the BLM rule from taking effect or continuing in force. This would affect oil and gas companies seeking to lease lands in this area, environmental stakeholders with concerns about the program, and the federal government's management of public lands and resources in Alaska.  The key provision is the invocation of the CRA mechanism to formally reject the BLM's Record of Decision. If approved by both chambers of Congress and signed by the President, the disapproval would effectively overturn the rule, though affected parties could potentially seek future regulatory action through standard administrative processes."
