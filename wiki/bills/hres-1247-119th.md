summary: "# Summary of H.R. 2102 Consideration  This bill proposes to amend federal law to allow certain military retirees to receive both veterans' disability compensation and military retirement pay simultaneously. Currently, federal law generally requires individuals to choose between these two forms of compensation rather than receive both. The bill would change this rule specifically for retirees whose disabilities are combat-related, allowing them to collect their full military retirement benefits while also receiving disability compensation from the Department of Veterans Affairs.  The primary groups affected by this change would be military service members who retired due to service-connected disabilities incurred in combat situations. Currently, these individuals must offset their military retirement pay by the amount they receive in VA disability benefits, effectively reducing their total income. Under this proposal, combat-disabled retirees would no longer face this reduction and would receive full concurrent payments from both systems.  The bill's main provision is the authorization of concurrent receipt of both compensation streams for combat-related disability cases. The title also notes the bill addresses \"other purposes,\" indicating there may be additional provisions not fully described in the title alone. The measure would require changes to Title 10 of the U.S. Code, which governs military pay and benefits."
