summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend Medicare (Title XVIII of the Social Security Act) to create a new option allowing patients and healthcare providers to enter into private contracts for Medicare-covered services without facing penalties. Under current Medicare rules, providers who opt out of Medicare face restrictions on billing Medicare beneficiaries. This bill would permit direct contracting arrangements while still enabling patients to apply their Medicare benefits toward the contracted services.  The legislation would primarily affect Medicare beneficiaries, healthcare providers who treat Medicare patients, and the Medicare program itself. It would allow eligible professionals (such as physicians and other practitioners) and patients greater flexibility in arranging their care through private agreements, potentially expanding options beyond the standard Medicare fee-for-service model.  Key provisions of the bill include establishing the mechanics of free contracting between patients and providers, defining which professionals and services are eligible, and ensuring that Medicare benefits can still be utilized in these private arrangements. The bill aims to remove financial penalties that currently discourage such contracts, though the specific details of implementation, eligibility criteria, and benefit coordination would depend on the full legislative text and any regulatory guidance that follows enactment."
