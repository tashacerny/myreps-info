summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill invokes the War Powers Resolution to direct the President to remove United States Armed Forces from any ongoing or imminent hostilities involving Iran. The War Powers Resolution, enacted in 1973, allows Congress to require the President to terminate military operations in specific circumstances. This bill represents Congress exercising its constitutional authority over military engagement by formally instructing the executive branch to cease or withdraw from such hostilities.  The bill would affect all branches of the U.S. Armed Forces currently engaged in military operations against Iran or Iranian forces. It would also impact the President's ability to initiate new military action against Iran without separate congressional authorization. The provision references section 5(c) of the War Powers Resolution, which provides a mechanism for Congress to direct the President to end military hostilities through concurrent resolution.  Key to understanding this bill's effect: it would require the removal of armed forces rather than simply limiting their deployment or requesting consultation. The precise scope—whether it applies to all U.S. military presence in the region or only direct combat operations—would depend on how \"hostilities with Iran\" is interpreted and applied in practice."
