summary: "# Summary of America's Olympic and Paralympic Games Commemorative Coins Act  This bill authorizes the U.S. Mint to design and issue commemorative coins celebrating the Olympic and Paralympic Games. Commemorative coins are special-edition coins produced in limited quantities to honor specific events, themes, or anniversaries. This legislation would allow the Mint to create coins dedicated to these international sporting competitions, typically with unique designs reflecting Olympic and Paralympic imagery and symbolism.  The bill affects the U.S. Mint, which would be responsible for producing the coins, and potentially Olympic and Paralympic organizing bodies or host communities. Key provisions would likely include specifications such as the number of coins to be minted, denominations, design requirements, and the time period during which coins can be issued. These coins are typically sold to collectors at a premium price above face value, with proceeds often directed toward specific purposes—which may include supporting Olympic operations, athlete programs, or other designated beneficiaries.  Such commemorative coin programs generate revenue for the government while creating collectible items for numismatists and the public. The legislation establishes the framework and authorization needed for the Mint to move forward with production and sales of Olympic and Paralympic-themed coins."
