summary: "# Summary of Bill  This is a procedural resolution that sets the terms for House floor consideration of four separate legislative items. It does not create new policy itself but rather establishes the rules and timeline under which the House will debate and vote on these bills. The resolution determines matters such as how much time will be allowed for debate, what amendments may be offered, and other procedural parameters for each measure.  The four bills covered are: (1) H.R. 8029, which appropriates funding for the Department of Homeland Security for fiscal year 2026; (2) H. Res. 1128, a resolution expressing House support for the Department of Homeland Security; (3) H.R. 5103, which would establish a beautification program for the District of Columbia and create an associated commission; and (4) H.R. 7084, which would amend federal maritime law to regulate what types of vessels may operate in U.S. navigable waters and transfer cargo in U.S. ports.  This resolution affects members of Congress and any stakeholders interested in these four legislative proposals, as it determines whether and how quickly they will receive a House vote. The substantive impacts of each individual bill would affect different groups—federal employees and security operations (H.R. 8029), the District of Columbia (H.R. 5103), and the maritime shipping industry (H.R. 7084)—but this resolution itself is a technical procedural measure."
