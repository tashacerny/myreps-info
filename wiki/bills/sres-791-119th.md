summary: "# Bill Summary  This resolution expresses disapproval of China's Ethnic Unity and Progress Law and its implementation in regions with ethnic minority populations, particularly Tibet, Xinjiang (home to Uyghurs), and Inner Mongolia. The resolution states concerns that the law affects the rights, freedoms, and cultural identity of these communities. It calls on the Chinese government to cease what the resolution characterizes as abusive practices and campaigns of transnational repression.  The resolution affects U.S.-China relations and immigrant and diaspora communities in the United States from the regions in question. Key provisions include condemning the Chinese law, urging the Chinese government to end alleged abuses and transnational repression activities, and implicitly addressing concerns about how such policies may impact people living in the United States. As a resolution rather than legislation, this measure expresses the sense of Congress but does not create law or binding requirements on U.S. agencies."
