summary: "# Bill Summary: Haiti Temporary Protected Status Designation  This bill would require the Secretary of Homeland Security to designate Haiti for Temporary Protected Status (TPS). TPS is a federal immigration program that allows nationals of designated countries experiencing ongoing armed conflict, natural disaster, or other extraordinary conditions to remain and work in the United States on a temporary basis. If enacted, this bill would mandate that Haiti receive this designation, rather than leaving it to the Secretary's discretion.  The designation would affect Haitian nationals currently in the United States, as well as those who may enter during the TPS period. Eligible individuals would be permitted to stay lawfully, obtain work authorization, and potentially receive other immigration benefits. The bill would also impact the Department of Homeland Security, which would be required to implement the program, and potentially affect employers who hire TPS beneficiaries.  Key provisions would include the automatic designation of Haiti for TPS and would likely establish or extend the period during which Haitian nationals can access these protections. The bill represents a legislative approach to immigration policy, since TPS designations are normally made administratively by the executive branch based on conditions in the country."
