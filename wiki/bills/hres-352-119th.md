The resolution affects elected officials at all levels of government, community leaders, educators, and the general public by encouraging them to participate in efforts to combat antisemitic incidents and sentiment, and to incorporate information about Jewish American accomplishments and experiences into public discourse and educational settings. It may also draw attention to existing antisemitism concerns and existing resources for education on this topic.  Key aspects likely include calls to: address antisemitic hate crimes and discrimination; promote awareness of the Jewish American community's contributions to American society, culture, and history; and encourage dialogue and cooperation among community leaders to prevent antisemitism. As a resolution rather than legislation, it carries symbolic weight and expresses congressional intent without creating binding requirements or appropriating funds."
