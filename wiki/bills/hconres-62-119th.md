summary: "# Bill Summary: Barbara Rose Johns Statue Unveiling  **What the Bill Proposes**  This bill authorizes the use of Emancipation Hall in the Capitol Visitor Center to host an event unveiling a statue of Barbara Rose Johns. The legislation grants permission for this specific commemorative event to take place in this congressional facility.  **Who It Affects**  The bill affects organizers planning the statue unveiling event, Congress (which controls Capitol facilities), and the public who may attend the ceremony. It also indirectly recognizes Barbara Rose Johns by facilitating a formal tribute in a prominent federal location.  **Key Provisions**  The bill allows organizers to use Emancipation Hall—a public space within the Capitol Visitor Center—for the unveiling event. This constitutes a one-time authorization for a specific commemorative purpose. Such authorizations typically require congressional approval before private or organizational events can use Capitol facilities."
