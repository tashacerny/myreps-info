summary: "# Summary of Bill (Rule for Consideration)  This is a procedural bill that establishes the rules for House floor debate and voting on three separate legislative measures. The bill does not itself enact policy; rather, it sets the terms under which the House will consider these three items: two Congressional Review Act resolutions (H.J. Res. 24 and H.J. Res. 75) and one substantive bill (H.R. 1048).  The first two resolutions seek to disapprove energy efficiency regulations issued by the Department of Energy. H.J. Res. 24 targets new energy conservation standards for walk-in coolers and freezers, while H.J. Res. 75 targets standards for commercial refrigerators, freezers, and refrigerator-freezers. Under the Congressional Review Act, Congress can vote to overturn federal regulations within a specified timeframe. These resolutions would affect businesses and manufacturers in the commercial refrigeration industry, as well as consumers who purchase or use these appliances.  H.R. 1048 would amend federal higher education law to require colleges and universities to disclose foreign gifts and contracts and would prohibit institutions from entering certain agreements with designated foreign entities and countries of concern. This measure would affect colleges, universities, and international academic partnerships. By establishing this procedural rule, the House determines how debate will be structured and what amendments, if any, lawmakers may propose to each of these measures before voting."
