summary: "# Summary of Bill to Reauthorize the National Volcano Early Warning and Monitoring System  This bill proposes to amend the John D. Dingell, Jr. Conservation, Management, and Recreation Act by reauthorizing the National Volcano Early Warning and Monitoring System (NVEWS). The NVEWS is a federal program that tracks volcanic activity and provides early warning of potential eruptions. Reauthorization means extending the program's legal authority and funding, allowing it to continue operating beyond its current authorization expiration date.  The bill would primarily affect the U.S. Geological Survey (USGS), which operates the monitoring system, and communities in volcanic regions—primarily in the western United States (including Hawaii and Alaska, which have active volcanoes). The program monitors volcanic hazards and disseminates warnings to state and local authorities and the public.  While the full text is not available, reauthorization bills typically specify funding levels, program duration, and any modifications to existing authorities or responsibilities. This bill appears focused on ensuring continued federal capability to monitor volcanic threats and provide timely warnings to protect public safety and infrastructure in affected areas."
