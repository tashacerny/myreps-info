summary: "# Summary of Senate Resolution on Highland Park Shooting Anniversary  This resolution expresses the condolences of the Senate and honors the memory of victims of the mass shooting that occurred at a Fourth of July parade in Highland Park, Illinois, on July 4, 2022. The resolution is being introduced on the third anniversary of the event. It is a commemorative measure rather than legislation that creates new laws or policies.  The resolution affects and honors the victims of the shooting, their families, and the Highland Park community. It serves as an official acknowledgment by the Senate of the tragedy and its impact. This type of resolution allows Congress to formally recognize significant events and express collective sentiment on matters of national concern.  As a commemorative resolution, this measure does not establish new legal requirements, funding, or programs. Instead, it functions as a formal statement of the Senate, allowing the chamber to place on record its acknowledgment of the tragedy and sympathy for those affected by the event."
