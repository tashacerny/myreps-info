summary: "# Bill Summary: Pasteurized Donor Human Milk Safety and Regulation  **Purpose and Scope** This bill aims to strengthen safety standards and regulatory oversight for pasteurized donor human milk. Donor human milk is collected from nursing mothers and made available to patients—typically infants in neonatal settings—who cannot receive their own mother's milk. The bill would establish or enhance federal regulations governing how this milk is processed, tested, stored, and distributed to ensure it meets consistent safety standards.  **Key Affected Parties** The bill affects milk banks (organizations that collect, process, and distribute donor milk), healthcare facilities that use donor milk (particularly neonatal intensive care units), nursing mothers who donate milk, and patients receiving donor milk. It may also impact the Food and Drug Administration (FDA), which oversees milk bank operations.  **Likely Provisions** While the full text is unavailable, bills on this topic typically address requirements such as donor screening and health testing protocols, pasteurization standards, microbial testing procedures, labeling and traceability requirements, storage and handling guidelines, and quality assurance measures. The legislation may also establish clearer FDA authority over milk banks or create standardized best practices across facilities."
