summary: "# Summary of Endangered Species Day Designation Bill  This bill proposes to designate May 15, 2026, as \"Endangered Species Day\" in the United States. The designation is a commemorative measure intended to raise public awareness about species at risk of extinction and the importance of wildlife conservation efforts.  The bill would affect all Americans by establishing an official day for recognizing endangered species and conservation work. The designation does not create new legal requirements or funding mechanisms; rather, it serves as a symbolic recognition that may be marked by government agencies, educational institutions, non-profit organizations, and private citizens through awareness activities and educational programs.  Key aspects of such designations typically include encouraging Americans to learn about threatened wildlife, highlighting successful conservation efforts, and promoting support for endangered species protection initiatives already in place under existing law—most notably the Endangered Species Act of 1973."
