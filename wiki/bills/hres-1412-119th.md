summary: "# Bill Summary: National Fireworks Month  This bill expresses congressional support for designating July as \"National Fireworks Month\" in the United States. The measure is a symbolic resolution rather than legislation that creates binding requirements or appropriates funds. It acknowledges July as the month containing Independence Day (July 4th) and proposes recognizing the month more broadly as a time associated with fireworks celebrations across the country.  The bill would affect how the federal government officially recognizes July through this designated title, though it carries no direct regulatory or budgetary impact. The designation is primarily ceremonial and would not change existing laws governing fireworks safety, sales, or use at the state or local level, which remain under separate jurisdiction.  Key provisions would include the formal designation of July as \"National Fireworks Month\" and an expression of support for celebrating America's birthday and patriotic traditions during that time. As a resolution of support rather than substantive legislation, the bill does not create new programs, impose requirements on businesses or individuals, or allocate federal resources."
