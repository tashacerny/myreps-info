summary: "# Bill Summary: Medal of Honor for James Capers, Jr.  This bill would authorize the President to award the Medal of Honor to James Capers, Jr., in recognition of acts of valor he performed as a member of the U.S. Marine Corps during the Vietnam War. The Medal of Honor is the highest military decoration awarded by the United States, given to service members who demonstrate extraordinary heroism in combat. The bill would grant the President the authority to present this award to Capers for his battlefield conduct during the Vietnam conflict.  The legislation affects James Capers, Jr., who served in the Marine Corps, and potentially his family. Congressional action is required because Medal of Honor awards typically require specific legislative authorization. The bill contains no other provisions beyond granting this authorization to the President to present the award based on Capers' documented acts of valor during his military service."
