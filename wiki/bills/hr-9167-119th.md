summary: "# Bill Summary  **What the Bill Proposes** This bill would amend the Congressional Budget Act of 1974 by modifying Section 313, which is known as the \"Byrd Rule.\" The Byrd Rule is a procedural rule that allows the Senate to remove provisions from budget reconciliation bills if they are deemed \"extraneous\"—meaning they fall outside the scope of the budget process. This bill would specifically classify provisions that result in the sale, disposal, or transfer of federal lands as extraneous under this rule.  **Who It Affects** This change would affect the legislative process for budget bills considered under reconciliation procedures in the Senate. It would directly impact any proposals to sell, dispose of, or transfer federal lands that might be included in budget legislation. The bill would effectively prevent such land-related provisions from being included in reconciliation bills, which are bills that require only a simple majority to pass (rather than the typical 60-vote threshold needed to overcome a filibuster).  **Key Effect** By designating land sale and transfer provisions as extraneous, the bill would require that any federal land transactions be addressed through separate, standard legislation rather than through budget reconciliation bills. This makes the procedural path for such provisions more difficult, as they would need to satisfy normal Senate procedures and potentially require broader support to advance."
