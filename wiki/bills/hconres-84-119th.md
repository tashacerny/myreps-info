Under section 5(c) of the War Powers Resolution, Congress can require the President to remove military forces from a foreign country within a specified timeframe if Congress determines such action is necessary. The bill would apply this congressional authority to U.S. military personnel and operations currently in Lebanon.  The measure would affect U.S. military forces deployed in Lebanon, as well as any ongoing military operations or support missions in that country. It would also impact the executive branch's ability to maintain a military presence there without explicit congressional authorization beyond this directive.  Key provisions would include a requirement for the President to comply with the withdrawal directive under the War Powers Resolution framework.
