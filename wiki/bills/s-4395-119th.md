summary: "# Summary: Terrorism Risk Insurance Program Reauthorization Act of 2026  **What the Bill Proposes** This bill reauthorizes the Terrorism Risk Insurance Program (TRIP), a federal backstop program that helps the insurance industry manage losses from terrorist attacks. The program was originally created after the September 11, 2001 attacks to ensure that property and casualty insurers could offer terrorism coverage and that businesses could obtain it. Without reauthorization, the program would expire and federal support for terrorism-related insurance claims would end.  **Who It Affects** The bill directly affects property and casualty insurers, businesses that need terrorism insurance coverage, and building owners and operators. Indirectly, it affects consumers and businesses that depend on available, affordable property insurance. The program is intended to protect the financial stability of the insurance industry during large-scale terrorist events that could otherwise cause massive losses.  **Key Provisions** While the full text is unavailable, reauthorization bills typically extend the program's duration, may adjust how losses are shared between insurers and the federal government, and may modify the insurance deductibles or coverage limits.
