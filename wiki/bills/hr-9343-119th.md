summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would amend federal postal law to establish formal rules and procedures governing how the U.S. Postal Service (USPS) implements centralized mail delivery for residential housing. Centralized delivery refers to systems where mail for multiple residential units is collected at a central location (such as a cluster mailbox or community mail center) rather than delivered to individual mailboxes at each unit. The bill would create standardized guidelines for when and how the USPS can require or implement this delivery method.  The legislation affects residential housing communities, individual mail recipients, and USPS operations. It would establish procedures that apply to both existing housing developments and new construction, potentially addressing disputes or inconsistencies in how different postal facilities implement centralized delivery policies. The bill aims to clarify the rules under which USPS can transition from door-to-door delivery to centralized delivery systems.  Key provisions would likely include procedures for implementing centralized delivery, requirements for notice to affected residents, and standards for placement and accessibility of centralized delivery facilities. The bill's purpose is to create consistency and transparency in postal delivery practices across residential communities nationwide by establishing explicit rules rather than leaving implementation decisions solely to individual postal facilities."
