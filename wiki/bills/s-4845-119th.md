summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would require explicit Congressional approval before troops or armed personnel can be stationed at polling places in the United States. Currently, the U.S. Code contains an exception that allows for military or armed forces presence at polling locations under certain circumstances. This legislation seeks to eliminate that automatic exception by making any such deployment subject to a mandatory Congressional vote and approval process, rather than allowing it to occur through executive action alone.  The bill would affect federal election administration and the respective authorities of Congress and the executive branch in matters of election security and military deployment. It impacts any scenario in which the federal government might consider deploying uniformed military or armed personnel near voting locations.  The key provision establishes that Congressional approval—rather than unilateral executive decision-making—is required before troops or armed forces can be present at polling places. This shifts decision-making authority over a specific national security matter from the executive branch to the legislative branch, requiring deliberation and voting by members of Congress before such a deployment could occur."
