summary: "# Bill Summary: Perinatal Care Alternative Payment Model Demonstration Project  This bill directs the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) Administrator to establish and operate a demonstration project testing alternative payment models for maternity care. The project would evaluate different ways to pay for services provided to pregnant individuals and those in the postpartum period, rather than relying solely on current payment structures. The demonstration aims to identify payment approaches that may improve care delivery, outcomes, or efficiency in the perinatal (pregnancy and early postpartum) care system.  The demonstration project would affect healthcare providers who deliver maternity care, pregnant individuals and postpartum patients covered by Medicare or Medicaid, and potentially CMS's payment systems. Participating providers might include hospitals, birthing centers, obstetric clinics, and other maternity care facilities that volunteer or are selected for the project.  Key aspects of the bill likely include establishing the scope and duration of the demonstration, defining which payment models will be tested, selecting participating providers and geographic areas, and requiring evaluation of results. The project would generate data on how different payment arrangements affect access, quality, and costs of perinatal care. The bill may also authorize related studies or require reports to Congress on project findings."
