summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would direct the removal of U.S. Armed Forces personnel from Venezuela who are not operating under congressional authorization. The proposal addresses the legal requirement that military deployments abroad generally require either explicit congressional approval or fall within existing authorized operations. By directing removal of unauthorized forces, the bill asserts congressional oversight of military deployment decisions.  The bill affects the Department of Defense and any U.S. military personnel currently stationed in or operating within Venezuela. It would require the executive branch to withdraw these forces unless Congress has specifically authorized their presence through legislation. The bill essentially enforces the constitutional principle that Congress holds the power to authorize military deployments, rather than allowing the executive branch to maintain armed forces in foreign countries indefinitely without legislative approval.  Key provisions would likely include a timeline for removal and possibly requirements for the Secretary of Defense to report to Congress on compliance. The bill does not appear to address military activities that Congress has already authorized, nor would it necessarily prevent future authorized operations in Venezuela if Congress chooses to approve them."
