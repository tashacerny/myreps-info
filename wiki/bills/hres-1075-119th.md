summary: "# Summary of House Resolution  This resolution is a procedural measure that sets rules for considering two separate bills in the House of Representatives. It does not itself enact policy, but rather establishes how and when these two bills will be debated and voted on.  The first bill addressed (H.R. 4626) would amend the Energy Policy and Conservation Act by requiring that any new or updated energy conservation standards set by the Secretary of Energy must be both technologically feasible and economically justified. This would add a requirement that the Department of Energy demonstrate products can actually meet proposed standards and that the benefits justify the costs before implementing new efficiency rules.  The second bill addressed (H.R. 4758) would repeal certain provisions of the Inflation Reduction Act (Public Law 117–169) that provide taxpayer subsidies for home electrification—likely referring to tax credits and rebates that help homeowners switch from fossil fuel-based heating and cooling systems to electric alternatives. The resolution allows both bills to proceed through the legislative process."
