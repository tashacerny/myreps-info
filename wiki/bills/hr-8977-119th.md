summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend the Federal Election Campaign Act of 1971 to establish a new felony offense related to ballot access documentation. Specifically, it would make it a felony for candidates seeking federal office, or their employees and agents, to knowingly and fraudulently sign any materials or documentation that are required to be filed in order to gain ballot access. The bill applies to elections for federal office, including positions such as President, U.S. Senator, and U.S. Representative.  The bill affects federal candidates, their campaign staff, and anyone acting on a candidate's behalf during the ballot access process. By creating a felony-level penalty for fraudulent documentation, the measure seeks to strengthen enforcement mechanisms around the requirements candidates must satisfy to appear on ballots in federal elections.  The phrase \"for other purposes\" in the title indicates the bill may contain additional provisions beyond what is described in the main title, though these are not specified in the available information."
