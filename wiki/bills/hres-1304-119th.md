summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill is a commemorative resolution that designates Memorial Day 2026 (May 25, 2026) as an occasion to recognize and draw attention to the lack of voting representation in Congress and statehood for residents of the District of Columbia. The resolution specifically highlights active duty servicemembers, National Guard members, reservists, veterans, and their families who live in D.C. and are affected by these limitations.  The bill does not create new laws or policies. Instead, it formally acknowledges that D.C. residents—including those with military service or military connections—cannot vote for members of Congress and do not have the ability to pursue statehood through standard processes. The resolution uses the symbolic platform of Memorial Day to bring attention to this civic issue affecting this population, drawing a connection between military service and the absence of full voting rights and self-governance for those serving in or residing in the nation's capital."
