summary: "# Summary  This bill invokes the War Powers Resolution to direct the President to remove U.S. Armed Forces from hostilities involving Iran. The War Powers Resolution, enacted in 1973, requires the President to notify Congress within 48 hours of committing armed forces to military action and limits such action to 60 days without congressional authorization, extendable to 90 days under specific conditions. Section 5(c) of the resolution allows Congress to direct the removal of armed forces by concurrent resolution.  The bill would apply to any ongoing or anticipated military operations involving Iran and would require the President to cease hostilities and withdraw U.S. forces engaged in such operations. This affects active-duty military personnel, Department of Defense operations in the Middle East, and U.S. foreign policy toward Iran. The practical effect would be to mandate disengagement from military confrontation with Iran absent further congressional action.  The bill represents a Congressional exercise of war powers authority. Passage would require support from both chambers of Congress and does not require presidential signature under the concurrent resolution process outlined in the War Powers Resolution."
