summary: "# Bill Summary  This resolution provides for a joint session of Congress at which the President will deliver a message to members of the House of Representatives and the Senate meeting together. The resolution establishes the procedural framework and logistics for this formal gathering, which is a standard congressional practice used for major presidential addresses such as the State of the Union address or other significant communications to the full legislature.  The resolution affects all members of Congress, as it requires their attendance or participation in the joint session. It typically specifies details such as the date, time, and location of the session, as well as any arrangements needed for seating, security, and the mechanics of the event. These resolutions are routine legislative measures necessary to coordinate the scheduling and conduct of joint sessions.  This type of resolution is largely procedural in nature and does not create new law or policy. Rather, it enables the constitutional function of the President to address Congress as a body and allows Congress to organize itself for this purpose."
