summary: "# Summary of Procedural Bill  This is a procedural resolution that sets the terms for House consideration of four separate legislative measures. It establishes the rules under which the House will debate and vote on these bills, including time limits, permitted amendments, and other procedural parameters.  The resolution addresses consideration of H.R. 4553, which would appropriate federal funding for energy and water development agencies for fiscal year 2026. It also addresses three joint resolutions (H.J. Res. 104, 105, and 106) that would invoke the Congressional Review Act—a mechanism allowing Congress to disapprove and overturn federal agency rules. These three resolutions target specific Bureau of Land Management (BLM) resource management decisions affecting public lands in Montana, North Dakota, and Alaska respectively.  This type of resolution does not change the substance of these bills; rather, it enables the House to proceed with their consideration by establishing the framework for debate. The actual merits and content of the energy and water appropriations bill and the three disapproval resolutions would be addressed during separate votes on those measures themselves."
