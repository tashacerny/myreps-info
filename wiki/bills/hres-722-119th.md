summary: "# Summary of H.R. 5371 Continuing Appropriations Bill  This bill provides for a continuing resolution (CR) that would extend federal government funding into fiscal year 2026. A continuing resolution is a temporary spending measure that allows federal agencies to operate when regular appropriations bills have not been enacted. This particular CR maintains funding at existing levels for government operations and programs, serving as a stopgap measure to prevent a government shutdown while Congress works on finalizing regular appropriations legislation.  The bill affects all federal agencies and departments, as well as the programs and services they provide to the public. Any agency or program reliant on annual appropriations would operate under this continuing resolution's funding levels rather than newly enacted budgets. The scope of impact is government-wide, affecting everything from defense and national security to social services, infrastructure, and administrative functions.  Additionally, the legislative vehicle includes consideration of House Resolution 719, which is a commemorative resolution honoring the life and legacy of Charles Charlie James Kirk. This is a procedural matter unrelated to the appropriations measures and allows the House to consider both pieces of legislation through a single rule governing floor debate and voting procedures."
