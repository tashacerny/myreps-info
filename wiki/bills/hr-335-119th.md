summary: "# Bill Summary: Repeal the NFA Act  **What the bill proposes:** This bill would repeal the National Firearms Act (NFA) of 1934, a federal law that regulates certain firearms and firearm accessories. The NFA currently imposes a $200 tax on and requires federal registration for items including silencers, short-barreled rifles and shotguns, machine guns manufactured after 1986, and other regulated weapons. Repealing the law would eliminate these federal requirements and the associated registration and taxation system.  **Who it affects:** The repeal would directly affect firearms manufacturers, dealers, and owners of NFA-regulated items. It would also impact the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives (ATF), which administers the current registration and tax system. Law enforcement agencies that rely on NFA regulations for certain investigative purposes could also be affected.  **Key provisions:** By repealing the entire NFA, the bill would eliminate the $200 tax on regulated items, remove the requirement to register firearms and accessories with federal authorities, and end the federal licensing process for these weapons. However, it would not necessarily affect state or local laws regulating the same items, which would remain in effect where they exist."
