summary: "# Summary: Bill to Repeal the Military Selective Service Act  This bill proposes to repeal the Military Selective Service Act, the federal law that establishes and maintains the Selective Service System. The Selective Service System currently requires all men ages 18–25 to register with the federal government within 30 days of turning 18. The system creates a database of potential military conscripts that the government could use to conduct a draft if Congress and the President authorized one during a national emergency. The bill would eliminate this registration requirement and the associated infrastructure.  The repeal would affect millions of young men currently required to register, as well as the Selective Service System's operations and staff. It would also affect the federal government's ability to conduct a rapid military draft in future emergencies, should policymakers determine one is necessary. Additionally, the bill could impact various federal benefits and programs that currently condition eligibility on Selective Service registration—including federal student aid, federal employment, job training programs, and state professional licenses.  The bill represents a significant change to longstanding federal law regarding military preparedness. Repealing the act would require action by both chambers of Congress and the President's signature."
