summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend the Consumer Financial Protection Act of 2010 to establish a mandatory minimum funding level for the Bureau of Consumer Financial Protection (CFPB). Currently, the CFPB receives funding through the Federal Reserve rather than through annual congressional appropriations, though Congress can cap that funding. This bill would set a floor—a guaranteed minimum amount—below which the agency's funding cannot fall.  The legislation would affect the CFPB's operations and budget stability, as well as the financial institutions the agency regulates and oversees. The bill impacts Congress's budgetary oversight process and could influence the agency's capacity to carry out its consumer protection activities, including supervision and enforcement actions related to mortgages, credit cards, student loans, and other consumer financial products.  Key provisions would include defining the specific compulsory funding floor amount and potentially addressing how this minimum interacts with existing funding mechanisms and congressional budget authority. The bill may also include clarifying language about the CFPB's funding structure and any related procedural or administrative changes necessary to implement the funding floor."
