summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would require the U.S. Secretary of Health and Human Services to develop and issue guidance to states regarding which tests for screening and early detection of preeclampsia can be covered under Medicaid and the Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP). Preeclampsia is a pregnancy-related condition characterized by high blood pressure and protein in urine that can pose serious health risks to pregnant women and developing fetuses. The bill aims to clarify which diagnostic tests states should make available to pregnant individuals enrolled in these federal-state insurance programs.  The bill would affect pregnant women and pregnant individuals covered by Medicaid or CHIP, as well as state Medicaid and CHIP programs responsible for determining which medical services to cover. By establishing federal guidance on preeclampsia screening and detection tests, the bill seeks to create more consistent standards across states regarding which tests are eligible for coverage, potentially improving access to early detection services for this population.  The key provision is the directive to the HHS Secretary to issue guidance identifying covered tests for preeclampsia screening and early detection. This guidance would serve as a reference for states as they establish or review their Medicaid and CHIP coverage policies related to these services."
