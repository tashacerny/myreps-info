summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend the Child Nutrition Act of 1966 by adding new limits on juice purchases within the Special Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women, Infants, and Children (WIC). Specifically, it would establish maximum monthly allowances for juice that WIC participants can purchase using program benefits. The bill affects WIC beneficiaries—primarily low-income pregnant women, postpartum women, breastfeeding women, infants, and children up to age five—as well as state WIC programs that administer these benefits.  The key provision is the creation of a cap on monthly juice allowances under WIC. This would limit the quantity or frequency of juice purchases covered by the program, though the specific allowance amounts are not detailed in the available information. The change would likely require state WIC programs to implement new eligibility or purchase guidelines at retail locations where WIC benefits are redeemed."
