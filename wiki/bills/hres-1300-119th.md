summary: "# Summary of Consideration Resolution  This resolution allows the House of Representatives to consider three separate bills. The resolution itself is procedural and does not create policy; rather, it sets the terms under which the House will debate and vote on these three measures.  The three bills covered are: (1) H.R. 1041, which would prohibit the Veterans Affairs Department from sharing certain information with the Department of Justice's national instant criminal background check system; (2) H.R. 6047, which would increase disability compensation and survivor benefits paid by the Veterans Affairs Department; and (3) H.R. 1329, which would allow the Smithsonian American Women's History Museum to be built in the National Mall Reserve area. The resolution also waives a procedural requirement under House Rule XIII regarding consideration of certain other resolutions.  This type of resolution is a standard legislative procedure used to establish debate parameters and floor consideration rules for multiple bills simultaneously."
