summary: "# Bill Summary: Nuclear Forensics and Attribution Realignment  This bill proposes to transfer responsibility for nuclear forensics and attribution activities from the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) to the National Nuclear Security Administration (NNSA), which operates under the Department of Energy. Nuclear forensics involves analyzing nuclear or radiological materials to determine their origin, history, and characteristics, while attribution refers to identifying the source of such materials. The bill would consolidate these functions under a single federal agency rather than splitting them between two departments.  The change would primarily affect federal agencies involved in national security and nuclear nonproliferation efforts, as well as the organizational structure and funding mechanisms for these technical activities. The NNSA already manages significant nuclear security programs, including nuclear weapons stewardship and nonproliferation initiatives, so this realignment would place related forensic and attribution work within the same organization.  Key provisions would involve the transfer of relevant personnel, equipment, budgets, and responsibilities from DHS to NNSA. The stated purpose of consolidating these functions is likely to improve coordination and efficiency in conducting nuclear forensics and attribution work, though the bill title does not specify additional operational details or implementation timelines."
