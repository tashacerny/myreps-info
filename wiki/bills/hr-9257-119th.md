summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would amend three federal laws—the Employee Retirement Income Security Act (ERISA), the Public Health Service Act, and the Internal Revenue Code—to require health plans and insurers to cover certain preventive health visits at no cost to patients. Specifically, the legislation would mandate that group health plans and both group and individual health insurance policies cover three primary care visits and three behavioral health care visits annually without any cost-sharing requirements such as copayments, coinsurance, or deductibles.  The bill would affect health insurers, group health plans (both self-insured and fully-insured), and individuals who obtain health insurance coverage. Employers offering group health plans would need to ensure compliance with the new coverage requirements, as would health insurance companies offering coverage in both the group and individual markets.  The key provision establishes that these six annual visits—three for primary care and three for behavioral health—must be provided with zero cost-sharing. This means beneficiaries would have access to these services without financial barriers at the point of care, while insurers and plans would be required to cover them in full without patient cost-sharing obligations."
