summary: "# Summary  This joint resolution seeks to disapprove a rule issued by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) regarding reporting requirements for brokers who facilitate digital asset (cryptocurrency) transactions. Under the Congressional Review Act (a procedure outlined in Chapter 8 of Title 5, U.S. Code), Congress can vote to reject federal agency rules within a specified timeframe. If passed, this resolution would invalidate the IRS rule on digital asset reporting.  # Key Effects  The rule being disapproved would have required brokers that regularly provide services for digital asset sales to report gross proceeds from those transactions. If Congress approves this resolution, the reporting rule would be repealed and could not be reissued without new legislation. The resolution would primarily affect cryptocurrency exchanges, platforms, and other financial intermediaries that facilitate digital asset transactions, as well as the tax reporting obligations they would face.  # Process  This is a Congressional Review Act resolution, which follows a streamlined legislative process with limited debate and amendment options. Passage requires a simple majority in both chambers. The specific details of what the IRS rule would have required and its effective date would be found in the full legislative text."
