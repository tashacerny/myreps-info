summary: "# Summary of Proposed Rule Disapproval Procedures  This bill is a procedural measure that establishes the terms for House consideration of three separate congressional disapproval resolutions. Under the Congressional Review Act (Chapter 8 of Title 5, U.S. Code), Congress can vote to overturn federal agency rules within a specified timeframe. This bill sets the rules and procedures for debating and voting on three such resolutions targeting recent regulations from the Department of Energy, Environmental Protection Agency, and Bureau of Ocean Energy Management.  The three regulations targeted are: (1) a Department of Energy rule on appliance certification, labeling, and enforcement requirements; (2) an Environmental Protection Agency rule establishing emission standards for hazardous air pollutants in rubber tire manufacturing; and (3) a Bureau of Ocean Energy Management rule on protection of marine archaeological resources. If any of these disapproval resolutions passes both chambers and is signed by the President, the corresponding agency rule would be invalidated and the agency would be prohibited from issuing substantially similar rules without explicit congressional authorization.  This bill affects appliance manufacturers and retailers, tire manufacturers, and companies engaged in offshore energy development and marine exploration, along with the relevant federal agencies. Consumers could also be indirectly affected depending on the outcome, as these rules address energy efficiency standards, air quality, and environmental protection."
