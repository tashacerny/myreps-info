summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would require the Secretary of Homeland Security to designate Haiti for Temporary Protected Status (TPS). TPS is a federal immigration program that allows nationals of designated countries experiencing ongoing armed conflict, environmental disaster, or other extraordinary conditions to live and work temporarily in the United States without fear of deportation. If enacted, this designation would apply to Haitian nationals currently in the United States.  The bill would affect Haitian nationals who are present in the U.S., granting them legal authorization to remain and work during the period of designation. It would also impact the Department of Homeland Security, which would be responsible for implementing the program, including processing applications, issuing work permits, and managing the administrative requirements of the TPS designation.  Key provisions would include the mandatory nature of the designation—rather than leaving it to the Secretary's discretion, the bill would require the designation to be made. The specific duration of the designation and any renewal provisions would depend on the full text, though TPS designations typically last 6 to 18 months and can be extended if conditions in the designated country persist."
