summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would amend the Bank Secrecy Act to extend federal anti-money laundering (AML) and anti-fraud regulations to operators of digital asset kiosks—machines that allow users to buy, sell, or exchange cryptocurrencies and other digital assets. Currently, these kiosk operators operate with limited federal oversight compared to traditional financial institutions. The bill would require kiosk operators to register with federal authorities and comply with existing AML requirements, similar to obligations already placed on banks and money services businesses.  The primary effects would be on digital asset kiosk operators and their customers. Operators would need to implement compliance programs, maintain records of transactions, report suspicious activity, and conduct customer due diligence. Customers using these kiosks would likely face increased verification requirements and reporting of larger transactions. The bill aims to close regulatory gaps that could be exploited for money laundering, terrorist financing, or fraud.  Key provisions would likely include registration requirements for kiosk operators, mandatory reporting of suspicious transactions, customer identification and verification procedures, and record-keeping obligations. The bill's stated purpose is to bring digital asset kiosks under the same regulatory framework as other money services businesses to enhance financial security and law enforcement capabilities."
