summary: "# Summary of the Conrad State 30 and Physician Access Reauthorization Act  This bill reauthorizes the Conrad State 30 program, a federal initiative that allows states to employ foreign-trained physicians on temporary visas. Under current law, the program permits up to 30 foreign medical graduates per state per fiscal year to work in the United States, typically in underserved areas. The bill extends this program's authorization, which would otherwise expire, and may modify its scope or provisions. The reauthorization affects foreign-trained doctors seeking to practice in the U.S., state governments administering the program, and healthcare facilities—particularly in rural and medically underserved communities that often struggle to recruit physicians.  Key provisions likely include extending the program's duration, potentially adjusting the number of visa allocations available, and clarifying eligibility requirements for participating states and physicians. The bill may also refine which practice settings qualify (such as rural areas, primary care specialties, or hospitals serving low-income populations) and specify oversight requirements. These changes aim to balance the program's role in addressing physician workforce shortages in underserved regions while maintaining regulatory standards for foreign medical graduate training and practice."
