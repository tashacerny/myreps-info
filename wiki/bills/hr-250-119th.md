summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill directs the Joint Committee on the Library to commission and acquire a statue of Benjamin Franklin for display in the Capitol building. The Joint Committee on the Library is a bicameral congressional committee responsible for overseeing the Library of Congress and managing artwork and monuments in the Capitol. The bill affects the committee's budget and workload by assigning it the specific task of procuring this statue.  The key provision is the mandate for the Joint Committee to obtain a Benjamin Franklin statue and arrange for its placement within the Capitol. This would add to the existing collection of statuary in the building, which currently includes figures from American history. The bill does not specify details about the statue's design, size, location within the Capitol, or funding mechanism based on the title alone, though such details would typically be included in the full legislative text."
