summary: "# Bill Summary: Clean Air Act Amendment for Marginal Wells  **What the bill proposes:** This bill would amend the Clean Air Act to create an exemption for \"marginal wells\" from certain federal air quality standards and performance requirements. Marginal wells are typically oil and gas wells that produce relatively small quantities of resources and may be economically sensitive to regulatory costs. The bill aims to reduce regulatory compliance burdens on this category of wells.  **Who it affects:** The primary beneficiaries would be operators of marginal oil and gas wells, which tend to be smaller independent producers rather than large energy companies. The bill could also indirectly affect workers in these operations and communities near marginal well sites. Environmental regulators at the federal and state levels would implement the exemption.  **Key provisions:** While the full text is unavailable, the bill's scope indicates it would likely: - Define which wells qualify as \"marginal\" for purposes of the exemption - Specify which Clean Air Act standards and performance requirements would no longer apply to these wells - Potentially establish procedures for wells to receive this exemption status  The phrase \"and for other purposes\" suggests the bill may contain additional provisions not captured in the main title."
