summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill directs two federal health agencies to conduct evaluations related to hormone changes during perimenopause (the transition period leading to menopause) in women. Specifically, it tasks the Director of the National Institutes of Health with evaluating hormone variability during this life stage, while the Secretary of Health and Human Services is directed to evaluate hormone testing methods and practices. The bill essentially calls for federal research and review of how hormonal fluctuations occur during perimenopause and how effectively current testing approaches measure these changes.  The bill would affect women experiencing or at risk for perimenopause, as well as healthcare providers who diagnose and treat perimenopause-related symptoms. It may also impact medical device and diagnostic testing companies that develop hormone tests. By directing these evaluations, the bill aims to improve scientific understanding of perimenopause and potentially inform better diagnostic and treatment approaches for this common life transition.  Key provisions include requiring the NIH to study hormone variability patterns during perimenopause and requiring the HHS to assess the reliability, accessibility, and clinical utility of existing hormone testing methods for diagnosing perimenopause. The bill does not appear to authorize new funding or programs directly, but rather directs existing agencies to evaluate these specific health areas and likely report findings to Congress."
