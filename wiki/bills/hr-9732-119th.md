summary: "# Multi-Class Stock Company Voting Transparency Act — Summary  **What the Bill Proposes**  Based on the title, this bill would establish transparency requirements for companies that have multiple classes of stock with different voting rights. Multi-class stock structures allow some shareholders (typically founders or insiders) to maintain voting control even with smaller ownership stakes, while other shareholders have reduced voting power per share. This bill would likely require such companies to disclose information about their voting structures, share classes, and how voting power is distributed among shareholders.  **Who It Affects**  The bill would primarily affect publicly traded companies that use multi-class stock structures, as well as their shareholders and investors. This includes companies in technology, media, and other sectors where founders have used such structures to retain control. Investors, financial analysts, and regulators would also be affected as they would gain access to required disclosures.  **Key Provisions**  While the full text is unavailable, the bill's likely provisions would include: mandatory disclosure of voting rights attached to each stock class; information about the concentration of voting power; and possibly requirements for how companies communicate these structures to shareholders. The stated purpose would be to help investors make informed decisions by clearly understanding voting dynamics before purchasing shares."
