summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend federal bankruptcy law (Title 11 of the United States Code) to address how oil, gas, and coal companies handle environmental reclamation obligations when they file for bankruptcy. The measure aims to ensure that companies in these extractive industries continue to meet their environmental cleanup and restoration responsibilities even while proceeding through bankruptcy proceedings, rather than potentially abandoning or delaying such obligations.  The bill would affect oil, gas, and coal companies that become debtors in bankruptcy, as well as relevant federal and state environmental agencies responsible for overseeing reclamation. Key provisions would likely establish requirements that environmental reclamation duties—such as site cleanup, land restoration, and pollution remediation—be treated as priority obligations in bankruptcy proceedings, potentially limiting companies' ability to shed these liabilities through bankruptcy reorganization or liquidation.  The underlying concern addressed by this bill is that companies in extractive industries may use bankruptcy to avoid funding environmental cleanup costs, leaving sites contaminated and shifting financial responsibility to governments or the public. By modifying bankruptcy law, the bill seeks to preserve the connection between companies and their environmental obligations regardless of their financial status."
