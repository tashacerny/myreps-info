The bill aims to establish regulatory and enforcement measures to reduce the availability and use of xylazine outside of legitimate medical and veterinary contexts. Xylazine is not currently a scheduled controlled substance under federal law, though it has been detected in heroin, fentanyl, and other street drugs, often without users' knowledge.  **Key Provisions and Effects**  The bill likely includes measures such as enhancing monitoring and enforcement of xylazine distribution, increasing penalties for unlawful manufacturing or possession, and potentially scheduling xylazine as a controlled substance. It may also authorize funding for research on xylazine's health effects, treatment options, and detection methods. The legislation would primarily affect law enforcement agencies, pharmaceutical distributors, healthcare providers, and individuals involved in illicit drug manufacturing and distribution.  **Who It Affects**  This bill impacts public health and law enforcement officials, medical and veterinary professionals who use xylazine legitimately, and individuals struggling with substance use disorder—particularly those exposed to contaminated drug supplies.
