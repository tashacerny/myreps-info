summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill uses the Congressional Review Act (CRA) to disapprove a Department of Energy rule regarding energy conservation standards for walk-in coolers and walk-in freezers. Under the CRA process, Congress can vote to reject a federal agency rule within 60 legislative days of its submission. If passed, this disapproval would prevent the rule from taking effect and prohibit the Department of Energy from issuing substantially similar regulations without new congressional authorization.  The rule being challenged establishes energy efficiency requirements for commercial walk-in cooling and freezing equipment—appliances commonly used by restaurants, grocery stores, warehouses, and food service providers. The disapproval would affect manufacturers of this equipment and businesses that use walk-in coolers and freezers, as they would no longer be subject to the new federal efficiency standards being proposed by the Department of Energy.  The bill does not modify the rule itself or propose alternative standards; it simply seeks to reject it entirely. If enacted, the previous energy conservation standards for this equipment would remain in effect unless and until the Department of Energy pursues a different regulatory approach with congressional support."
