summary: "# Summary: Bonus Depreciation for Long-Term Residential Rental Housing  This bill would amend federal tax law to allow owners of long-term residential rental housing to claim bonus depreciation. Bonus depreciation is a tax provision that permits businesses to deduct a larger portion of an asset's cost in the year it is placed in service, rather than spreading the deduction over the asset's useful life. The bill would extend this existing tax benefit—currently available for certain business property and equipment—to residential rental properties held for long-term use.  The provision would primarily affect individual and corporate owners of rental housing. By accelerating depreciation deductions, the bill would reduce taxable income for qualifying property owners in earlier years, lowering their federal tax liability during that period. The specific details regarding which properties qualify (such as how \"long-term\" is defined and any restrictions on property type or owner size) would depend on the full legislative text.  The bill represents a tax incentive approach intended to encourage investment in the residential rental housing stock. Like other tax expenditures, it would reduce federal revenue relative to current law, though the magnitude would depend on implementation details and how many property owners claim the benefit."
