summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would require the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) to follow specific procedures before constructing, acquiring, renovating, or operating any new processing site or detention center. Under the proposed requirements, DHS must provide a mechanism for the public to submit comments on such activities, obtain a signed written agreement with relevant state and local officials, and give Congress advance notice before proceeding.  The bill affects DHS operations related to immigration processing and detention, as well as state and local governments in jurisdictions where such facilities would be located. It also involves Congress, which would receive notification of planned activities. The measure would apply to new facilities or significant renovations of existing ones, rather than routine operations of established centers.  The key provisions include: mandatory public comment opportunities; written agreements with state and local authorities as a condition of facility development; and advance congressional notification. Together, these requirements are intended to increase transparency and coordination between federal agencies, state and local governments, and the public regarding the location and operation of immigration-related facilities."
