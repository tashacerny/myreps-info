summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to prioritize local government units in the distribution of Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant (Byrne-JAG) program funding based on whether they submit gang affiliation information to the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI). Byrne-JAG is a federal grant program that provides funding to state, local, and tribal law enforcement agencies for criminal justice initiatives, including crime prevention, prosecution, and officer training. The bill would create a preference or weighting system favoring grant applicants that report gang-related data to federal authorities.  The legislation would directly affect local police departments, sheriffs' offices, and other law enforcement agencies that apply for Byrne-JAG funding. It would incentivize these agencies to collect and report gang membership or affiliation data to the FBI, potentially as part of the National Crime Information Center (NCIC) or similar federal databases. Agencies that participate in this information-sharing would receive enhanced consideration in the competitive grant process.  The key provision establishes that gang affiliation reporting would be a factor in Byrne-JAG funding decisions, creating a financial incentive for local governments to develop or enhance gang intelligence systems and share that data federally. The bill aims to improve information sharing about gang-related criminal activity across law enforcement jurisdictions while leveraging grant funding as a policy tool to encourage participation."
