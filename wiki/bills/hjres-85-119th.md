summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would allow Congress to formally disapprove a proposed foreign military sale to Israel involving certain defense articles and services. Under the Arms Export Control Act, Congress has the authority to review and potentially block foreign military sales by the executive branch. This bill is a mechanism for exercising that congressional review power in this specific case.  The bill affects the executive branch's ability to proceed with the proposed sale and impacts the defense relationship between the United States and Israel. The specific defense articles and services involved are referenced but not detailed in the title provided. If passed, the bill would prevent the proposed sale from moving forward unless the executive branch could overcome the congressional disapproval.  This type of legislation represents Congress's constitutional role in overseeing foreign policy and military assistance. Foreign military sales require advance notification to Congress, which then has a set period to pass a disapproval resolution if it chooses to block the transaction."
