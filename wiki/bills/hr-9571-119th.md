summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would establish a minimum wage requirement for workers employed by federal contractors—companies that have contracts to provide goods or services to the federal government. Currently, federal contractors must comply with the prevailing wage requirements under the Davis-Bacon Act (for construction) and Service Contract Act (for service work), but there is no single, unified minimum wage floor across all federal contracting. This bill would set a minimum hourly wage that applies uniformly to federal contractor employees.  The legislation would directly affect federal contractors and their employees. Any private company or organization holding a federal contract would need to pay workers at least the established minimum wage. This could impact millions of workers across construction, service, manufacturing, and other sectors where federal contracting occurs, as well as the businesses employing them.  Key provisions would likely include defining which workers and contract types fall under the requirement, specifying the minimum wage amount, and establishing enforcement mechanisms to ensure compliance.
