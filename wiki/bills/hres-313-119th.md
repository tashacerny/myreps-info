summary: "# Summary of H. Con. Res. 14 (Senate Amendment)  This measure is a procedural resolution that governs how Congress will consider the Senate's amendments to the concurrent resolution establishing the federal budget for fiscal year 2025, along with budgetary projections through 2034. Concurrent resolutions on the budget do not require the President's signature and serve as binding internal guidelines for Congress on spending and revenue levels. This specific resolution determines the parliamentary process and rules under which the House will debate and vote on the Senate's proposed changes to the budget framework.  The resolution affects all federal agencies, programs, and spending priorities covered by the budget, as well as members of Congress who must operate within the established budgetary constraints. It also indirectly affects taxpayers and beneficiaries of federal programs, since the budget sets spending and revenue levels across the government.  Key provisions typically found in such procedural measures include the amount of debate time allowed, which amendments (if any) may be offered, and the voting procedures the House will follow. The resolution essentially sets the \"rules of the road\" for considering the budget rather than determining specific spending or policy outcomes itself. The substance of actual spending levels and priorities would be contained in the underlying budget resolution and subsequent appropriations bills."
