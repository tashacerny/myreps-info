summary: "# Summary of H.Res. [Bill Number]  This is a procedural resolution that allows the House of Representatives to consider three separate bills. It does not itself create new policy, but rather sets the terms under which the House will debate and vote on these three measures. The resolution establishes the rules, timeline, and amendment procedures for consideration of each bill on the House floor.  The three bills covered are: (1) **H.R. 6945**, which would clarify that states can use certain federal funds (under Social Security Act Title IV Part A) to support pregnancy centers; (2) **H.R. 6359**, which would require colleges and universities to provide information to pregnant students about their rights, accommodations, and available resources; and (3) **H.J.Res. 140**, which would disapprove a Bureau of Land Management rule that withdrew federal lands in Cook, Lake, and Saint Louis Counties, Minnesota from certain uses.  This resolution affects members of Congress and their ability to debate these bills, as well as the institutions and populations addressed by the underlying measures—state governments and pregnancy centers under H.R. 6945; colleges and universities and their pregnant students under H.R. 6359; and federal land management policy in Minnesota under H.J.Res. 140. The resolution itself does not enact any substantive changes; it only enables House consideration of the three bills."
