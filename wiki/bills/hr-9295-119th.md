summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes amendments to federal law governing awards of fees and expenses in two contexts: agency adjudications (proceedings conducted by federal agencies) and cases under chapter 161 of title 28 (which covers Equal Access to Justice Act claims). The bill would establish maximum caps on the amount of fees and other expenses that can be awarded to prevailing parties in these proceedings.  The legislation would affect parties involved in federal agency disputes and litigation against the federal government, including private individuals, small businesses, nonprofits, and advocacy organizations that challenge agency actions and prevail. It would also impact federal agencies and the Department of Justice, which defend agency actions in these proceedings.  Key provisions would include setting monetary limits on attorney fees, expert witness fees, and other expenses awarded in agency adjudications and Equal Access to Justice Act cases. By capping recoverable expenses, the bill would reduce the financial exposure federal agencies face when they lose disputes and lose cases, while potentially affecting the ability of private parties to recover full costs when they successfully challenge government action."
