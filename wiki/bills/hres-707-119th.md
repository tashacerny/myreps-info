summary: "# Summary of House Resolution  This resolution provides for floor consideration of seven separate bills. Four bills address criminal justice and law enforcement policy in the District of Columbia: H.R. 4922 would limit \"youth offender\" status to individuals 18 and younger, require the D.C. Attorney General to publish juvenile crime statistics online, and restrict the D.C. Council's authority to modify criminal sentences; H.R. 5143 would establish standards for police vehicular pursuits; H.R. 5140 would lower the age at which juveniles can be tried as adults in D.C. from the current threshold to 14 years old; and H.R. 5125 would eliminate the D.C. Judicial Nomination Commission. These bills would affect D.C.'s criminal justice system, law enforcement practices, and judicial appointment processes.  The remaining three bills address energy and infrastructure policy affecting the broader United States. H.R. 1047 would reform the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission's process for approving interconnection projects to the electrical grid. H.R. 3015 would reestablish the National Coal Council within the Department of Energy to advise the Secretary on coal-related matters. H.R. 3062 would create a streamlined, uniform authorization process for international border-crossing facilities for oil, natural gas, and electricity transmission. Together, these bills would affect energy project development, federal energy advisory structures, and cross-border energy infrastructure."
