The transfer would shift control of this property from the U.S. Forest Service to local county authority.  **Who It Affects**  The transfer would primarily affect Gila County, Arizona, which would gain ownership and management responsibility for the property. It would also impact the U.S. Forest Service, which currently operates the site, and potentially the local community that uses or relies on services provided at the administrative site.  **Key Provisions**  The bill requires the Secretary of Agriculture to execute the conveyance, though the specific terms—such as any conditions on the transfer, timeline for completion, or financial arrangements—cannot be detailed without access to the full legislative text.
