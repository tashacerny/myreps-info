summary: "# Summary: Washington's Trail—1753 National Historic Trail Feasibility Study Act of 2026  This bill would authorize a feasibility study to examine whether a historic trail associated with George Washington's 1753 expedition should be designated as a National Historic Trail. The study would assess the historical significance of the route, its current condition, and the practicality of establishing it as a formally recognized national trail under federal management or protection.  The feasibility study would likely involve the National Park Service evaluating factors such as the trail's historical authenticity, existing infrastructure, accessibility, and the interest and capacity of states, local governments, and private landowners to support such a designation. The bill would affect communities along the historic route, potential land managers, and organizations involved in cultural and outdoor heritage preservation.  If the study recommends designation and Congress approves it, a National Historic Trail would provide resources for preservation, interpretation, and public access to the route. This designation would not typically require federal acquisition of land but would establish standards for management and funding mechanisms for trail development and historical education."
