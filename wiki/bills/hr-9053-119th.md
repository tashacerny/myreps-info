The bill would primarily affect U.S. agricultural producers, particularly those in states dependent on water from shared water sources governed by the 1944 treaty (primarily in the Southwest). It would also impact federal water management agencies responsible for implementing treaty provisions. Key provisions likely include: (1) mechanisms or requirements to secure reliable water delivery from Mexico as specified in the treaty; (2) a compensation program or fund to reimburse farmers and agricultural businesses for economic damages caused by water shortfalls; and (3) possibly provisions for dispute resolution or enforcement of treaty obligations.
