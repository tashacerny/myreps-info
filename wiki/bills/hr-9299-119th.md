summary: "# Summary of eDNA Sport Fish Survey Grant Program Bill  This bill directs the Secretary of the Interior to establish a grant program that awards funding to eligible entities for developing and implementing programs that use eDNA (environmental DNA) monitoring techniques in sport fish surveys. eDNA monitoring is a scientific method that detects fish species and populations by analyzing genetic material in water samples, offering an alternative or complementary approach to traditional survey methods. The program would support both the initial development of eDNA-based programs and their ongoing use in fish survey activities.  The bill would primarily affect state and local fish and wildlife agencies, universities, research institutions, and other organizations involved in fish population monitoring and management. These entities would be eligible to apply for grants to adopt or expand eDNA monitoring as part of their sport fish survey operations. The funding could support training, equipment, technology infrastructure, and operational costs associated with implementing these monitoring techniques.  While the full text is not available, the bill's core purpose is to modernize fish survey methods by providing financial support for eDNA technology adoption. This approach may enhance the accuracy, efficiency, or comprehensiveness of sport fish population assessments and help wildlife managers make more informed decisions about fish conservation and sport fisheries management."
