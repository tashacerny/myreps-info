The bill primarily affects patient advocacy groups, research organizations, and medical institutions focused on pediatric cancers. By establishing an official awareness day, the measure seeks to draw attention to DIPG specifically and pediatric cancers more broadly. The designation does not appear to include direct funding or regulatory changes, but rather serves as a symbolic congressional statement of support for disease awareness and research efforts.  This type of bill is a commemorative resolution that does not create new laws or programs, but rather designates a specific date to recognize a cause and encourage public engagement and research focus on childhood cancer treatment and prevention."
