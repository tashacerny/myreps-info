summary: "# Bill Summary: Puerto Rico Political Status Determination  **What the Bill Proposes**  This bill would establish a process allowing the people of Puerto Rico to determine the island's political status. Based on the title, the legislation creates a mechanism—likely through a referendum or similar democratic procedure—for Puerto Rico residents to choose among different political options for the territory's future relationship with the United States.  **Who It Affects**  The bill directly affects Puerto Rico's approximately 3.2 million residents, who would participate in the status determination process. It could also affect the federal government and Congress, as any change to Puerto Rico's status would require federal action. The outcome could influence matters under federal jurisdiction, including Puerto Rico's representation in Congress, taxation, and governance structure.  **Key Context**  Puerto Rico is an unincorporated territory of the United States. Residents are U.S. citizens but cannot vote in presidential elections and have limited congressional representation. Political status options typically discussed include statehood, independence, or an enhanced commonwealth arrangement. This bill would give Puerto Rico residents a formal, federally-supported mechanism to express their preference on the territory's future political status, though the specific voting procedures, options presented, and implementation requirements would depend on the bill's detailed provisions."
