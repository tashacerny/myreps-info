summary: "# Summary of House Consideration Resolution  This resolution is a procedural measure that sets the terms for House floor consideration of three separate bills. It establishes debate schedules and voting procedures for bills addressing firearms regulations, marine sanctuary operations, and energy resource supply.  The three bills covered are: (1) H.R. 2189, which would update federal firearms laws to reflect technological advances and the availability of less-lethal weapons; (2) H.R. 261, which would streamline permitting requirements for undersea fiber optic cable installation and maintenance in national marine sanctuaries by allowing previously authorized federal or state activities to proceed without additional sanctuary-specific authorization; and (3) H.R. 3617, which would modify the Department of Energy Organization Act to enhance the federal government's ability to secure supplies of critical minerals and materials needed for energy production.  As a procedural resolution, this measure does not enact policy itself. Rather, it determines how the House will debate and vote on these three bills. The resolution affects members of Congress and any individuals or industries whose operations or rights would be impacted by the underlying bills, including firearms manufacturers and users, telecommunications companies, marine sanctuary stakeholders, and energy sector participants."
