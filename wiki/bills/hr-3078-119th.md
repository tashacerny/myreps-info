summary: "# Summary  This bill directs the Secretary of Energy to coordinate with other federal officials to research and develop new technologies capable of detecting fentanyl vapor or particles. The focus is on creating rapid screening methods for use in specific high-risk locations, particularly the U.S. mail system, prisons, and borders. The bill frames this as a research and development initiative aimed at addressing fentanyl detection challenges in these settings.  # Key Provisions and Effects  The bill centers on a research mandate requiring the Department of Energy to work with relevant federal agencies to test and evaluate detection technologies. These efforts would support screening operations in multiple contexts: postal facilities (to identify contaminated mail), correctional institutions (to prevent drug smuggling into prisons), border crossings (for drug interdiction), and other related applications. The bill authorizes the Secretary of Energy to lead this interagency coordination effort.  # Who Is Affected  This proposal primarily affects federal agencies involved in mail screening, border security, and corrections, as well as the Department of Energy's research operations. It may also indirectly affect mail carriers, border security personnel, and prison staff who would use any resulting detection technologies. The research outcomes could potentially influence screening procedures across multiple federal operations."
