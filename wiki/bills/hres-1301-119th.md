summary: "# Bill Summary: Mental Health Awareness in Agriculture Day  This bill proposes to officially designate May 29, 2026, as \"Mental Health Awareness in Agriculture Day.\" The designation is intended to bring national attention to mental health issues affecting the agricultural industry and the people who work in farming, ranching, and related agricultural occupations. The bill aims to support efforts to raise public awareness and reduce the stigma associated with mental illness within agricultural communities.  The designation would primarily affect agricultural workers, farmers, ranchers, and related industry professionals, as well as organizations that work on mental health issues in rural and agricultural sectors. The bill itself does not appear to establish new programs, funding, or requirements; rather, it creates an official recognition date to encourage awareness-raising activities and advocacy.  The key purpose is to promote dialogue and visibility around mental health challenges in agriculture—a sector where workers face unique occupational stressors—and to support broader efforts to destigmatize mental illness and encourage individuals to seek help when needed."
