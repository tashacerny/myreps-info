summary: "# Gas Tax Suspension Act – Summary  **What the Bill Proposes**  Based on the title, this bill would suspend the federal excise tax on gasoline. The federal gas tax is currently 18.4 cents per gallon and funds the Highway Trust Fund, which finances highway and bridge construction and maintenance projects nationwide. A suspension would temporarily eliminate or pause this tax.  **Who It Affects**  The suspension would directly affect consumers at the pump, potentially lowering gas prices. It would also impact states and the federal government, since the Highway Trust Fund would lose revenue normally dedicated to transportation infrastructure projects. Trucking companies, delivery services, and other businesses that consume significant fuel would also be affected.  **Key Provisions**  Without access to the full text, the main provision would be the suspension mechanism itself—typically this would specify the duration of the suspension (days, months, or until a certain date) and how long the tax would remain paused. The bill would likely need to address how transportation infrastructure projects would be funded during the suspension period, either through alternative funding sources or by allowing the Highway Trust Fund to draw from general revenues or reserves."
