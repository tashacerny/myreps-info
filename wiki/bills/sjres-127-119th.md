summary: "# Bill Summary  This joint resolution seeks to block a rule issued by the Bureau of Consumer Financial Protection (CFPB) that would withdraw a previously existing rule on fair credit reporting and file disclosure. Under a federal law called the Congressional Review Act (Chapter 8 of Title 5, U.S. Code), Congress can disapprove of certain agency regulations within a specified timeframe. This resolution uses that mechanism to prevent the CFPB's withdrawal rule from taking effect.  The bill directly affects consumers who rely on credit reporting protections and the credit reporting industry. If passed, it would preserve the existing \"Fair Credit Reporting; File Disclosure\" rule, preventing the CFPB from eliminating it. The practical effect would be to maintain current regulatory requirements related to how credit reporting agencies must disclose consumer credit files and related information.  The resolution represents a formal congressional check on regulatory action. It requires only a simple majority vote in both chambers and does not require presidential signature. If approved, it would overturn the CFPB's decision to withdraw the rule, effectively mandating that the regulation remain in place."
