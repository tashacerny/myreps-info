summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would modify the Internal Revenue Code to create an alternative tax structure for mobile mounted concrete boom pumps. Rather than paying the standard federal excise tax on taxable fuels (gasoline and diesel), operators of these specialized pieces of concrete construction equipment would instead pay a mileage-based user fee. The bill represents a shift from fuel-consumption-based taxation to distance-traveled-based taxation for this specific equipment category.  The measure would affect businesses and contractors that own or operate mobile mounted concrete boom pumps—large machinery used to transport and distribute concrete at construction sites. These operators would be subject to the new mileage fee structure in place of the fuel tax they currently pay on these vehicles.  Key provisions would establish the mileage-based fee system as an alternative to existing fuel taxes for this equipment, though without access to the full bill text, specific details regarding fee rates, calculation methods, collection procedures, and effective dates cannot be confirmed from the title alone."
