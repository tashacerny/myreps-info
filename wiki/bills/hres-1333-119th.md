summary: "# Bill Summary: Rule for Consideration of Four Separate Bills  This is a procedural rule that establishes the terms for House floor consideration of four unrelated bills. The rule allows the House to debate and vote on these bills under specified parameters. The four bills covered are: (1) an appropriations bill funding Agriculture, Rural Development, FDA, and related agencies for fiscal year 2027; (2) a bill to enforce compliance with child care funding requirements by withholding federal funds from states that do not meet standards; (3) a bill requiring the Department of Education to use fraud detection systems to screen student financial aid (FAFSA) applications; and (4) a bill modifying the Temporary Assistance for Needy Families program to improve program integrity, reduce fraud and improper payments, and target assistance to low-income families.  The rule affects all federal agencies and programs under these jurisdictions, as well as the states that receive and administer these funds. It impacts families seeking child care assistance, students applying for federal financial aid, and low-income families receiving temporary assistance benefits. By establishing consideration procedures, the rule enables Congress to move these bills toward votes but does not enact any substantive policy changes itself."
