summary: "# Summary of Proposed Bill  This bill would amend the Robert T. Stafford Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Act, the primary federal law governing disaster assistance. The bill proposes two main changes: (1) prohibiting federal disaster assistance, funding, or relief decisions from being based on an applicant's or recipient's political affiliation, and (2) establishing a specific timeline for federal response to requests for major disaster declarations.  The bill would affect state and local governments, disaster victims, and federal agencies responsible for administering disaster relief programs, primarily the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA). It would apply to all federal disaster assistance decisions made under the Stafford Act, which covers grants, loans, emergency protective measures, and other disaster relief resources.  Key provisions would include a prohibition on using political affiliation as a factor in determining disaster assistance eligibility or distribution, and a mandated response timeline for processing major disaster declaration requests. These changes aim to establish procedural standards and safeguards for federal disaster assistance administration."
