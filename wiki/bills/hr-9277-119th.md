summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill would modify the Administrative Procedure Act (APA), which governs how federal agencies develop regulations and how courts review agency decisions. Specifically, it would allow courts to exclude evidence from judicial review if the court determines that evidence is not based on reliable scientific principles and methods. In practice, this means courts could disregard scientific evidence presented by federal agencies (or others) in legal challenges to agency rules if the court finds the evidence scientifically unreliable.  The bill would affect federal agencies that rely on scientific evidence to support their regulations, as well as parties challenging those regulations in court. It would also impact the standards courts use when reviewing agency decisions. Currently, courts apply the \"arbitrary and capricious\" standard under the APA, examining whether agencies reasonably justified their decisions. This bill would add a scientific reliability filter, potentially making it easier for challengers to invalidate agency rules by arguing that underlying scientific evidence fails to meet certain standards.  The key provision establishes that courts have authority to determine which scientific evidence meets reliability standards and to exclude evidence they deem unreliable from consideration during judicial review. This could affect regulations across agencies dealing with environmental protection, public health, workplace safety, and other areas where scientific research informs policy decisions."
