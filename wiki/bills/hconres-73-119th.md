summary: "# Summary of Capitol Grounds Authorization Bill  This bill grants permission for the Capitol Grounds to be used to host two events: the National Peace Officers' Memorial Service and the National Honor Guard and Pipe Band Exhibition. The legislation authorizes the use of federal property—specifically the grounds surrounding the U.S. Capitol building—for these commemorative and ceremonial purposes.  The bill primarily affects event organizers, participants in the honor guard and pipe band exhibition, attendees of the memorial service, and Capitol facility management. The National Peace Officers' Memorial Service typically honors law enforcement officers who have died in the line of duty, while the Honor Guard and Pipe Band Exhibition showcases ceremonial military and police units. By passing this authorization, Congress permits these organizations to use the Capitol Grounds without additional special permission requirements.  The key provision is the formal authorization to use the Capitol Grounds for these specific events, which ensures the gatherings have proper legal authorization and likely coordinates logistics with Capitol Police and facilities management. This type of legislation is routine when organizations seek to hold events at major federal locations."
