summary: "# Summary of Bill to Establish Inadmissibility Ground for Close Relatives of Foreign Terrorists  **Purpose and Scope**  This bill would create a new ground under U.S. immigration law that could render certain foreign nationals inadmissible to the United States. Specifically, it would bar entry to close relatives of individuals designated or identified as foreign terrorists. The bill aims to address national security concerns by preventing family members of known or suspected terrorists from obtaining visas or entering the country.  **Who Is Affected**  The bill would directly affect foreign nationals seeking to enter the U.S. who have close family relationships with individuals engaged in terrorist activities. Immigration officials and consular staff would be responsible for identifying and applying this inadmissibility ground during visa processing and port-of-entry screening. U.S. citizens and lawful permanent residents with close terrorist-connected relatives could also be indirectly affected if the bill influences family-based immigration sponsorships.  **Key Considerations**  The bill's impact would depend on several factors not specified in the title alone, including: how \"close relatives\" is defined (spouse, children, parents, siblings, etc.); what designations or evidence of terrorist activity trigger the ground; and what procedures would apply. These details would determine how broadly the provision applies and what due process protections exist for individuals affected by it."
