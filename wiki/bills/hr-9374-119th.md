summary: "# Bill Summary: Detainee Locator System  This bill would require the Secretary of Homeland Security to create and maintain an online system that allows the public to locate information about individuals in custody under the department's jurisdiction. The system would serve as a centralized database for tracking detainees held by Homeland Security agencies, making their location and status accessible through the internet.  The bill affects the Department of Homeland Security and its agencies (including Immigration and Customs Enforcement and Customs and Border Protection), as well as anyone in their custody or their family members and legal representatives seeking information about them. The primary users would likely include detainees' relatives, attorneys, advocacy organizations, and members of the public.  Key provisions would establish requirements for the department to develop this locator system, maintain it with current information, and ensure it is publicly accessible online. The bill aims to increase transparency and allow faster access to information about where detainees are being held, rather than requiring individuals to contact agencies directly or through existing channels. The specific details of what information would be included in the system, privacy protections, and implementation timelines are not available from the title alone."
