summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill invokes the War Powers Resolution to direct the President to remove U.S. Armed Forces from any ongoing or imminent hostilities involving Iran. The War Powers Resolution, enacted in 1973, allows Congress to require the President to withdraw armed forces from military action after 60 days without congressional authorization. This bill seeks to exercise that congressional authority to end U.S. military involvement in Iran-related hostilities.  The bill would affect all branches of the U.S. military currently engaged in operations related to Iran, including personnel stationed in the Middle East and nearby regions. It also impacts the President's ability to conduct military operations in this theater without separate congressional approval.  The key provision directs the President to remove armed forces from hostilities with Iran in compliance with the War Powers Resolution's procedures. This type of legislation is typically used when Congress believes military action lacks proper authorization or should be terminated. The bill does not address the legality of previous military actions or create new substantive law—it functions as a directive under an existing statutory framework."
