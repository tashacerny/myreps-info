summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill is a resolution that acknowledges the stigmatization associated with menstruation and its effects on women, girls, and people who menstruate. The bill expresses support for designating May as \"National Menstrual Health Awareness Month\" to raise public awareness about menstrual health issues.  The bill affects public health messaging and education efforts, as designating a national awareness month typically encourages government agencies, schools, health organizations, and media to promote information and resources related to the designated topic. The resolution speaks to individuals who menstruate and organizations focused on public health, education, and gender equity.  Key provisions of the resolution include formal recognition of how stigma surrounding menstruation impacts quality of life and access to information, and official designation of May for heightened awareness and education efforts. As a resolution rather than legislation, this bill does not create new laws or mandatory programs, but serves as an expression of congressional support for increased attention to menstrual health in public discourse and education."
