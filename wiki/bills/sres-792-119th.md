summary: "# Summary: Asteroid Day Resolution  This resolution designates June 30, 2026, as \"Asteroid Day\" in the United States. The designation commemorates the anniversary of the Tunguska event, a major asteroid explosion that occurred on June 30, 1908, in Siberia. By establishing this observance, the resolution aims to raise public awareness about asteroids, asteroid impacts, and planetary defense efforts.  The resolution does not create any regulatory requirements or direct spending. Instead, it serves as a symbolic measure encouraging awareness and education about asteroid science and the potential risks posed by near-Earth objects. It may prompt government agencies, educational institutions, and private organizations to organize events or educational activities related to asteroids and planetary protection on that date.  The resolution affects no specific groups materially, though it may indirectly benefit educators, scientists, and organizations working in asteroid research and planetary defense by providing an official date for public outreach and engagement on these topics."
