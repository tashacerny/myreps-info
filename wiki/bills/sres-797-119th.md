summary: "# Bill Summary  This resolution requests information from the U.S. State Department regarding Israel's human rights practices, as authorized under section 502B(c) of the Foreign Assistance Act of 1961. Section 502B establishes a statutory framework requiring the State Department to evaluate human rights conditions in countries receiving U.S. military or economic assistance and to report on these practices to Congress. The resolution seeks to invoke this existing reporting requirement specifically regarding Israel.  The resolution affects the State Department, which would be required to provide requested documentation, and Congress, which would receive information to inform deliberations on U.S. foreign aid policy. Israel, as a recipient of U.S. security assistance, is the subject of the inquiry. The key provision directs the State Department to compile and submit information about Israel's adherence to human rights standards, consistent with the factfinding requirements already established in the Foreign Assistance Act."
