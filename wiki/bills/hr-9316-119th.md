summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend federal veterans law (title 38, U.S. Code) to expand eligibility for beneficiary travel payments or allowances. Beneficiary travel is a Veterans Affairs program that reimburses or covers transportation costs for eligible veterans when they travel to receive VA medical care or vocational rehabilitation services. The bill specifically targets veterans with service-connected disabilities who live in U.S. territories or in the Freely Associated States (the Marshall Islands, Micronesia, and Palau), expanding who among these populations can access these travel benefits.  The bill affects veterans residing in geographic areas outside the continental United States who have service-connected disabilities and need to travel for VA-provided medical care. It also affects the Department of Veterans Affairs, which administers the beneficiary travel program. By broadening eligibility criteria, the bill would make more veterans in these remote locations eligible to receive financial assistance for necessary travel to access VA services, which may be geographically distant or require significant out-of-pocket costs.  The key provision is the expansion of eligibility requirements for beneficiary travel benefits, though the specific details of how eligibility would be expanded are not available in the title alone. Such changes could involve removing or adjusting restrictions on which veterans in these territories and states qualify for the benefit, potentially broadening access to populations previously excluded or limited under existing rules."
