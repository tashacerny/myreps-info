summary: "# PBM Kickback Prohibition Act – Summary  **What the Bill Proposes**  This bill would prohibit pharmacy benefit managers (PBMs) from accepting or receiving kickbacks, rebates, or other financial incentives in connection with their management of prescription drug benefits. The legislation aims to address financial arrangements between PBMs and drug manufacturers, pharmacies, or other entities that may influence PBM decisions about which drugs to cover, how much patients pay, or which pharmacies patients use.  **Who It Affects**  The bill would directly regulate PBMs—companies that administer prescription drug benefits for health insurers, employers, and government programs. It would also affect drug manufacturers, pharmacy chains, and other entities that currently engage in financial arrangements with PBMs. Ultimately, the regulations could impact health insurance companies, employers offering health benefits, and patients who use prescription medications.  **Key Provisions**  While the full text is unavailable, bills with this title typically include: defining prohibited kickback arrangements, establishing penalties for violations, and creating exceptions for certain legitimate business practices (such as negotiated rebates or legitimate service fees). Such legislation generally aims to increase transparency in drug pricing and reduce financial conflicts of interest that might increase patient costs or limit access to medications."
