summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill directs the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) to develop best practices for measuring energy consumption in data centers. The legislation also requires NIST to study the availability of data that could be used to improve forecasting of energy demand. The bill addresses the growing energy footprint of data centers, which house servers and computing infrastructure for cloud services, social media platforms, streaming services, and other digital operations.  The bill affects data center operators, technology companies, utilities, energy regulators, and policymakers who work on energy efficiency and demand planning. By establishing standardized measurement practices, the legislation aims to create consistency in how data center energy use is tracked and reported across the industry.  Key provisions include directing NIST to develop measurement best practices and conducting a study on data availability for energy demand forecasting. These efforts would provide a foundation for better understanding and potentially reducing data center energy consumption, and would support more accurate predictions of future energy needs as data center usage continues to grow."
