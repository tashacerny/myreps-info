summary: "# Summary: Juneteenth National Independence Day Commemoration  This resolution designates June 19, 2026, as \"Juneteenth National Independence Day.\" The commemoration recognizes June 19, 1865, the date when enslaved people in southwestern states learned that slavery had ended. This date is historically significant because it marks when news of emancipation reached enslaved individuals in Texas and other parts of the Southwest, which was considerably later than the Emancipation Proclamation issued in 1863.  The resolution affects the federal government and the general public by establishing an official national day of commemoration. It directs attention to a specific historical milestone in American history related to the end of slavery. Such commemorative resolutions do not typically create new laws or impose requirements on individuals or businesses, but rather serve as official statements by Congress recognizing the historical and cultural significance of the date."
