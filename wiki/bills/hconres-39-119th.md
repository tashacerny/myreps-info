summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill authorizes the use of Emancipation Hall, located in the Capitol Visitor Center, to host a ceremony for presenting Congressional Gold Medals. The medals are being awarded under the Harlem Hellfighters Congressional Gold Medal Act, which previously authorized these medals to be struck and awarded to honor the Harlem Hellfighters, the 369th Regiment Combat Team of the U.S. Army during World War I.  The bill enables Congress to hold a formal presentation ceremony in this prominent Capitol location. Emancipation Hall is a public space within the Capitol Visitor Center that is available for official congressional events and ceremonies. This authorization permits a dignified public occasion where the medals can be officially presented to recipients or their representatives, allowing members of Congress, the public, and media to participate in honoring those being recognized.  The legislation affects the Congressional Gold Medal recipients associated with the Harlem Hellfighters and allows for the logistical and ceremonial use of federal space to commemorate their service and contributions to U.S. military history."
