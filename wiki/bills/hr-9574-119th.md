summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to expand the definition of \"accredited investor\" under federal securities law to include individuals who receive investment advice from certain qualified professionals. Currently, accredited investor status is primarily based on income and net worth thresholds, which limits who can participate in certain private investment offerings. The bill would add a pathway for individuals to qualify as accredited investors if they work with advisors meeting specified professional criteria, potentially broadening access to private securities offerings.  The change would affect individual investors seeking to participate in private placements, hedge funds, and other investment opportunities typically restricted to accredited investors. It would also impact investment professionals, private companies raising capital, and the securities industry more broadly. Financial advisors and other professionals whose credentials would qualify clients for accredited investor status would need to understand the new standards.  Key provisions likely include establishing which professional credentials or licenses qualify an advisor to certify clients as accredited investors, defining the scope of advice required, and any related recordkeeping or compliance requirements. The bill may also include conforming changes to SEC regulations implementing the new definition."
