The amendments in question modify the hemp production provisions of the Agricultural Marketing Act of 1946, which establishes federal standards for hemp cultivation and marketing. By delaying implementation, this bill would postpone the effective date of those regulatory changes.  The bill would primarily affect hemp producers, agricultural regulatory agencies (particularly the U.S. Department of Agriculture), and related industries that depend on hemp production rules. The delay would maintain current hemp production regulations for a period of time rather than immediately shifting to the new standards established in the 2026 appropriations act.
