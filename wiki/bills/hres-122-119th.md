summary: "# Summary of H.R. 77 Consideration Bill  This bill is a procedural measure that governs how Congress considers resolutions of disapproval related to \"midnight rules\"—regulations finalized near the end of an administration, typically in the final weeks before a new president takes office. The bill proposes to amend Chapter 8 of Title 5, United States Code, which contains the Congressional Review Act (CRA). It would allow Congress to consider multiple disapproval resolutions together \"en bloc\" (as a single package) rather than voting on each rule individually.  The change would affect the legislative process for reviewing recently finalized federal regulations. Currently, Congress must vote separately on each disapproval resolution under CRA procedures. Allowing en bloc consideration would streamline the process by permitting legislators to vote on multiple rules at once, potentially reducing the time required for these votes. This would primarily impact Congress members and the executive branch agencies whose regulations are subject to review, though it could indirectly affect the public by changing how quickly regulations can be disapproved or modified.  The bill is a procedural rule governing legislative consideration rather than a substantive policy change. It does not eliminate the Congressional Review Act or prevent Congress from disapproving rules; it simply modifies the voting procedure to make the process more efficient."
