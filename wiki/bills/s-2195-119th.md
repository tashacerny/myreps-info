summary: "# WWII Nurses Congressional Gold Medal Act — Summary  This bill proposes to award the Congressional Gold Medal to nurses who served during World War II. The Congressional Gold Medal is the highest civilian honor Congress can bestow and is typically awarded to individuals or groups for exceptional contributions to American history and culture. This legislation would recognize the service and sacrifices of military nurses who provided medical care to American armed forces during WWII.  The medal would honor nurses who served in the U.S. Army Nurse Corps, U.S. Navy Nurse Corps, and U.S. Coast Guard Nurse Corps during the war years (1941–1945). These healthcare workers provided critical medical support across multiple theaters of operation, often under dangerous conditions. The award acknowledges their contributions to preserving American lives and supporting the war effort.  The bill affects surviving WWII nurses and their families, as well as the historical record by formally recognizing this group's service. Key provisions would likely include authorizing the medal's design and striking, determining how the medal will be presented or displayed (such as in a museum), and specifying the legislative process for the award. This type of legislation is typically ceremonial and honors historical contributions without creating new programs or funding requirements."
