summary: "# Summary of Congressional Disapproval Resolution  This resolution allows Congress to disapprove a rule issued by the Bureau of Land Management (BLM) regarding a Record of Decision and Approved Resource Management Plan Amendment for the Miles City Field Office. Under the Congressional Review Act (Chapter 8 of Title 5, U.S. Code), Congress can vote to reject federal agency regulations within a specified timeframe after they are issued. If both chambers pass this resolution and it becomes law, the BLM rule would be invalidated and the agency would be prohibited from issuing substantially similar rules in the future without new congressional authorization.  The rule affects public land management decisions made by the Miles City Field Office, which oversees federal lands in Montana. The specific impacts depend on the content of the BLM's original rule, which could address topics such as land use, resource extraction, conservation, or grazing rights on public lands under the office's jurisdiction.  This is a procedural mechanism that gives Congress a tool to overturn regulatory decisions made by executive branch agencies, in this case related to how the BLM manages lands in the Miles City area."
