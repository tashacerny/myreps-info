Currently, federal student loans taken out by parents (primarily Parent PLUS loans) cannot be transferred to the student borrower. This change would create a mechanism allowing loan ownership to shift from parent to child after the loan is originated.  **Who It Affects**  The primary groups affected would be parents who have taken out federal student loans for their children's education and the student borrowers themselves. This could potentially impact millions of families with outstanding Parent PLUS loans or similar federal education debt. It may also affect federal student loan servicers who would need to implement new transfer procedures.  **Key Provisions**  While the full bill text is unavailable, based on the title, the legislation would establish rules for which loans qualify for transfer, the process for transferring loans between parent and child borrowers, and likely specify any conditions or limitations on the transfer authority.
