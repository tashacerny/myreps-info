summary: "# Bill Summary: Local Zoning Authority and Data Center Tax Incentives  This bill would establish protections for local government zoning authority over data center development projects. It would prevent federal policies or incentives from overriding local land-use decisions, ensuring that communities retain control over where and how data centers may be built within their jurisdictions. The bill would affirm that local zoning boards and planning authorities maintain their traditional role in evaluating data center proposals against existing land-use regulations and community needs.  The bill also conditions federal tax incentives for data center development on the execution of community benefit agreements. Under this provision, companies seeking federal tax benefits for data center projects would be required to negotiate and finalize agreements with local communities. These agreements would typically address local impacts and benefits, though the specific terms would be determined through negotiations between developers and local governments.  The bill would affect data center developers seeking federal tax incentives, local governments involved in zoning decisions, and communities where data centers are proposed. By linking federal tax benefits to community agreements, the legislation aims to balance federal economic development goals with local control and community input in the development process."
