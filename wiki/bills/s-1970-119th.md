summary: "# MACV–SOG Congressional Gold Medal Act — Summary  This bill proposes to award the Congressional Gold Medal to MACV–SOG (Military Assistance Command, Vietnam – Studies and Observations Group), a joint special operations unit that conducted classified military operations during the Vietnam War. The Congressional Gold Medal is the highest civilian honor Congress can award and is typically given to individuals or groups for outstanding achievements or contributions to American history and culture.  MACV–SOG was a highly classified special operations command that existed from 1964 to 1973 and conducted covert missions in Vietnam, Cambodia, and Laos. The medal would recognize the service and sacrifices of the personnel who served in this unit. The bill affects the members of MACV–SOG and honors their military service, though as a symbolic measure, it does not provide direct financial benefits or entitlements. The award would be struck by the U.S. Mint and may be displayed in museums or institutions, serving as an official congressional recognition of the unit's contributions during the Vietnam War."
