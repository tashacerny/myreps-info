Commemorative coins are special-edition coins produced by the U.S. Mint in limited quantities, typically sold to collectors and the public at a premium price above face value. This legislation would allow the Treasury Department to design and produce coins specifically honoring the anniversary and the victims, first responders, and survivors of 9/11.  The bill affects the U.S. Mint's production schedule and involves the Treasury Department. Revenue from coin sales generally goes to the federal government, though commemorative coin legislation often directs a portion of surcharges (the markup above the coin's intrinsic value) to designated beneficiaries—which could include 9/11 memorials, victim funds, or first responder organizations, depending on the bill's specific provisions.
