summary: "# Summary of Dietary Supplement Ingredients Prohibition Bill  This bill would require the Secretary of Defense to create and publish an official list of dietary supplement ingredients that are prohibited for use by members of the Armed Forces. The bill addresses concerns about military personnel consuming supplements that may contain banned substances, compromise their health, or interfere with military readiness and drug-testing protocols. By establishing a clear, published list, the bill aims to provide service members with transparent guidance about which supplement ingredients are off-limits.  The bill would directly affect active-duty service members, reservists, and National Guard personnel who use dietary supplements. It may also affect supplement manufacturers and retailers who market products to military consumers. The key provision requires the Department of Defense to maintain and publicly distribute the prohibited ingredients list, making it easier for military personnel to ensure compliance and for commanders to enforce supplement policies consistently across all service branches.  The bill represents an administrative and transparency measure intended to standardize supplement restrictions across the Armed Forces, though the full text would clarify details about how the list would be updated, what criteria determine prohibited ingredients, and any enforcement mechanisms."
