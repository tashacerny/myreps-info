summary: "# Bill Summary  This joint resolution seeks to disapprove a rule issued by the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) concerning safety standards for hydrogen-powered vehicles. Specifically, the rule addresses fuel system integrity and the structural integrity of compressed hydrogen storage systems in these vehicles. The rule also incorporates technical standards by reference, meaning it relies on external documents to define specific safety requirements rather than detailing them entirely within the regulation itself.  The bill uses the Congressional Review Act (CRA) process outlined in Chapter 8 of Title 5, United States Code, which allows Congress to overturn federal agency rules through a joint resolution. If passed, this joint resolution would eliminate the NHTSA hydrogen vehicle safety rule. The rule would affect manufacturers of hydrogen fuel cell vehicles and related components, as well as the regulatory framework governing how these vehicles are designed and tested for safety.  The key provision is the disapproval mechanism itself—if adopted, the rule would be rescinded and could not be reissued in substantially the same form without new legislative authority. This affects the ongoing federal regulation of hydrogen vehicle safety standards at a time when hydrogen fuel cell vehicle technology is still in relatively early market stages."
