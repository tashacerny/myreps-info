summary: "# Bill Summary: Paraquat Pesticide Registration Cancellation  **What the Bill Proposes** This bill would direct the cancellation of all registered uses of paraquat, a herbicide currently approved for use in the United States. The bill aims to eliminate paraquat from the market by revoking its pesticide registrations, which would prevent manufacturers from producing it and prevent farmers and other users from legally applying it.  **Who It Affects** The bill would affect multiple groups: agricultural producers who currently use paraquat as a weed control tool, pesticide manufacturers and distributors who produce and sell the product, and potentially farmworkers and communities near areas where paraquat is applied. It may also affect regulatory agencies responsible for pesticide oversight, such as the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA).  **Key Context** The bill's focus on paraquat reflects ongoing debate about the herbicide's safety. Paraquat is widely used in farming but has faced regulatory scrutiny in some countries and scientific discussion regarding potential health effects. The bill would represent a complete ban on the substance, eliminating all current and future approved uses in the United States. The legislative text would likely address implementation timelines, transition periods for current users, and any related regulatory procedures."
