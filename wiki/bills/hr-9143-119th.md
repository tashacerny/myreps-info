summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend federal patent law to require the U.S. Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO) Director to establish disclosure requirements for patent applicants. Specifically, applicants would need to disclose any financial, business, or organizational ties to the People's Republic of China and other designated foreign adversaries in their patent applications. The bill aims to increase transparency about potential foreign connections in the patent application process.  The bill would affect patent applicants, including individual inventors, companies, and research institutions that file applications with the USPTO. It would require these entities to provide information about specified foreign relationships as part of the application process, though the bill title does not detail what specific types of ties must be disclosed, what threshold triggers disclosure, or how the disclosed information would be used.  The key provision establishes a disclosure mandate administered by the USPTO Director, though the specific implementation details—such as which countries beyond China would be considered \"foreign adversaries,\" what consequences exist for non-disclosure, and how the information would be handled—are not outlined in the title alone."
