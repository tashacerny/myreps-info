summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill invokes the War Powers Resolution to direct the President to remove U.S. Armed Forces from any hostilities with Iran. The War Powers Resolution, enacted in 1973, establishes procedures for Congressional oversight of military operations. Section 5(c) of that law allows Congress to order the termination of military actions through concurrent resolution. This bill uses that authority to mandate withdrawal from Iran-related hostilities.  The bill would affect U.S. military personnel currently engaged in operations involving Iran, as well as the Department of Defense and other relevant federal agencies responsible for implementing such withdrawal. It would also constrain the President's ability to direct continued military operations in this context without specific Congressional authorization.  Key provisions would include a directive to cease hostilities and remove armed forces from Iran-related military operations. The bill does not appear to address the timeline for withdrawal, funding mechanisms, or other implementation details based on the title alone. The measure represents an exercise of Congressional war powers authority and reflects disagreement with the executive branch regarding the scope of current military engagement."
