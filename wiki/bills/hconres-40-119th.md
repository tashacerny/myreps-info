summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill invokes the War Powers Resolution to direct the President to remove U.S. Armed Forces from any ongoing or imminent hostilities with Iran. The War Powers Resolution, enacted in 1973, allows Congress to require the withdrawal of armed forces from military action if Congress has not declared war or authorized the use of military force. Section 5(c), specifically, provides a mechanism for Congress to direct such removal through a concurrent resolution.  The bill would affect U.S. military personnel involved in operations related to Iran and would constrain executive authority over military deployments in that theater. It applies to all branches of the U.S. Armed Forces engaged in hostilities with Iran. The measure does not authorize new military action; rather, it seeks to limit or end existing military involvement.  Key provisions include a directive for the President to cease hostilities and withdraw forces, as specified under the War Powers Resolution framework. The bill's scope depends on how \"hostilities with Iran\" is defined—whether it covers direct combat, broader military operations, or specific incidents. Like other War Powers measures, this bill reflects the constitutional tension between presidential command of military operations and congressional war powers."
