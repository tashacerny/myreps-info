summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to establish that federal agencies' compliance with a specific biological opinion automatically satisfies their obligations under the Endangered Species Act of 1973. A \"biological opinion\" is a document issued by the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service or National Marine Fisheries Service evaluating how a proposed federal action might affect endangered or threatened species. Under current law, agencies must independently ensure their actions comply with the Endangered Species Act; this bill would streamline that process by treating adherence to a designated biological opinion as sufficient legal compliance for the agency action in question.  The bill would affect federal agencies undertaking actions that require biological opinions, as well as species listed under the Endangered Species Act. It could impact industries and activities subject to Endangered Species Act review—such as water management, infrastructure projects, and resource development—by potentially reducing regulatory requirements or legal uncertainty. The bill would also affect the Fish and Wildlife Service and National Marine Fisheries Service, which issue the biological opinions.  Without access to the full text, the specific biological opinion referenced and the scope of agency actions covered cannot be determined. The bill may include provisions defining which biological opinions qualify, establishing conditions for compliance, or clarifying procedures for the affected agencies."
