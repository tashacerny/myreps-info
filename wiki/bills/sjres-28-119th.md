summary: "# Bill Summary  This joint resolution would disapprove a rule issued by the Bureau of Consumer Financial Protection (CFPB) that defines which companies qualify as \"larger participants\" in the market for general-use digital consumer payment applications. The rule likely establishes size thresholds or criteria for determining when digital payment app providers—such as payment platforms and services—must comply with certain CFPB regulations and oversight requirements.  **Who It Affects:** The rule impacts digital payment application companies and providers. Depending on how the CFPB defines \"larger participants,\" the disapproval would affect which companies face regulatory requirements under the rule. It may also influence consumer protections tied to these regulations, as well as the competitive landscape for payment app providers.  **Key Provision:** The primary provision is the disapproval itself. If passed, this joint resolution would invalidate the CFPB's rule using the Congressional Review Act, a process that allows Congress to overturn recent federal agency regulations. This would prevent or eliminate the regulatory framework the CFPB created for identifying and regulating larger participants in the digital payment app market, likely returning the regulatory status to its previous state."
