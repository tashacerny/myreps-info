summary: "# Bill Summary  This bill proposes to amend the Employee Retirement Income Security Act of 1974 (ERISA) to expand the definition of \"employer\" under federal law. Specifically, it would allow health marketplace pools—collaborative arrangements where multiple organizations pool resources to offer health benefits—to be treated as employers for purposes of establishing and offering group health plans or group health insurance coverage. Currently, ERISA's definition of \"employer\" does not explicitly include such pooling arrangements, which limits their ability to offer group coverage.  The bill would primarily affect small businesses, organizations, and individuals who participate in health marketplace pools. By deeming these pools as employers under ERISA, the legislation would allow them to more easily establish group health plans, potentially expanding access to group health coverage options outside traditional employer-based arrangements. This could increase flexibility in how workers and organizations obtain health insurance, particularly for self-employed individuals, gig workers, or small business owners who band together through pooling mechanisms.  The bill also includes language for \"other purposes,\" which suggests additional provisions beyond the primary amendment, though specific details are not available from the title alone. The overall intent appears to be modernizing ERISA's regulatory framework to accommodate newer models of health benefit arrangement and pooling."
