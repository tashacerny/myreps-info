summary: "# Summary of Joint Resolution on Brazil Trade Emergency  This joint resolution would terminate a national emergency declaration that was previously issued to impose tariffs (duties) on articles imported from Brazil. The resolution uses Congress's authority under the National Emergencies Act to end an existing emergency declaration, which would revoke the legal basis for the tariffs currently in place on Brazilian imports.  The bill would directly affect importers and businesses that trade with Brazil, as well as consumers of Brazilian goods. Terminating the emergency declaration would likely eliminate or suspend the tariffs that were imposed under it, potentially lowering prices for affected products and changing trade conditions between the United States and Brazil.  The joint resolution operates as a legislative check on executive power. Under the National Emergencies Act, Congress can terminate national emergency declarations through passage of a joint resolution. If enacted, this would remove the emergency authority being used to maintain the tariffs, though it would not necessarily address any underlying trade disputes or agreements between the two countries."
