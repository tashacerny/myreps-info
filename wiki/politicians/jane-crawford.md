Jane Crawford has served as U.S. Senator from Vermont since January 2013, when she was elected on a platform focused on healthcare access, rural economic development, and climate policy. She chairs the Senate Committee on Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions (HELP).

## Background

Before entering federal politics, Crawford served in the Vermont state legislature for over a decade, first as a state representative representing the Windsor district (2001–2007) and then as a state senator for Windsor County (2007–2013). She is a former emergency room physician and founded the Montpelier Free Clinic in 1998.

Crawford holds a medical degree from the University of Vermont and a master's degree in public policy from Harvard Kennedy School.

## Policy Positions

Crawford is a consistent advocate for universal healthcare coverage, having championed the Universal Healthcare Coverage Act in the 119th Congress. She has also been a leading voice on rural infrastructure, authoring the Rural Broadband Equity Act and securing funding for Vermont's dairy farming communities.

On climate policy, she supports aggressive federal emissions targets and has backed both the Clean Energy Transition Act and federal investment in flood resilience programs, an issue of particular importance for Vermont after record flooding events.
