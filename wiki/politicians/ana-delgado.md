Ana Delgado has represented California's 11th congressional district since 2021. She sits on the House Committee on Energy and Commerce and the House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure.

## Background

Delgado was born in San Jose, California, to parents who immigrated from Mexico and the Philippines. She earned a degree in urban planning from UC Berkeley and worked as a housing policy analyst before winning a seat on the San Francisco Board of Supervisors in 2013.

She served three terms on the Board of Supervisors before running for Congress in 2020. Her 2020 campaign focused primarily on housing affordability, transit investment, and climate policy.

## Policy Positions

Delgado is one of the House's most active legislators on clean energy, authoring the Clean Energy Transition Act that passed the House in 2025. She is also a vocal advocate for housing affordability, arguing that federal zoning reform is necessary to address the housing crisis in high-cost metro areas like San Francisco.

She has been a consistent opponent of expanded detention at the southern border, voting against the Border Security Enhancement Act and co-signing a letter urging the administration to pursue alternative enforcement approaches.
