Patricia Okonkwo has served in the Vermont State Senate representing the Essex-Orleans District since 2019. She chairs the Senate Committee on Health and Welfare.

## Background

Okonkwo grew up in Newport, Vermont, and earned a nursing degree from Vermont Technical College. She worked as a registered nurse at North Country Hospital for nearly twenty years before entering politics. She was elected to the Newport City Council in 2012 and served three terms before running for the state senate.

Her parents emigrated from Nigeria in the 1970s, and she is one of the first Black women elected to the Vermont State Senate.

## Policy Positions

Okonkwo's legislative priorities center on rural healthcare access, which she views as the defining challenge facing northeast Vermont. She has championed loan forgiveness for rural healthcare workers, expanded telehealth coverage under Medicaid, and increased funding for community health centers in underserved areas.

On climate policy, she supported the Clean Heat Standard Act but expressed concerns about its impact on low-income households in rural areas that rely heavily on heating oil, and pushed for income-based assistance provisions to be included in the final bill.
