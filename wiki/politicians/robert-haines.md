Robert Haines has represented Texas in the U.S. Senate since 2015. He is a former oil and gas attorney and serves on the Senate Finance Committee and the Senate Committee on Energy and Natural Resources.

## Background

Haines grew up in Midland, Texas, and earned a law degree from the University of Texas School of Law. Before entering politics, he spent fifteen years as an energy attorney in Houston, representing independent oil and gas producers.

He was first elected to the Texas state legislature in 1998, then won a seat in the U.S. House of Representatives in 2004, representing Houston's 7th congressional district for a decade before winning his Senate seat in 2014.

## Policy Positions

Haines is a consistent advocate for deregulation of the energy sector, reduced federal spending, and strong border security. He opposed the Infrastructure Modernization and Jobs Act on the grounds that it represented excessive federal spending and should have been funded through existing transportation budgets rather than new deficit spending.

He is one of the Senate's most active voices on energy policy, opposing federal mandates on renewable energy targets while supporting expanded natural gas exports and domestic energy production.
